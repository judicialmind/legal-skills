# Visa Categories Comparison Reference Guide

## Overview

This guide provides a comprehensive comparison of common nonimmigrant work visa categories and employment-based immigrant visa categories, including eligibility requirements, processing times, and strategic considerations.

---

## Nonimmigrant Work Visas

### H-1B Specialty Occupation

**Basic Requirements**
- Position requires specialized knowledge
- Bachelor's degree or higher in specific specialty
- Beneficiary must possess required degree
- Employer-employee relationship

**Specialty Occupation Criteria**
1. Bachelor's degree minimum entry requirement
2. Degree requirement common in industry
3. Employer normally requires degree
4. Nature of duties so complex/specialized that degree required

**Numerical Limitations**
- Regular cap: 65,000 per fiscal year
- Master's cap exemption: Additional 20,000
- Cap-exempt employers: Universities, research institutions, government

**Processing**

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Registration Period | March 1-20 |
| Selection Lottery | Late March |
| Filing Window | April 1 - June 30 |
| Regular Processing | 3-6 months |
| Premium Processing | 15 business days |

**Maximum Duration**
- Initial: 3 years
- Extension: Additional 3 years
- Maximum: 6 years (exceptions for pending green card)

**Key Benefits**
- Dual intent allowed
- Spouse (H-4) may work if H-1B holder has approved I-140
- Portability with pending or approved I-140

### L-1 Intracompany Transferee

#### L-1A (Managers and Executives)

**Requirements**
- Employed by qualifying organization abroad for 1 year in preceding 3 years
- Coming to work for parent, subsidiary, affiliate, or branch
- Manager or executive capacity

**Manager Definition**
- Manages organization, department, or function
- Supervises professionals or manages essential function
- Authority to hire/fire or recommend such
- Exercises discretion over day-to-day operations

**Executive Definition**
- Directs management of organization or major component
- Establishes goals and policies
- Exercises wide latitude in discretionary decision-making
- Receives only general supervision from higher executives

**Duration**
- Initial: 1 year (new office) or 3 years
- Extension: 2-year increments
- Maximum: 7 years

#### L-1B (Specialized Knowledge)

**Requirements**
- Employed by qualifying organization abroad for 1 year
- Possesses specialized knowledge of company products, services, or procedures
- Coming to use that knowledge in U.S. position

**Specialized Knowledge Defined**
- Knowledge of company's product and its application in international markets
- Advanced knowledge of company processes and procedures
- Not merely skilled worker knowledge

**Duration**
- Initial: 1 year (new office) or 3 years
- Extension: 2-year increments
- Maximum: 5 years

**L-1 Advantages**
- No numerical cap
- Blanket L-1 for qualifying organizations
- Spouse (L-2) employment authorized

### O-1 Extraordinary Ability

#### O-1A (Sciences, Business, Education, Athletics)

**Standard**: "Extraordinary ability" = risen to very top of field

**Evidence Requirements (3 of 8 criteria)**
1. National/international awards or prizes
2. Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
3. Published material about the person
4. Judge of others' work in the field
5. Original contributions of major significance
6. Authorship of scholarly articles
7. Employment in distinguished reputation organization
8. High salary or remuneration

#### O-1B (Arts, Motion Pictures, Television)

**Standard**: "Distinction" for arts; "extraordinary achievement" for film/TV

**Evidence Requirements (Arts - 3 criteria)**
1. Lead or starring role in distinguished productions
2. Critical reviews or published material
3. Lead role for distinguished organizations
4. Record of major commercial or critically acclaimed success
5. Significant recognition from organizations, critics, government
6. High salary relative to others in field

**O-1 Processing**
- No annual cap
- Premium processing available (15 business days)
- Initial validity: Up to 3 years
- Extensions: 1-year increments as needed

---

### E-1/E-2 Treaty Investor/Trader

#### E-1 Treaty Trader

**Requirements**
- National of treaty country
- Substantial trade between U.S. and treaty country
- Trade principally (>50%) with treaty country
- Trade is continuous and significant volume

**Qualifying Trade**
- Goods
- Services
- Banking, insurance
- Transportation
- Technology transfer

#### E-2 Treaty Investor

**Requirements**
- National of treaty country
- Substantial investment in U.S. enterprise
- Investment "at risk"
- Enterprise must be more than marginal
- Investor must develop and direct enterprise

**Investment Standards**
- No minimum dollar amount
- Proportionality test (substantial relative to total cost)
- Generally $100,000+ recommended for strong case
- Must be committed and irrevocable

**E Visa Duration**
- Initial: 2 years
- Extensions: 2-year increments
- No maximum (but must maintain status)

## Employment-Based Immigrant Visas

### EB-1: Priority Workers

#### EB-1A (Extraordinary Ability)

**Standard**: "Extraordinary ability" in sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics

**Evidence (3 of 10 criteria)**
1. Lesser nationally/internationally recognized awards
2. Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
3. Published material about the person
4. Judge of others' work
5. Original contributions of major significance
6. Authorship of scholarly articles
7. Display of work at artistic exhibitions
8. Leading role in distinguished organizations
9. High salary relative to field
10. Commercial success in performing arts

**Benefits**
- No labor certification required
- No job offer required
- Self-petition allowed
- Premium processing available

#### EB-1B (Outstanding Professors/Researchers)

**Requirements**
- International recognition as outstanding in academic field
- 3 years teaching or research experience
- Tenured, tenure-track, or comparable research position

**Evidence (2 of 6 criteria)**
1. Major awards for outstanding achievement
2. Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
3. Published material by others about the person's work
4. Judge of others' work
5. Original scientific contributions
6. Authorship of scholarly articles

#### EB-1C (Multinational Managers/Executives)

**Requirements**
- Employed by qualifying organization abroad for 1 year in preceding 3 years
- Manager or executive position
- Coming to work for U.S. parent, subsidiary, affiliate, or branch
- Manager/executive capacity in U.S.

**Relationship to L-1A**
- Similar standards
- No degree requirement
- No labor certification required
- Employer files petition

---

### EB-2: Professionals with Advanced Degrees

#### EB-2A (Advanced Degree)

**Requirements**
- Job requires advanced degree
- Beneficiary possesses advanced degree or Bachelor's + 5 years experience

#### EB-2B (Exceptional Ability)

**Requirements**
- Exceptional ability in sciences, arts, or business
- 3 of 6 criteria:
  1. Academic record of degrees relating to area
  2. Letters documenting 10 years full-time experience
  3. License to practice profession
  4. High salary commanding exceptional ability
  5. Membership in professional associations
  6. Recognition by peers, government, or organizations

#### EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW)

**Standard**: Labor certification waived if in national interest

**Matter of Dhanasar Framework (2016)**
1. Proposed endeavor has substantial merit and national importance
2. Well positioned to advance the proposed endeavor
3. On balance, beneficial to waive job offer and labor certification

**Advantages**
- No employer required
- No labor certification
- Self-petition allowed
- Flexibility in employment

---

### EB-3: Skilled Workers, Professionals, Other Workers

#### EB-3A (Skilled Workers)

**Requirements**
- Job requires minimum 2 years training or experience
- Beneficiary has 2 years training or experience
- No qualified U.S. worker available

#### EB-3B (Professionals)

**Requirements**
- Job requires Bachelor's degree
- Beneficiary has Bachelor's degree
- No qualified U.S. worker available

#### EB-3C (Other Workers)

**Requirements**
- Job requires less than 2 years training
- Unskilled labor positions
- No qualified U.S. worker available
- Limited to 10,000 per year

---

### EB-4: Special Immigrants

**Categories Include**
- Religious workers
- Broadcasters
- Iraqi/Afghan translators
- International organization employees
- Various other special categories

---

### EB-5: Immigrant Investors

**Standard Investment**
- $1,050,000 investment in new commercial enterprise
- Create 10 full-time jobs for U.S. workers

**Targeted Employment Area (TEA)**
- $800,000 investment
- Rural area or high unemployment area

**Regional Center Program**
- Pooled investments
- Direct and indirect job creation counted
- Reauthorization required periodically

## Immigrant Visa Priority Dates

### Visa Bulletin

**Monthly Updates**
- Department of State publishes monthly
- Final Action Dates and Filing Dates
- Varies by preference category and country of chargeability

**Country Backlogs (Typical)**

| Category | Worldwide | India | China | Philippines |
|----------|-----------|-------|-------|-------------|
| EB-1 | Current | 2+ years | 2+ years | Current |
| EB-2 | Current | 10+ years | 4+ years | Current |
| EB-3 | Current | 8+ years | 3+ years | Current |

### Priority Date Establishment

**Labor Certification Cases**
- Priority date = PERM filing date

**Non-Labor Certification**
- Priority date = I-140 filing date

**Priority Date Retention**
- Approved I-140 retains priority date
- Can port to new employer
- New I-140 can capture earlier priority date

## F-1 OPT and STEM Extension

### Post-Completion OPT

**Eligibility**
- F-1 student
- Completed degree requirements
- Employment related to field of study

**Duration**
- 12 months standard OPT
- Must apply 90 days before graduation or 60 days after

### STEM OPT Extension

**Eligibility**
- 12-month OPT holder
- STEM degree from SEVP-certified school
- Employer enrolled in E-Verify
- Training plan (Form I-983)

**Duration**
- Additional 24 months
- Total 36 months possible

**Requirements**
- Employer must be E-Verify enrolled
- STEM-designated degree (DHS STEM list)
- Training plan with employer
- Six-month reporting requirements
- May not be unemployed more than 150 days

### Cap-Gap Extension

**Eligibility**
- F-1 student or OPT holder
- H-1B cap petition filed
- Gap between OPT end and October 1

**Coverage**
- Extends F-1 status and EAD through September 30
- Automatic upon timely H-1B filing

## Strategic Considerations

### H-1B vs. L-1

| Factor | H-1B | L-1 |
|--------|------|-----|
| Prior employment abroad | Not required | Required (1 year) |
| Annual cap | Yes (85,000) | No |
| Specialized knowledge | Not required | L-1B requires |
| New office startup | Available | Available (1-year limit) |
| Green card pathway | EB-2/EB-3 (PERM required) | EB-1C (no PERM for L-1A) |

### Nonimmigrant to Immigrant Strategy

**H-1B Holders**
- File PERM as early as possible
- I-140 with premium processing
- H-1B extensions beyond 6 years possible
- Port priority date if changing employers

**L-1A Holders**
- Consider EB-1C (no PERM required)
- 7-year maximum creates urgency
- Manager/executive definitions consistent with L-1A

**O-1 Holders**
- Consider EB-1A self-petition
- Same evidence may support both
- No dual intent, but single intent not required
