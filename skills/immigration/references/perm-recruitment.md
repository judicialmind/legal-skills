# PERM Labor Certification Reference Guide

## Overview

The PERM (Program Electronic Review Management) process is the Department of Labor's system for employers to obtain labor certification for sponsoring foreign workers for permanent residence. This guide covers recruitment requirements, filing procedures, and audit response strategies.

---

## PERM Basics

### Purpose of Labor Certification

**Test of the Labor Market**
- Demonstrates no qualified U.S. workers available
- Protects U.S. workers from wage depression
- Employer must offer prevailing wage

**When Required**
- EB-2 (Advanced Degree/Exceptional Ability) - unless NIW
- EB-3 (Skilled Workers, Professionals, Other Workers)

**When Not Required**
- EB-1 categories (Extraordinary Ability, Outstanding Researcher, Multinational Manager)
- EB-2 National Interest Waiver
- Schedule A occupations (nurses, physical therapists)

### Basic Requirements

**Employer Obligations**
1. Offer full-time permanent position
2. Pay at least prevailing wage
3. Job opportunity is bona fide
4. Conduct good faith recruitment
5. Not tailored to beneficiary's qualifications

**Position Requirements**
1. Must be for full-time employment
2. Must be permanent position
3. Cannot require foreign language unless business necessity
4. Requirements must be normal for occupation

## Prevailing Wage Determination

### Wage Request Process

**Form ETA-9141**
- Submit to National Prevailing Wage Center (NPWC)
- Processing time: 2-4 months
- Valid for 6 months from determination date (can be extended)

**Information Required**
- Job title and duties
- Minimum requirements
- Worksite location
- Wage offered

### Wage Levels

**Four Wage Levels**

| Level | Description | Percentile |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Level I | Entry level | 17th percentile |
| Level II | Qualified | 34th percentile |
| Level III | Experienced | 50th percentile |
| Level IV | Fully competent | 67th percentile |

**Level Determination Factors**
- Complexity of job duties
- Judgment required
- Supervision received
- Understanding required

### Challenging Wage Determinations

**Request for Review**
- Within 30 days of determination
- Director of NPWC reviews
- May submit additional information

**Appeal**
- If review unsuccessful
- Board of Alien Labor Certification Appeals (BALCA)
- Limited circumstances

## Job Requirements

### Minimum Requirements

**Actual Minimum Requirements**
- Must reflect actual minimum to perform job
- Cannot include requirements substantially equivalent to training or experience beneficiary acquired on the job

**Documentation Needed**
- Business necessity for each requirement
- Industry standards
- Employer's own hiring history

### Experience Requirements

**Types of Experience**
- General experience: Overall experience in occupation
- Specific experience: Experience with particular duties or tools

**Experience from Current Employer**
- Generally cannot require experience gained with sponsoring employer
- Exception: Experience in different job that was not substantially comparable

**Alternative Requirements**
- "Or any suitable combination" provisions
- Must be genuine alternative
- Cannot be designed to exclude U.S. workers

### Education Requirements

**Degree Requirements**
- Must be normal for occupation
- SVP levels from O*NET provide guidance
- "Degree in related field" may be too broad

**Foreign Degree Equivalency**
- Evaluated against U.S. standards
- Three-year degrees may require evaluation
- Credential evaluation services

## Recruitment Process

### Required Recruitment Steps

**All PERM Cases Require**

| Step | Requirement | Timing |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Job Order with SWA | 30 days minimum |
| 2 | Two Sunday newspaper ads | Two different Sundays |
| 3 | Notice of Filing | Posted 10 business days |

**Professional Positions Additionally Require**

Three of the following:
1. Employer's website posting
2. Job search website posting
3. On-campus recruiting
4. Trade or professional organization posting
5. Private employment firm
6. Employee referral program with incentive
7. Campus placement office
8. Local/ethnic newspaper
9. Radio/TV advertisement
10. Job fair

### Job Order with State Workforce Agency (SWA)

**Requirements**
- Submit job order to SWA serving job location
- Must be active for at least 30 days
- Complete during recruitment period
- Ends at least 30 days before PERM filing

**Content**
- Job title and duties
- Minimum requirements
- Wage offered
- Contact information

### Newspaper Advertisements

**Two Sunday Newspaper Ads**
- Must be in general circulation newspaper
- Two different Sundays
- In area of intended employment
- May be in print or online edition

**Required Content**
- Name of employer
- Geographic area of employment
- Contact information
- Description of vacancy (not full job description required)

**Note**: Not required to include wage, though some practitioners recommend

### Notice of Filing

**Posting Requirements**
- Posted for 10 consecutive business days
- In conspicuous location(s) at worksite
- If no physical worksite, electronic notice acceptable
- Must be complete before filing PERM

**Alternative for Union Positions**
- Written notice to bargaining representative
- Must provide 10 days for response

**Required Content**
- Job title and duties
- Work location
- Rate of pay
- How to file complaints with DOL

### Additional Recruitment for Professional Positions

**Employer's Website**
- Posted on employer's own website
- No minimum duration specified (but best practice: at least 30 days)
- Print or save screenshot with date

**Job Search Website**
- National job search websites (Indeed, Monster, LinkedIn, etc.)
- Posted for at least 30 days recommended
- Print confirmation of posting

**Trade or Professional Organization**
- Organization related to occupation
- Online or print publication
- Relevant to industry

**Documentation**
- Save tearsheets, printouts, screenshots
- Note dates of posting
- Retain for 5 years

## Recruitment Timeline

### Standard Timeline

```
PHASE 1: Prevailing Wage (2-4 months)
  Submit ETA-9141
  Wait for determination

PHASE 2: Recruitment (60-75 days minimum)
  Day 1: Begin job order and first newspaper ad
  Day 7-14: Second newspaper ad
  Days 1-30+: Additional recruitment steps
  Day 30+: End job order (minimum 30 days)
  Day 31-60: 30-day "quiet period" (no recruitment)
  Day 60+: Post Notice of Filing (10 business days)

PHASE 3: PERM Filing
  Must file within 180 days of completing recruitment
  Must file within 180 days of completing additional recruitment
```

### Critical Timing Rules

**30-Day Rule**
- Job order must be posted at least 30 days
- Must end at least 30 days before PERM filing

**Quiet Period**
- 30-day recruitment report period after recruitment ends
- No recruitment activity during this period
- Time for U.S. workers to apply

**180-Day Validity**
- Recruitment valid for 180 days
- PERM must be filed within 180 days of recruitment completion
- Otherwise, must conduct new recruitment

## Evaluating U.S. Worker Applicants

### Lawful Job-Related Reasons for Rejection

**Acceptable Reasons**
- Does not meet minimum requirements
- Unwilling to work required hours or location
- Salary expectation too high
- Failed required test or assessment
- Poor references
- Unable to obtain required license

**Unacceptable Reasons**
- Overqualified
- Personality conflicts
- Subjective preferences
- Any discriminatory reason

### Documentation of Rejections

**For Each Applicant**
- Name and contact information
- Resume or application
- Specific reason for rejection
- Interview notes (if interviewed)
- Date of rejection and method of communication

### "Able, Willing, Qualified, and Available"

**All Four Required**
- Able: Capable of performing job
- Willing: Interested in position
- Qualified: Meets minimum requirements
- Available: Can start within reasonable time

## Filing the PERM Application

### Form ETA-9089

**Electronic Filing**
- File through DOL FLAG system
- Employer account required
- Attorney/representative access

**Information Required**
- Employer information
- Job opportunity details
- Foreign worker information
- Recruitment information
- Declaration of lawful recruitment

### Processing Times

**Current Processing**
- Approximately 6-12 months
- Varies significantly over time
- Check DOL processing times

**No Premium Processing Available**
- PERM has no expedite option
- Must wait for standard processing

## Audits

### Audit Selection

**Random Audits**
- DOL randomly selects percentage of cases
- No specific trigger
- Approximately 25-35% of cases audited

**Targeted Audits**
- Certain factors trigger review
- Travel requirements
- Foreign language requirements
- Work-from-home positions
- Wages significantly above prevailing wage

### Audit Letter

**Response Deadline**
- 30 days from date of letter
- No extensions available
- Failure to respond = denial

**Common Documentation Requests**
1. Signed Recruitment Report
2. Resumes of all U.S. applicants
3. Evidence of rejection reasons
4. Recruitment documentation (ads, job orders, etc.)
5. Business necessity documentation
6. Employer financial documents

### Preparing Audit Response

**Organization Critical**
- Clearly labeled exhibits
- Table of contents
- Chronological organization
- Summary cover letter

**Recruitment Report**

Must include:
- Number of applicants
- Number hired
- Number rejected and reasons
- Recruitment steps taken
- Results of each step

### Supervised Recruitment

**When Required**
- DOL orders supervised recruitment
- All recruitment monitored by Certifying Officer
- Employer conducts recruitment under DOL supervision

**Process**
- DOL provides instructions
- Employer conducts prescribed recruitment
- Reports results directly to CO
- DOL makes determination based on results

## Common PERM Mistakes

### Timing Errors

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Filing before quiet period ends | Denial |
| Job order too short | Denial |
| Recruitment expired (>180 days) | Must start over |
| Notice of Filing not 10 business days | Denial |

### Documentation Errors

- Insufficient rejection documentation
- Inconsistent job requirements
- Requirements not documented as business necessity
- Incomplete audit response

### Substantive Errors

- Requirements tailored to beneficiary
- Using beneficiary's experience with same employer
- Foreign language without business necessity
- Wage below prevailing wage

## Special Situations

### Multiple Worksites

**Itinerant Workers**
- May need to designate primary worksite
- Prevailing wage for headquarters area
- SWA for headquarters area

**Remote Work**
- Must have physical worksite for posting notice
- Electronic notice may be acceptable
- Prevailing wage for actual work location

### Schedule A Occupations

**Pre-Certified Occupations**
- Physical therapists
- Professional nurses
- Certain exceptional ability aliens (sciences or arts)

**Process**
- No PERM recruitment required
- File I-140 with attestation
- USCIS determines labor market

### College and University Teachers

**Special Recruitment Procedures**
- Competitive recruitment in accordance with usual practices
- Must have selected most qualified candidate
- Selection of foreign national must have been result of competitive process
- Documentation of selection process required

---

## After PERM Approval

### Validity Period

- PERM approval valid for 180 days
- Must file I-140 within 180 days
- No extensions

### Retention Requirements

**Five-Year Retention**
- All recruitment documentation
- Resumes and applications
- Rejection documentation
- Advertisements and postings
- Interview notes
- PERM filing documents

### Amendments and Modifications

**Cannot Amend PERM**
- If significant changes needed, must refile
- New recruitment required
- New prevailing wage determination may be needed

## PERM Checklist

### Pre-Recruitment
- [ ] Determine job requirements (document business necessity)
- [ ] Obtain prevailing wage determination
- [ ] Draft job description and advertisement text
- [ ] Identify recruitment sources for professional positions

### During Recruitment
- [ ] Post SWA job order (30+ days)
- [ ] Place first Sunday newspaper ad
- [ ] Place second Sunday newspaper ad (different Sunday)
- [ ] Complete 3 additional steps (if professional)
- [ ] Track all applicants
- [ ] Document all rejections with specific reasons
- [ ] Collect and organize all recruitment evidence

### Post-Recruitment
- [ ] Observe 30-day quiet period
- [ ] Post Notice of Filing (10 business days)
- [ ] Prepare recruitment report
- [ ] Ensure within 180-day validity period
- [ ] File ETA-9089

### Post-Filing
- [ ] Monitor for audit letter
- [ ] Prepare audit response package
- [ ] File I-140 within 180 days of approval
- [ ] Retain all documents for 5 years
