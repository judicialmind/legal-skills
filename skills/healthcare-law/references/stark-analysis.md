# Stark Law Analysis Reference Guide

## Overview

The Physician Self-Referral Law, commonly known as the Stark Law, prohibits physicians from referring Medicare or Medicaid patients for designated health services (DHS) to entities with which the physician (or immediate family member) has a financial relationship, unless an exception applies.

**Primary Citation**: 42 USC § 1395nn
**Regulations**: 42 CFR § 411.350 et seq.

---

## Statutory Framework

### The Prohibition (42 USC § 1395nn(a))

#### Referral Prohibition
A physician shall not make a referral to an entity for the furnishing of designated health services for which payment may be made under Medicare or Medicaid if the physician (or an immediate family member) has a **financial relationship** with the entity, unless an exception applies.

#### Billing Prohibition
An entity that furnishes DHS pursuant to a prohibited referral shall not present or cause to be presented a claim for payment.

### Key Characteristics
- **Strict liability statute** - No intent requirement
- **Applies to Medicare and Medicaid** - State Medicaid programs may have additional requirements
- **Self-executing** - If no exception applies, prohibition is automatic

---

## Designated Health Services (42 CFR § 411.351)

The Stark Law applies only to referrals for the following DHS categories:

1. **Clinical laboratory services**
2. **Physical therapy services**
3. **Occupational therapy services**
4. **Outpatient speech-language pathology services**
5. **Radiology and certain other imaging services** (MRI, CT, ultrasound)
6. **Radiation therapy services and supplies**
7. **Durable medical equipment and supplies**
8. **Parenteral and enteral nutrients, equipment, and supplies**
9. **Prosthetics, orthotics, and prosthetic devices and supplies**
10. **Home health services**
11. **Outpatient prescription drugs**
12. **Inpatient and outpatient hospital services**

---

## Financial Relationships (42 CFR § 411.354)

### Ownership or Investment Interest
- **Direct ownership**: Equity, stock, partnership interest
- **Indirect ownership**: Through subsidiary, holding company
- **Includes**: Debt instruments, options, convertible securities
- **Excludes**: Ownership in publicly traded securities and mutual funds (under specific conditions)

### Compensation Arrangement
- **Direct**: Arrangement between physician and DHS entity
- **Indirect**: Unbroken chain of financial relationships; aggregate compensation varies with referrals

#### Indirect Compensation Arrangement Elements (42 CFR § 411.354(c)(2))
1. Unbroken chain of persons/entities between physician and DHS entity
2. Aggregate compensation varies with volume or value of referrals, or other business generated
3. DHS entity has actual knowledge or acts in reckless disregard/deliberate ignorance of this fact

## Key Exceptions

### Ownership/Investment Interest Exceptions

#### Publicly Traded Securities (§ 411.356(a))
- Securities purchased on terms available to the public
- Traded on NYSE, NASDAQ, or similar exchange
- Corporation has stockholder equity exceeding $75 million

#### Rural Providers (§ 411.356(c))
- Entity located in rural area (as defined)
- Substantially all DHS furnished to individuals residing in rural area

### Compensation Arrangement Exceptions

#### Employment Exception (§ 411.357(c))
Requirements:
1. Bona fide employment relationship
2. Compensation for identifiable services
3. Compensation consistent with fair market value (FMV)
4. Compensation not determined by volume or value of referrals (except as permitted)
5. Commercially reasonable even absent referrals
6. Meets additional requirements for productivity bonuses and profit-sharing

#### Personal Service Arrangements (§ 411.357(d))
Requirements:
1. Written agreement signed by parties
2. Specifies services covered
3. If part-time, specifies schedule and duration
4. Term of at least one year
5. Compensation set in advance, consistent with FMV
6. Compensation not determined by volume or value of referrals
7. Services do not exceed those reasonably necessary

#### In-Office Ancillary Services Exception (§ 411.355)

**Purpose**: Permits physicians to provide certain DHS in their offices

##### Location Requirements (satisfy one)
1. **Same building** - Services furnished in same building where referring physician provides physician services unrelated to DHS
2. **Centralized building** - Used by group practice for provision of DHS

##### Supervision Requirements
DHS must be furnished under supervision of:
- Referring physician, OR
- Another physician in the group practice, OR
- Physician, NP, PA, or other individual supervised by referring or group physician to extent permitted

##### Billing Requirements
Billed by one of the following:
- Physician performing or supervising the service
- Group practice in which physician is a member
- Entity wholly owned by physician or group
- Independent third-party billing company

**Excluded Services**: The following do NOT qualify for in-office ancillary exception:
- Parenteral and enteral nutrients
- Durable medical equipment (with limited exceptions)
- Home health services

#### Fair Market Value Exception (§ 411.357(l))
Requirements:
1. Written agreement signed by parties
2. Specifies items or services covered
3. Specifies time frame (at least one year) and schedule if part-time
4. Compensation set in advance, consistent with FMV
5. Compensation not determined by volume or value of referrals
6. Commercially reasonable
7. Services do not involve counseling or promotion of business inconsistent with medical ethics

#### Rental of Office Space (§ 411.357(a))
Requirements:
1. Written lease signed by parties
2. Specifies premises covered
3. If part-time, specifies schedule and duration (at least one year)
4. Aggregate rental charge set in advance, consistent with FMV
5. Aggregate rental charge not determined by volume or value of referrals
6. Commercially reasonable even absent referrals

#### Rental of Equipment (§ 411.357(b))
Requirements:
1. Written lease signed by parties
2. Specifies equipment covered
3. If part-time, specifies schedule and duration (at least one year)
4. Term at least one year
5. Aggregate rental charge set in advance, consistent with FMV
6. Aggregate rental charge not determined by volume or value of referrals
7. Commercially reasonable even absent referrals

#### Isolated Transactions (§ 411.357(f))
Requirements:
1. Consistent with FMV
2. Not determined by volume or value of referrals
3. Commercially reasonable
4. No additional transactions between parties for 6 months (except for commercially reasonable business reasons unrelated to referrals)

### Group Practice Requirements (§ 411.352)

To qualify as a "group practice" for Stark purposes:

1. **Two or more physicians** legally organized as single entity
2. **Full range of services** - Members provide substantially full range of patient care services
3. **Unified business** - Substantially all services provided through group
4. **Expenses and income distributed** per predetermined methods
5. **No productivity bonuses based on DHS referrals** - Overall profits may be distributed based on productivity if not directly tied to DHS referrals
6. **Members personally conduct** no less than 75% of physician-patient encounters

## Stark II Final Rules - Key Modifications

### Phase I (2001) - Clarifications
- Defined compensation arrangements
- Established group practice rules
- Created academic medical center exception

### Phase II (2004) - Refinements
- Indirect compensation arrangements defined
- "Set in advance" clarified
- "Volume or value" standard refined

### Phase III (2007) - Compliance
- Signature requirements
- Writing requirement modifications
- Period of disallowance clarified

### CMS Final Rule (2020)
- Established value-based exception categories
- Modified "commercially reasonable" definition
- Added new exceptions for cybersecurity technology

---

## Value-Based Exceptions (2021 Effective)

### Value-Based Enterprise (VBE) Participants
New exceptions for arrangements within VBEs:

#### Full Financial Risk (§ 411.357(aa))
- VBE assumes full financial risk
- Compensation directly connected to VBE's value-based purposes

#### Meaningful Downside Risk (§ 411.357(bb))
- Physician takes on meaningful financial risk
- Arrangement directly connected to value-based activities

#### Value-Based Arrangements (§ 411.357(cc))
- Compensation is for value-based activities
- Not determined by volume or value of referrals
- Commercially reasonable

## Penalties

### Civil Monetary Penalties (42 USC § 1395nn(g))

| Violation Type | Penalty |
|---------------|---------|
| Improper claim submission | Refund of amounts collected |
| Circumvention scheme | Up to $100,000 per arrangement |
| Failure to meet reporting requirements | Up to $10,000 per day |

### Additional Consequences
1. **False Claims Act liability** - Treble damages plus penalties ($11,665 - $23,331 per claim as adjusted)
2. **Exclusion from federal health care programs**
3. **Loss of Medicare/Medicaid billing privileges**
4. **Civil monetary penalties under CMP statute**

## Stark Law Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Is there a Referral?
- Does the arrangement involve a "referral" as defined?
- Is the referring party a "physician"?

### Step 2: Is the Service a DHS?
- Does the service fall within one of the 11 DHS categories?
- Is payment sought under Medicare or Medicaid?

### Step 3: Is there a Financial Relationship?
- Ownership/investment interest? (Direct or indirect)
- Compensation arrangement? (Direct or indirect)

### Step 4: Does an Exception Apply?
- Analyze all potentially applicable exceptions
- Ensure ALL requirements are met
- Document compliance

### Step 5: Ongoing Compliance
- Monitor for changes in arrangements
- Annual compliance review
- Update documentation as needed

## Fair Market Value Determination

### Methods
1. **Independent appraisal** - Preferred for significant arrangements
2. **Salary surveys** - For employment arrangements (MGMA, AMGA, SullivanCotter)
3. **Market rate analysis** - Comparison to arm's length transactions
4. **Cost-based analysis** - When market data unavailable

### Documentation Requirements
- Methodology used
- Data sources
- Qualifications of appraiser
- Date of valuation
- Assumptions made

## Compliance Best Practices

### Documentation
1. Written agreements for all financial relationships
2. FMV documentation contemporaneous with arrangement
3. Services performed log
4. Periodic compliance audits

### Structural Compliance
1. Compliance officer designation
2. Regular training
3. Hotline for reporting concerns
4. Annual risk assessment

### Self-Disclosure Protocol
CMS Self-Referral Disclosure Protocol (SRDP):
- Voluntary disclosure mechanism
- Potential for reduced penalties
- Settlement in lieu of False Claims Act exposure

## Distinction from Anti-Kickback Statute

| Factor | Stark Law | Anti-Kickback Statute |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Intent | Strict liability | Intent required |
| Scope | Medicare/Medicaid DHS only | All federal health care programs |
| Exception approach | Must fit within exception | Safe harbors available but not required |
| Enforcement | CMS, OIG | DOJ, OIG |
| Civil/Criminal | Civil only | Both civil and criminal |
