# FDA Approval Pathways Reference Guide

## Overview

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) regulates medical devices under the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FD&C Act), 21 USC § 301 et seq., and drugs under the same statute with additional requirements under the Public Health Service Act for biologics.

---

## MEDICAL DEVICE PATHWAYS

### Device Classification System

The FDA classifies medical devices into three regulatory classes based on risk:

| Class | Risk Level | Regulatory Controls | Examples |
|-------|------------|---------------------|----------|
| Class I | Lowest | General controls only | Tongue depressors, bandages |
| Class II | Moderate | General + Special controls | Powered wheelchairs, pregnancy tests |
| Class III | Highest | General + Special + Premarket approval | Pacemakers, heart valves |

**Citation**: 21 USC § 360c; 21 CFR Part 860

---

### 510(k) Premarket Notification

**Citation**: 21 USC § 360(k); 21 CFR Part 807, Subpart E

#### Purpose
Demonstrate that a device is "substantially equivalent" (SE) to a legally marketed predicate device.

#### Applicability
- Most Class II devices
- Some Class I devices with special controls
- Class III devices with predicates (until PMA required)

#### Substantial Equivalence Standard
A device is SE if it:
1. Has the **same intended use** as the predicate, AND
2. Has the **same technological characteristics**, OR
3. Has different technological characteristics but:
   - Does not raise new safety/effectiveness questions, AND
   - Demonstrates substantial equivalence through appropriate testing

#### 510(k) Types

| Type | Description | Review Time |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Traditional | Standard pathway with full review | ~90 days (MDUFA goal) |
| Abbreviated | Uses guidance documents, special controls, or recognized standards | ~60-90 days |
| Special | Design/device modification with minor differences | ~30 days |

#### Submission Requirements
- Device description and specifications
- Intended use statement
- Substantial equivalence comparison
- Performance data (bench, animal, clinical as needed)
- Labeling
- Sterilization information (if applicable)
- Biocompatibility data (if applicable)
- Software documentation (if applicable)

#### Review Process
1. Receipt and acknowledgment
2. Acceptance review (15 days)
3. Substantive review
4. Interactive review (RTA, AI letters)
5. SE/NSE determination

### Premarket Approval (PMA)

**Citation**: 21 USC § 360e; 21 CFR Part 814

#### Purpose
Demonstrate reasonable assurance of safety and effectiveness for Class III devices.

#### Applicability
- Class III devices
- High-risk devices without predicates
- Devices where 510(k) insufficient for safety assurance

#### Approval Standard
"Reasonable assurance of safety and effectiveness" based on:
- Valid scientific evidence
- Well-controlled investigations (typically clinical trials)
- Sufficient for persons qualified to evaluate device safety/effectiveness

#### Submission Requirements
1. **Administrative information**
   - Applicant information
   - Device description
   - Indications for use

2. **Technical sections**
   - Device description and design
   - Manufacturing information
   - Performance standards
   - Non-clinical laboratory studies
   - Clinical investigations
   - Labeling

3. **Clinical Data**
   - Typically requires IDE clinical trial
   - Pivotal study demonstrating safety and effectiveness
   - Statistical analysis

#### PMA Supplements

| Supplement Type | Use | Review Time |
|-----------------|-----|-------------|
| 180-day | Significant changes affecting safety/effectiveness | 180 days |
| Real-Time | Manufacturing changes with protocols | 30 days |
| Special | Minor manufacturing changes | 30 days |
| Panel-Track | Significant change requiring advisory committee | 180+ days |
| 30-day Notice | Minor changes under documented protocols | 30 days |

#### Annual Reports
- Required within 60 days of anniversary date
- Includes manufacturing changes, clinical data, literature review

### De Novo Classification

**Citation**: 21 USC § 360c(f)(2); 21 CFR Part 860

#### Purpose
Risk-based classification pathway for novel, low-to-moderate risk devices without predicates.

#### Eligibility
- Novel device with no predicate
- Device received NSE determination
- Low-to-moderate risk profile
- General/special controls adequate for safety/effectiveness assurance

#### Process Options

1. **Direct De Novo**
   - Submit De Novo request without prior 510(k)
   - Appropriate when no predicate exists

2. **Post-NSE De Novo**
   - Following NSE determination from 510(k)
   - 30-day deadline from NSE to request De Novo

#### Submission Requirements
- Device description
- Proposed classification (Class I or II)
- Proposed special controls
- Risk analysis
- Performance data
- Labeling

#### Outcome
- Device classified into Class I or II
- Creates new device type and regulation
- Serves as predicate for future 510(k)s

### Emergency Use Authorization (EUA)

**Citation**: 21 USC § 360bbb-3

#### Purpose
Authorize unapproved medical products during public health emergencies.

#### Requirements for Issuance
1. **Emergency declaration** by HHS Secretary
2. **Serious or life-threatening condition**
3. **Evidence of effectiveness** - Reasonable belief based on totality of scientific evidence
4. **Known/potential benefits outweigh risks**
5. **No adequate approved alternative** available

#### EUA vs. Full Approval

| Factor | EUA | PMA/510(k) |
|--------|-----|------------|
| Evidence standard | May be effective | Reasonable assurance |
| Duration | Emergency period | Indefinite |
| Conditions | May include restrictions | Standard labeling |
| Revocability | Readily revocable | Full administrative process |

#### Post-EUA Transition
- Manufacturers expected to pursue standard approval
- FDA may revoke EUA when emergency ends
- Transition guidance typically issued

---

### Investigational Device Exemption (IDE)

**Citation**: 21 USC § 360j(g); 21 CFR Part 812

#### Purpose
Permit clinical investigation of devices not yet approved/cleared.

#### Risk Categories

| Category | Requirements |
|----------|--------------|
| Significant Risk (SR) | FDA approval + IRB approval |
| Non-Significant Risk (NSR) | IRB approval only (abbreviated requirements) |
| Exempt Studies | Limited requirements (e.g., already approved devices used according to labeling) |

#### IDE Application Requirements
- Investigational plan
- Manufacturing information
- Investigator agreements
- IRB approvals
- Patient informed consent forms
- Monitoring procedures
- Prior investigations
- Device labeling

## DRUG APPROVAL PATHWAYS

### New Drug Application (NDA)

**Citation**: 21 USC § 355; 21 CFR Part 314

#### Purpose
Seek approval to market a new drug product in the United States.

#### NDA Types

| Type | Section | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 505(b)(1) | Full NDA | Complete reports of safety and effectiveness investigations |
| 505(b)(2) | Abbreviated | Relies on studies not conducted by applicant |

#### Application Components (CTD Format)
1. **Module 1** - Administrative and prescribing information
2. **Module 2** - Summaries (quality, nonclinical, clinical)
3. **Module 3** - Quality (CMC) documentation
4. **Module 4** - Nonclinical study reports
5. **Module 5** - Clinical study reports

#### Review Timeline
- Standard review: 10 months from filing
- Priority review: 6 months from filing
- Filing decision: 60 days from submission

#### Clinical Trial Phases

| Phase | Purpose | Size | Duration |
|-------|---------|------|----------|
| Phase 1 | Safety, pharmacology | 20-100 | Several months |
| Phase 2 | Efficacy signals, dosing | 100-500 | Several months to 2 years |
| Phase 3 | Confirm efficacy, safety monitoring | 1,000-5,000 | 1-4 years |
| Phase 4 | Post-marketing surveillance | Varies | Ongoing |

### Abbreviated New Drug Application (ANDA)

**Citation**: 21 USC § 355(j); 21 CFR Part 314, Subpart C

#### Purpose
Obtain approval for generic drug products that are bioequivalent to previously approved reference listed drugs (RLD).

#### Requirements
- **Same active ingredient** as RLD
- **Same route of administration**
- **Same dosage form**
- **Same strength**
- **Bioequivalence demonstration**

#### Bioequivalence Standards
- Rate and extent of absorption
- Typically demonstrated through pharmacokinetic studies
- 90% confidence interval within 80-125% of RLD

#### Paragraph IV Certifications
When patents listed in Orange Book:
- **Paragraph I** - No patent information
- **Paragraph II** - Patent expired
- **Paragraph III** - Will not market until patent expires
- **Paragraph IV** - Patent invalid or not infringed (triggers 30-month stay if sued)

#### 180-Day Exclusivity
First ANDA filer with Paragraph IV certification may receive 180-day market exclusivity.

### Biologics License Application (BLA)

**Citation**: 42 USC § 262; 21 CFR Part 601

#### Purpose
Obtain licensure for biological products (vaccines, blood products, gene therapy, cellular therapy, monoclonal antibodies, therapeutic proteins).

#### Definition of Biological Product
"Virus, therapeutic serum, toxin, antitoxin, vaccine, blood, blood component or derivative, allergenic product, protein...applicable to the prevention, treatment, or cure of a disease or condition of human beings."

#### BLA Requirements
1. **Manufacturing information** - Complete description of manufacturing process
2. **Facility information** - Establishment registration and inspection
3. **Product characterization** - Identity, purity, potency, stability
4. **Nonclinical data** - Pharmacology, toxicology
5. **Clinical data** - Adequate and well-controlled studies

#### Biosimilar Applications (351(k))
**Citation**: 42 USC § 262(k)

Requirements:
- **Biosimilar** - Highly similar to reference product, no clinically meaningful differences
- **Interchangeable** - Produces same clinical result; can be substituted without prescriber intervention

### Expedited Programs

#### Fast Track Designation
**Citation**: 21 USC § 356

- For serious conditions
- Potential to address unmet medical need
- Benefits: Frequent FDA meetings, rolling review

#### Breakthrough Therapy Designation
**Citation**: 21 USC § 356(a)

- Serious condition
- Preliminary clinical evidence of substantial improvement
- Benefits: Intensive FDA guidance, senior management involvement

#### Accelerated Approval
**Citation**: 21 CFR Part 314, Subpart H; Part 601, Subpart E

- Serious condition
- Meaningful advantage over available therapy
- Based on surrogate endpoint
- Requires confirmatory trials

#### Priority Review
**Citation**: 21 CFR § 314.101

- Significant improvement in safety or effectiveness
- 6-month review (vs. 10 months standard)

---

### Investigational New Drug Application (IND)

**Citation**: 21 USC § 355(i); 21 CFR Part 312

#### Purpose
Permit clinical investigation of unapproved drugs.

#### IND Types
1. **Commercial IND** - Traditional drug development
2. **Research IND** - Academic or non-commercial research
3. **Emergency IND** - Single patient emergency use
4. **Treatment IND** - Expanded access for serious conditions

#### IND Content
- Preclinical data (pharmacology, toxicology)
- Manufacturing information (CMC)
- Clinical protocols
- Investigator information
- Informed consent forms

#### Review Timeline
- 30-day review period
- Study may proceed if no clinical hold
- Clinical hold may be imposed for safety concerns

## Special Pathways and Designations

### Orphan Drug Designation
**Citation**: 21 USC § 360bb; 21 CFR Part 316

- Disease affecting <200,000 persons in US
- Benefits: Tax credits, protocol assistance, 7-year market exclusivity

### Rare Pediatric Disease Priority Review Voucher
- For approved drugs treating rare pediatric diseases
- Voucher transferable/saleable
- May be used for any subsequent application

### Humanitarian Device Exemption (HDE)
**Citation**: 21 USC § 360j(m); 21 CFR Part 814, Subpart H

- Conditions affecting <8,000 patients annually
- Probable benefit outweighs risk
- No reasonable alternative device available
