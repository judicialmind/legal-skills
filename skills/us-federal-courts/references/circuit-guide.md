## Key Procedural Differences

### Notice of Appeal
- **Deadline**: 30 days (civil), 60 days (US party), 14 days (criminal)
- **Extension**: 30 days for excusable neglect (civil only)

### Brief Page Limits (FRAP 32)
| Document | Limit |
|----------|-------|
| Principal brief | 13,000 words or 30 pages |
| Reply brief | 6,500 words or 15 pages |
| Cross-appeal | 15,300 words or 35 pages |

### Oral Argument
- Not guaranteed - must be requested
- Typically 15-20 minutes per side
- Some circuits use screening panels

## Circuit Splits to Watch

| Issue | Split |
|-------|-------|
| Qualified immunity | 5th (narrow) vs. 9th (broader liability) |
| Chevron deference | DC (strong) vs. 5th (skeptical) |
| Arbitration FAA preemption | Varies widely |
| Title VII scope | Evolving post-Bostock |

## En Banc Review
- Requires majority of active judges to grant
- 9th Circuit uses limited en banc (11 judges)
- Very rare (< 1% of cases)

## Certiorari Statistics
- ~7,000 petitions filed annually
- ~70-80 granted (1%)
- Circuit splits increase chances significantly
