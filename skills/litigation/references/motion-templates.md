# Litigation Motion Templates

## Motion to Dismiss (12(b)(6))

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
[DISTRICT]

[CASE CAPTION]
                                    Case No. [XX-cv-XXXX]

DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO DISMISS PLAINTIFF'S COMPLAINT
PURSUANT TO FEDERAL RULE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE 12(b)(6)

Defendant [NAME], by and through undersigned counsel, respectfully moves
this Court pursuant to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 12(b)(6) for an order
dismissing Plaintiff's Complaint for failure to state a claim upon which
relief can be granted. In support of this Motion, Defendant states as follows:

INTRODUCTION

[1-2 paragraph summary of why complaint fails]

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

[Relevant facts from complaint - accepted as true for purposes of motion]

LEGAL STANDARD

To survive a motion to dismiss under Rule 12(b)(6), a complaint must contain
"sufficient factual matter, accepted as true, to 'state a claim to relief
that is plausible on its face.'" Ashcroft v. Iqbal, 556 U.S. 662, 678 (2009)
(quoting Bell Atl. Corp. v. Twombly, 550 U.S. 544, 570 (2007)). A claim is
plausible when the plaintiff "pleads factual content that allows the court
to draw the reasonable inference that the defendant is liable for the
misconduct alleged." Id.

ARGUMENT

I. [CLAIM ONE] FAILS TO STATE A CLAIM

   A. [Element 1 is not adequately pleaded]

   B. [Element 2 is not adequately pleaded]

II. [CLAIM TWO] FAILS TO STATE A CLAIM

III. DISMISSAL SHOULD BE WITH PREJUDICE

CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, Defendant respectfully requests that this Court
dismiss Plaintiff's Complaint in its entirety with prejudice.

Dated: [DATE]

                                    Respectfully submitted,

                                    /s/ [Attorney Name]
                                    [Attorney Name]
                                    [Bar Number]
                                    [Firm Name]
                                    [Address]
                                    [Phone]
                                    [Email]

                                    Counsel for Defendant [NAME]
```

## Motion for Summary Judgment

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
[DISTRICT]

[CASE CAPTION]
                                    Case No. [XX-cv-XXXX]

DEFENDANT'S MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT

Defendant [NAME] respectfully moves this Court for summary judgment pursuant
to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 56 on all claims asserted by Plaintiff.
As demonstrated in the accompanying Memorandum of Law and Statement of
Undisputed Material Facts, there is no genuine dispute of material fact
and Defendant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.

Dated: [DATE]

                                    /s/ [Attorney Name]
                                    Counsel for Defendant


MEMORANDUM OF LAW IN SUPPORT OF
DEFENDANT'S MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT

INTRODUCTION

[2-3 paragraph summary of case and why summary judgment is appropriate]

STATEMENT OF UNDISPUTED MATERIAL FACTS

[Each fact should be numbered and include record citations]

1. [Fact]. (Cite to deposition, document, or declaration, with page/line)

2. [Fact]. (Citation)

3. [Continue...]

LEGAL STANDARD

Summary judgment is appropriate when "there is no genuine dispute as to any
material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law."
Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a). A fact is "material" if it "might affect the outcome
of the suit under the governing law." Anderson v. Liberty Lobby, Inc.,
477 U.S. 242, 248 (1986). A dispute is "genuine" if "the evidence is such
that a reasonable jury could return a verdict for the nonmoving party." Id.

The moving party bears the initial burden of showing the absence of a genuine
dispute of material fact. Celotex Corp. v. Catrett, 477 U.S. 317, 323 (1986).
Once this burden is met, the non-moving party must present specific facts
showing a genuine issue for trial. Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(c)(1).

ARGUMENT

I. PLAINTIFF CANNOT ESTABLISH [ELEMENT] OF [CLAIM]

   A. The Undisputed Facts Show [...]

   B. Plaintiff Lacks Evidence of [...]

II. DEFENDANT IS ENTITLED TO [AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE]

III. ALTERNATIVELY, [PARTIAL SUMMARY JUDGMENT ARGUMENT]

CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, this Court should grant Defendant's Motion for
Summary Judgment and enter judgment in Defendant's favor on all claims.

Dated: [DATE]

                                    /s/ [Attorney Name]
                                    Counsel for Defendant
```

## Opposition to Motion for Summary Judgment

```
PLAINTIFF'S OPPOSITION TO DEFENDANT'S
MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT

INTRODUCTION

[Summary of why genuine disputes of material fact preclude summary judgment]

COUNTER-STATEMENT OF DISPUTED FACTS

[Respond to each of defendant's "undisputed" facts]

1. DISPUTED. [Defendant's assertion] is contradicted by [evidence]. See
   [Citation]. [Explain dispute].

2. UNDISPUTED but IMMATERIAL. [Accept fact but explain why it doesn't matter]

3. DISPUTED. [Continue...]

ADDITIONAL MATERIAL FACTS

[Facts defendant omitted that create genuine disputes]

1. [Fact favorable to plaintiff]. (Citation)

LEGAL STANDARD

[Same as above, but emphasizing inferences must be drawn in non-movant's favor]

ARGUMENT

I. GENUINE DISPUTES OF MATERIAL FACT PRECLUDE SUMMARY JUDGMENT

   A. [Evidence creates triable issue on element]

   B. [Credibility determinations required]

II. VIEWING THE EVIDENCE IN THE LIGHT MOST FAVORABLE TO PLAINTIFF,
    A REASONABLE JURY COULD FIND FOR PLAINTIFF

III. AT MINIMUM, RULE 56(d) RELIEF IS WARRANTED

CONCLUSION

Defendant's Motion for Summary Judgment should be denied.
```

## Motion to Compel Discovery

```
[CASE CAPTION]

PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY RESPONSES

Plaintiff [NAME] moves this Court for an order compelling Defendant to
provide complete responses to Plaintiff's [First Set of Interrogatories /
First Request for Production of Documents] and for sanctions pursuant to
Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 37(a)(5).

CERTIFICATE OF CONFERENCE

Pursuant to [Local Rule] and Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 37(a)(1),
counsel for Plaintiff conferred with counsel for Defendant on [DATE] and
[DATE] in a good faith effort to resolve this dispute without court
intervention. Despite these efforts, the parties remain at an impasse
regarding [specific discovery at issue].

BACKGROUND

1. On [DATE], Plaintiff served [discovery] on Defendant.

2. Defendant's responses were due on [DATE].

3. On [DATE], Defendant served responses that were deficient because [...]

4. On [DATE], counsel conferred and Plaintiff identified specific deficiencies.

5. On [DATE], Defendant [refused to supplement / provided inadequate supplement].

ARGUMENT

I. [INTERROGATORY/REQUEST] NO. [X] - [TOPIC]

   A. The Request Seeks Relevant, Non-Privileged Information

   B. Defendant's Objections Lack Merit

   C. Defendant Should Be Compelled to Respond

II. SANCTIONS ARE APPROPRIATE UNDER RULE 37(a)(5)

CONCLUSION

Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court:
(1) Order Defendant to provide complete responses within [X] days;
(2) Award Plaintiff reasonable expenses, including attorneys' fees; and
(3) Grant such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.

[PROPOSED ORDER attached]
```

## Motion in Limine

```
[CASE CAPTION]

DEFENDANT'S MOTION IN LIMINE NO. [X]
TO EXCLUDE [EVIDENCE/TESTIMONY]

Defendant [NAME] respectfully moves this Court for an order excluding
[description of evidence] from trial.

INTRODUCTION

[Brief description of evidence and why it should be excluded]

BACKGROUND

[Context for the evidence and how it arose in the case]

LEGAL STANDARD

The court has broad discretion to exclude evidence under Federal Rules of
Evidence 402 (relevance) and 403 (prejudice substantially outweighs
probative value). See Old Chief v. United States, 519 U.S. 172, 180 (1997).

[Add specific rule if applicable - e.g., Rule 404(b), hearsay, expert testimony]

ARGUMENT

I. THE EVIDENCE IS [IRRELEVANT / UNDULY PREJUDICIAL / INADMISSIBLE HEARSAY /

grant this Motion in Limine and exclude [evidence] from trial.
```

## Preliminary Injunction Motion

```
[CASE CAPTION]

PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR PRELIMINARY INJUNCTION

Plaintiff [NAME] respectfully moves this Court for a preliminary injunction
pursuant to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 65 [restraining/requiring]
Defendant to [specific relief sought].

INTRODUCTION

[2-3 paragraph summary of emergency and need for injunctive relief]

STATEMENT OF FACTS

[Detailed facts supporting injunction - with declarations attached]

LEGAL STANDARD

A preliminary injunction is appropriate where the movant demonstrates:
(1) a likelihood of success on the merits; (2) a likelihood of irreparable
harm absent the injunction; (3) the balance of hardships favors the movant;
and (4) the injunction serves the public interest. Winter v. NRDC,
555 U.S. 7, 20 (2008).

ARGUMENT

I. PLAINTIFF IS LIKELY TO SUCCEED ON THE MERITS

   A. [Claim 1] - [elements and evidence]

   B. Defendant's Defenses Are Unlikely to Succeed

II. PLAINTIFF WILL SUFFER IRREPARABLE HARM WITHOUT AN INJUNCTION

   [Economic harm generally not sufficient - need harm that cannot be
   compensated by damages, such as loss of goodwill, constitutional
   violations, ongoing harm]

III. THE BALANCE OF HARDSHIPS FAVORS PLAINTIFF

   [Plaintiff's harm vs. burden on defendant from injunction]

IV. THE PUBLIC INTEREST SUPPORTS AN INJUNCTION

   [Policy considerations, third-party effects]

V. NO BOND OR NOMINAL BOND IS APPROPRIATE

CONCLUSION

Plaintiff requests a preliminary injunction [specific terms of requested order].

[Declarations and exhibits attached]
[Proposed Order attached]
```
