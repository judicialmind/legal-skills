# Discovery Forms and Templates

## Overview

This reference provides templates and guidance for the three primary written discovery devices under the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure: Interrogatories (Rule 33), Requests for Production (Rule 34), and Requests for Admission (Rule 36). Each section includes form language, common objections, and practical guidance.

---

## Interrogatories (FRCP Rule 33)

### Rule Summary

- Limited to 25 interrogatories (including subparts) without leave of court
- Must be answered under oath within 30 days
- Must respond or object to each interrogatory
- Objections must state grounds with specificity
- May answer by producing business records if burden of deriving answer is substantially the same

### Form: Interrogatories

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
[DISTRICT NAME]

[CASE CAPTION]

PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to Rule 33 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Plaintiff
[NAME] requests that Defendant [NAME] answer the following interrogatories
under oath within thirty (30) days of service.

DEFINITIONS

1. "You" and "Your" refer to Defendant [NAME], including its officers,
   directors, employees, agents, representatives, attorneys, and all persons
   acting on its behalf.

2. "Document" means the original or any copy of any written, printed, typed,
   electronic, or graphic matter of any kind or nature, however produced or
   reproduced, including but not limited to correspondence, memoranda, notes,
   reports, emails, text messages, spreadsheets, presentations, contracts,
   and any other recorded information.

3. "Communication" means any oral or written exchange of information,
   including but not limited to conversations, meetings, telephone calls,
   emails, letters, text messages, and instant messages.

4. "Identify" when used with respect to a person means to state the person's
   full name, present or last known address, telephone number, employer, and
   job title.

5. "Identify" when used with respect to a document means to state its date,
   author, recipients, subject matter, and present location or custodian.

INSTRUCTIONS

1. These interrogatories are continuing in nature. You are required to
   supplement your responses pursuant to Rule 26(e).

2. If you object to any interrogatory, state the specific grounds for the
   objection. If you object to only part of an interrogatory, answer the
   remainder.

3. If any information is withheld based on a claim of privilege, identify
   the document or communication withheld, the privilege claimed, and the
   factual basis for the privilege.

INTERROGATORIES

INTERROGATORY NO. 1:
Identify all persons who have knowledge of facts relevant to this litigation
and describe the subject matter of their knowledge.

INTERROGATORY NO. 2:
Identify all documents that support, refer to, or relate to any defense You
intend to assert in this action.

INTERROGATORY NO. 3:
Describe in detail the factual basis for each affirmative defense asserted
in Your Answer.

INTERROGATORY NO. 4:
Identify all persons who participated in, witnessed, or have knowledge of
the events described in Paragraphs [X] through [Y] of the Complaint.

INTERROGATORY NO. 5:
For each expert witness You expect to call at trial, state:
(a) The expert's name, address, and qualifications;
(b) The subject matter of the expected testimony;
(c) The facts and data considered by the expert;
(d) The expert's opinions and the bases therefor;
(e) The compensation to be paid for the expert's study and testimony.

INTERROGATORY NO. 6:
Describe all policies, procedures, or practices in effect at [RELEVANT TIME]
regarding [RELEVANT SUBJECT MATTER].

INTERROGATORY NO. 7:
Identify all contracts or agreements between You and [PARTY/ENTITY] from
[DATE] to the present, and describe the subject matter of each.

INTERROGATORY NO. 8:
State the amount of damages You contend You have suffered, itemize each
component of damages, and explain the basis for each calculation.

INTERROGATORY NO. 9:
Identify all insurance agreements that may cover any judgment or settlement
in this action, including the policy limits.

INTERROGATORY NO. 10:
Describe all communications between You and [RELEVANT PARTY] concerning
[SUBJECT MATTER] from [DATE] to [DATE].

Dated: _____________

                                    Respectfully submitted,

                                    /s/ [Attorney Name]
                                    [Attorney Name]
                                    [Firm Name]
                                    [Address]
                                    [Phone/Email]
                                    Counsel for Plaintiff
```

### Common Interrogatory Objections

**Overbroad:**
"Objection. This interrogatory is overbroad and unduly burdensome in that it seeks information regarding [scope] without limitation as to time period, subject matter, or relevance to the claims or defenses in this action."

**Vague and Ambiguous:**
"Objection. This interrogatory is vague and ambiguous in that the term '[term]' is undefined and susceptible to multiple interpretations."

**Attorney-Client Privilege:**
"Objection. This interrogatory seeks information protected by the attorney-client privilege. [Describe nature of withheld information without waiving privilege.]"

**Work Product:**
"Objection. This interrogatory seeks information protected by the work product doctrine as it requests disclosure of mental impressions, conclusions, and opinions of counsel prepared in anticipation of litigation."

**Not Reasonably Calculated to Lead to Discoverable Information:**
"Objection. This interrogatory is not relevant to any party's claim or defense and is not proportional to the needs of the case considering [factors]."

**Contention Interrogatory (Premature):**
"Objection. This contention interrogatory is premature as fact discovery has not concluded. Subject to and without waiving this objection, Defendant responds as follows..."

## Requests for Production (FRCP Rule 34)

### Rule Summary

- No numerical limit (but proportionality applies)
- Response due within 30 days of service
- Must describe items with reasonable particularity
- May request inspection of tangible things or entry onto land
- Must produce documents as kept in ordinary course or organize/label by request

### Form: Requests for Production

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
[DISTRICT NAME]

[CASE CAPTION]

PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to Rule 34 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Plaintiff
[NAME] requests that Defendant [NAME] produce the following documents and
things for inspection and copying within thirty (30) days at the offices of
[FIRM], or at another mutually agreeable location.

DEFINITIONS

[Include same definitions as Interrogatories, plus:]

6. "Relating to" or "concerning" means referring to, describing, evidencing,
   constituting, mentioning, connected with, pertaining to, or in any way
   relating to the subject matter.

7. "ESI" means electronically stored information as defined in Rule 34(a),
   including emails, databases, spreadsheets, word processing documents,
   presentations, and metadata.

INSTRUCTIONS

1. Documents shall be produced as they are kept in the usual course of
   business or organized and labeled to correspond to the categories in
   these requests.

2. If any document is withheld on the basis of privilege, provide a
   privilege log identifying: (a) the type of document; (b) general subject
   matter; (c) date; (d) author; (e) recipients; and (f) privilege claimed.

3. Produce all responsive documents in Your possession, custody, or control,
   including documents in the possession of Your agents, attorneys,
   accountants, or other representatives.

4. For ESI, produce documents in their native format or in a reasonably
   usable form. Preserve and produce relevant metadata.

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION

REQUEST NO. 1:
All documents identified in Your responses to Plaintiff's First Set of
Interrogatories.

REQUEST NO. 2:
All contracts, agreements, or amendments between You and [PARTY] from [DATE]
to the present.

REQUEST NO. 3:
All correspondence, including emails and text messages, between You and
[PARTY/PERSON] concerning [SUBJECT MATTER] from [DATE] to [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 4:
All documents concerning Your policies, procedures, or practices regarding
[RELEVANT SUBJECT MATTER] in effect from [DATE] to [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 5:
All documents supporting, contradicting, or relating to the allegations in
Paragraphs [X] through [Y] of the Complaint.

REQUEST NO. 6:
All documents supporting any affirmative defense asserted in Your Answer.

REQUEST NO. 7:
All documents concerning the calculation or determination of any damages
You claim in this action.

REQUEST NO. 8:
All reports, analyses, or summaries concerning [RELEVANT SUBJECT MATTER]
prepared by or for You from [DATE] to [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 9:
Personnel files, including performance evaluations, for [EMPLOYEE NAME(S)]
from [DATE] to [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 10:
All documents reviewed, relied upon, or prepared by any expert witness You
intend to call at trial.

REQUEST NO. 11:
The complete contents of any investigation files concerning [INCIDENT/MATTER].

REQUEST NO. 12:
All photographs, videos, or recordings relating to [INCIDENT/SUBJECT].

REQUEST NO. 13:
All insurance policies that may provide coverage for claims asserted in
this litigation.

REQUEST NO. 14:
All organizational charts showing the reporting structure for [DEPARTMENT/
DIVISION] from [DATE] to [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 15:
All documents concerning any litigation, claims, complaints, or disputes
involving [SIMILAR SUBJECT MATTER] from [DATE] to [DATE].

Dated: _____________

                                    Respectfully submitted,

                                    /s/ [Attorney Name]
                                    [Counsel information]
```

### Common Production Objections

**Overbroad and Unduly Burdensome:**
"Objection. This request is overbroad and unduly burdensome as it seeks 'all documents' relating to [broad topic] without reasonable limitation. Responding would require review of [describe burden]. Subject to this objection, Defendant will produce [limited scope of production]."

**Disproportionate:**
"Objection. This request is not proportional to the needs of the case under Rule 26(b)(1). The burden and expense of the proposed discovery outweighs its likely benefit considering [factors: amount in controversy, parties' resources, importance of issues, importance of discovery to resolving issues]."

**Confidential/Trade Secret:**
"Objection. This request seeks documents containing confidential business information and trade secrets. Subject to entry of an appropriate protective order, Defendant will produce responsive, non-privileged documents with confidentiality designations."

**Third-Party Privacy:**
"Objection. This request seeks private information of non-parties. Subject to appropriate redaction of personal identifying information and/or notice to affected individuals, Defendant will produce responsive documents."

**Not in Possession, Custody, or Control:**
"Objection. The documents requested are not within Defendant's possession, custody, or control. [If known, identify custodian.]"

## Requests for Admission (FRCP Rule 36)

### Rule Summary

- No numerical limit (but reasonableness applies)
- Response due within 30 days
- Matters admitted are conclusively established for purposes of pending action
- May request admission of facts, application of law to fact, or genuineness of documents
- Failure to respond = admission
- Must specifically admit, deny, or explain inability to admit or deny

### Form: Requests for Admission

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
[DISTRICT NAME]

[CASE CAPTION]

PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to Rule 36 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Plaintiff
[NAME] requests that Defendant [NAME] admit or deny the following matters
within thirty (30) days of service.

INSTRUCTIONS

1. If you cannot truthfully admit or deny a request, you must state in
   detail why you cannot do so.

2. When a request relates to part of a matter, admit the part that is true
   and deny or qualify the remainder.

3. If you contend that any matter is privileged, state the basis for the
   privilege.

4. These requests are continuing. Supplement your responses as required
   by Rule 26(e).

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION

REQUEST NO. 1:
Admit that the document attached hereto as Exhibit A is a true and correct
copy of the [CONTRACT/AGREEMENT] between Plaintiff and Defendant dated
[DATE].

REQUEST NO. 2:
Admit that You signed the document attached as Exhibit A.

REQUEST NO. 3:
Admit that [SPECIFIC FACT FROM COMPLAINT].

REQUEST NO. 4:
Admit that on [DATE], You [SPECIFIC ACTION].

REQUEST NO. 5:
Admit that [EMPLOYEE NAME] was acting within the scope of employment with
Defendant on [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 6:
Admit that Defendant received Plaintiff's [LETTER/NOTICE] dated [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 7:
Admit that Defendant did not respond to Plaintiff's [LETTER/NOTICE] dated
[DATE] within [TIME PERIOD].

REQUEST NO. 8:
Admit that Defendant's [POLICY/PROCEDURE] required [SPECIFIC REQUIREMENT]
on [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 9:
Admit that Defendant failed to [SPECIFIC ACTION] as required by [STATUTE/
CONTRACT/POLICY].

REQUEST NO. 10:
Admit that the document attached as Exhibit B accurately reflects [SUBJECT
MATTER, e.g., payments made by Plaintiff to Defendant in 2023].

REQUEST NO. 11:
Admit that Defendant had actual knowledge of [SPECIFIC FACT] on or before
[DATE].

REQUEST NO. 12:
Admit that Plaintiff suffered damages as a result of Defendant's [CONDUCT].

REQUEST NO. 13:
Admit that the [SPECIFIC PRODUCT/SERVICE] failed to [PERFORM/CONFORM TO
SPECIFICATION] on [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 14:
Admit that the photograph attached as Exhibit C accurately depicts [SUBJECT]
as it appeared on [DATE].

REQUEST NO. 15:
Admit that no written agreement exists between Plaintiff and Defendant
regarding [SUBJECT MATTER] other than [IDENTIFIED AGREEMENT].

Dated: _____________

                                    Respectfully submitted,

                                    /s/ [Attorney Name]
                                    [Counsel information]
```

### Proper Responses to Requests for Admission

**Admission:**
"Admitted."

**Denial:**
"Denied."

**Qualified Admission:**
"Admitted that [partial admission]. Denied to the extent this Request
suggests [clarification of what is denied]."

**Lack of Information:**
"Defendant cannot, after reasonable inquiry, admit or deny this Request.
The information known and readily obtainable is insufficient to enable
Defendant to admit or deny. [State specifically what inquiry was made and
why it was insufficient.]"

**Objection (with response):**
"Objection. This Request is vague and ambiguous because [explanation].
Subject to and without waiving this objection, Defendant [admits/denies]
that [response]."

### Cost-Shifting (Rule 37(c)(2))

If a party fails to admit and the requesting party later proves the matter:
- Court may order payment of reasonable expenses, including attorney's fees
- Exceptions: objection was sustained, admission was not important, party had reasonable ground to believe it might prevail, other good reason

## Supplementation Obligations (Rule 26(e))

All discovery responses must be supplemented if:
- Information provided was incomplete or incorrect
- Additional or corrective information has not been made known during discovery or in writing

Timing: Supplementation must be made in a timely manner

---

## Discovery Dispute Resolution

### Meet and Confer Requirement (Rule 37(a)(1))
Before filing any discovery motion, movant must certify that a good faith
effort was made to resolve the dispute without court action.

### Motion to Compel (Rule 37(a))
- File if party fails to respond or provides evasive/incomplete response
- May seek expenses and fees under Rule 37(a)(5)

### Protective Order (Rule 26(c))
- File if discovery request is oppressive, burdensome, or seeks confidential information
- Court may limit scope, method, or timing of discovery

---

## Practice Tips

1. **Tailor requests** to the specific case; avoid boilerplate when possible
2. **Track numerical limits** on interrogatories; subparts count
3. **Preserve objections** while still providing substantive responses
4. **Use RFAs strategically** to establish undisputed facts and authenticate documents
5. **Maintain privilege logs** contemporaneously with document review
6. **Meet and confer genuinely** before seeking court intervention
7. **Propound early** to allow time for follow-up discovery
8. **Consider proportionality** in both drafting requests and raising objections
