# Criminal Appeals Reference Guide

## Overview

Criminal appeals provide defendants the opportunity to challenge convictions and sentences based on errors of law that occurred during trial proceedings. This guide covers the primary post-conviction remedies available in federal court.

---

## Direct Appeal

### Timeline and Jurisdiction

- **Notice of Appeal**: Must be filed within 14 days of entry of judgment (Fed. R. App. P. 4(b)(1)(A))
- **Extension**: Court may extend for excusable neglect or good cause up to 30 additional days
- **Jurisdiction**: Appeal lies to the Circuit Court of Appeals for the circuit where the district court sits

### Preservation of Issues

**Contemporaneous Objection Rule**
- Issues must be raised and ruled upon at trial to be preserved for appeal
- Failure to object results in plain error review on appeal

**Plain Error Standard** (Fed. R. Crim. P. 52(b))
1. There must be an error
2. The error must be plain (clear or obvious)
3. The error must affect substantial rights
4. The error must seriously affect the fairness, integrity, or public reputation of judicial proceedings

### Standard of Review

| Issue Type | Standard | Application |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Questions of Law | De novo | Statutory interpretation, constitutional issues |
| Factual Findings | Clear error | Sufficiency of evidence to support findings |
| Discretionary Rulings | Abuse of discretion | Evidentiary rulings, sentencing decisions |
| Mixed Questions | Varies | Depends on predominant character |

### Anders Brief Procedure

When appointed counsel finds appeal frivolous:
1. File motion to withdraw with brief citing anything arguably supporting appeal
2. Furnish copy to defendant
3. Defendant may file pro se response
4. Court independently reviews record
5. If appeal frivolous, grant withdrawal; if not, appoint new counsel

## Motion Under 28 U.S.C. Section 2255

### Overview

Section 2255 allows federal prisoners to collaterally attack their sentence or conviction based on:
- Sentence imposed in violation of Constitution or federal law
- Court lacked jurisdiction
- Sentence exceeded maximum authorized by law
- Sentence otherwise subject to collateral attack

### Timing Requirements

**One-Year Limitations Period** (28 U.S.C. Section 2255(f))
Clock starts from latest of:
1. Date judgment of conviction becomes final
2. Date impediment created by government is removed
3. Date Supreme Court recognizes new constitutional right made retroactive
4. Date facts supporting claim could have been discovered with due diligence

### Procedural Default

**Cause and Prejudice Standard**
- Claims not raised on direct appeal are procedurally defaulted
- To overcome default, must show:
  - Cause: External impediment preventing claim
  - Prejudice: Actual harm from alleged error

**Exceptions**
- Actual innocence (Schlup gateway)
- Ineffective assistance of counsel as cause

### Successive Petitions

**Certification Required** (28 U.S.C. Section 2255(h))
Second or successive motion requires certification from Court of Appeals based on:
1. Newly discovered evidence that would establish innocence by clear and convincing evidence, OR
2. New rule of constitutional law made retroactive by Supreme Court

## Habeas Corpus (28 U.S.C. Section 2241)

### When Available for Federal Prisoners

Generally, Section 2255 is exclusive remedy, but Section 2241 available when:
- Section 2255 remedy is "inadequate or ineffective"
- Savings clause applies (circuit-specific standards)

### Common Savings Clause Scenarios

- Statutory interpretation claims after circuit precedent change
- Claims based on Supreme Court decisions not retroactive under Section 2255
- Actual innocence based on intervening statutory interpretation

### Exhaustion Requirements

- Must exhaust administrative remedies before filing
- BOP Administrative Remedy Program (28 C.F.R. Part 542)

## Ineffective Assistance of Counsel Claims

### Strickland v. Washington Standard (466 U.S. 668 (1984))

**Two-Prong Test**
1. **Deficient Performance**: Counsel's representation fell below objective standard of reasonableness
2. **Prejudice**: Reasonable probability that but for counsel's errors, result would have been different

### Application at Trial

**Presumption of Competence**
- Strong presumption counsel's conduct falls within wide range of reasonable professional assistance
- Court must evaluate from counsel's perspective at time

**Examples of Deficient Performance**
- Failure to investigate and present viable defense
- Failure to object to inadmissible evidence
- Failure to request appropriate jury instructions
- Failure to file timely notice of appeal when requested

### Application at Sentencing

**Hill v. Lockhart Standard** (474 U.S. 52 (1985))
For guilty pleas, prejudice requires showing reasonable probability defendant would not have pleaded guilty and would have insisted on trial

**Lafler/Frye Claims**
- Counsel's failure to communicate plea offer
- Counsel's bad advice leading to rejection of favorable plea
- Must show reasonable probability of accepting plea and court would have accepted it

### Application on Appeal

**Ineffective Assistance of Appellate Counsel**
- Failure to raise obvious winning issues
- Evaluated under same Strickland standard
- Generally raised in Section 2255, not on direct appeal

## Certificate of Appealability

### Requirement (28 U.S.C. Section 2253(c))

For appeals from Section 2255 denials:
- Must obtain certificate of appealability (COA)
- District court or circuit court may issue

### Standard

**Slack v. McDaniel** (529 U.S. 473 (2000))
- For merits denial: Reasonable jurists could debate whether petition states valid claim
- For procedural denial: Reasonable jurists could debate whether petition states valid claim AND whether district court was correct in procedural ruling

## Practical Considerations

### Record Development

**Evidentiary Hearing**
- Movant entitled to hearing unless motion, files, and records conclusively show no relief entitled
- Hearing required when material facts in dispute

**Expansion of Record**
- May supplement with affidavits, declarations
- Discovery available upon good cause showing

### Counsel Appointment

- No constitutional right to counsel in Section 2255 proceedings
- Court may appoint counsel if interests of justice require (18 U.S.C. Section 3006A(a)(2)(B))
- Typically appointed when evidentiary hearing ordered

### Time Considerations

| Stage | Typical Timeline |
|-------|------------------|
| Direct Appeal Brief | 40 days after record filed |
| Government Response | 30 days after appellant's brief |
| Reply Brief | 21 days after response |
| Oral Argument | 2-6 months after briefing |
| Decision | 2-12 months after argument |

### Waiver Issues

**Appeal Waivers in Plea Agreements**
- Generally enforceable if knowing and voluntary
- Exceptions: Sentence above statutory maximum, based on unconstitutional factor
- Ineffective assistance claims generally survive waiver

## Checklist for Appeal Analysis

- [ ] Was notice of appeal timely filed?
- [ ] Were issues properly preserved at trial?
- [ ] What standard of review applies to each issue?
- [ ] Are there any plain error issues worth raising?
- [ ] Is Section 2255 deadline calculated correctly?
- [ ] Are there any IAC claims to investigate?
- [ ] Does appeal waiver apply?
- [ ] Is COA required and obtainable?
- [ ] Has defendant been advised of risks and benefits?
