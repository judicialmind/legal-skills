# Federal Sentencing Guidelines Reference Guide

## Overview

The United States Sentencing Guidelines provide a framework for federal sentencing following United States v. Booker (2005), which rendered them advisory. Judges must consider the Guidelines but may vary based on 18 U.S.C. Section 3553(a) factors.

---

## The Sentencing Table

### Structure

The Sentencing Table creates a grid with:
- **Vertical Axis**: Offense Level (1-43)
- **Horizontal Axis**: Criminal History Category (I-VI)
- **Cells**: Sentencing range in months

### Zone System

| Zone | Offense Levels | Available Sentences |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| Zone A | 1-8 (CHC I) | Probation available |
| Zone B | 9-10 (varies) | Probation with conditions or imprisonment |
| Zone C | 11-12 (varies) | At least half imprisonment or intermittent confinement |
| Zone D | 13+ (varies) | Imprisonment required |

### Step 2: Apply Specific Offense Characteristics

Each guideline contains specific offense characteristics that adjust the base level.

**Example: Drug Trafficking (Section 2D1.1)**

| Drug Quantity | Base Level |
|---------------|------------|
| 30+ kg heroin | 38 |
| 10-30 kg heroin | 36 |
| 3-10 kg heroin | 34 |
| 1-3 kg heroin | 32 |
| 700g-1kg heroin | 30 |
| 400-700g heroin | 28 |
| 100-400g heroin | 26 |
| 80-100g heroin | 24 |
| 60-80g heroin | 22 |
| 40-60g heroin | 20 |

**Common Specific Offense Characteristics**
- Weapon involvement: +2 to +6
- Leadership role: +2 to +4
- Vulnerable victim: +2
- Loss amount (fraud): Varies by amount
- Number of victims: +2 to +6

## Chapter 3 Adjustments

### Victim-Related Adjustments (Part A)

| Adjustment | Levels |
|------------|--------|
| Vulnerable Victim (Section 3A1.1(b)) | +2 |
| Official Victim (Section 3A1.2) | +3 to +6 |
| Restraint of Victim (Section 3A1.3) | +2 |
| Hate Crime Motivation (Section 3A1.1(a)) | +3 |

### Role in the Offense (Part B)

**Aggravating Roles (Section 3B1.1)**

| Role | Levels | Requirements |
|------|--------|--------------|
| Organizer/Leader (5+ participants or extensive) | +4 | Led 5+ participants OR organization was otherwise extensive |
| Manager/Supervisor (5+ participants) | +3 | Managed 5+ participants |
| Organizer/Leader (otherwise) | +3 | Led criminal activity |
| Manager/Supervisor (otherwise) | +2 | Exercised management responsibility |

**Mitigating Roles (Section 3B1.2)**

| Role | Levels | Requirements |
|------|--------|--------------|
| Minimal Participant | -4 | Substantially less culpable than average participant |
| Minor Participant | -2 | Less culpable than most other participants |
| Between Minimal and Minor | -3 | Court's discretion |

**Abuse of Trust (Section 3B1.3)**: +2

### Obstruction of Justice (Part C)

**Section 3C1.1**: +2 levels for:
- Perjury
- Suborning perjury
- Witness tampering
- Destroying evidence
- Fleeing from justice

### Multiple Counts (Part D)

**Grouping Rules (Section 3D1.2)**
Counts grouped when:
- Same victim and same act/transaction
- Same victim and connected common scheme
- Counts embody ongoing conduct

**Combined Offense Level (Section 3D1.4)**

| Number of Units | Increase |
|-----------------|----------|
| 1 | None |
| 1.5 | +1 |
| 2 | +2 |
| 2.5-3 | +3 |
| 3.5-5 | +4 |
| More than 5 | +5 |

### Acceptance of Responsibility (Part E)

**Section 3E1.1**
- 2-level decrease for clearly demonstrating acceptance
- Additional 1-level decrease for:
  - Offense level 16 or greater, AND
  - Timely notification of intent to plead guilty

**Factors Indicating Acceptance**
- Truthful admission of conduct
- Voluntary termination of criminal conduct
- Voluntary surrender to authorities
- Voluntary payment of restitution
- Voluntary assistance to authorities
- Timely guilty plea

## Criminal History Calculation

### Criminal History Points

**Prior Sentences (Section 4A1.1)**

| Prior Sentence Length | Points |
|-----------------------|--------|
| More than 1 year and 1 month imprisonment | 3 |
| 60 days to 1 year and 1 month imprisonment | 2 |
| Less than 60 days or probation | 1 |

**Additional Points**
- Crime committed while under sentence: +2
- Crime committed less than 2 years after release: +1

### Criminal History Categories

| Points | Category |
|--------|----------|
| 0-1 | I |
| 2-3 | II |
| 4-6 | III |
| 7-9 | IV |
| 10-12 | V |
| 13+ | VI |

### Career Offender (Section 4B1.1)

**Requirements**
1. Defendant was at least 18 at time of offense
2. Instant offense is felony crime of violence or controlled substance offense
3. Two or more prior felony convictions for crime of violence or controlled substance offense

**Effect**
- Offense level at least 17 (higher if otherwise applicable)
- Criminal history category VI

### Armed Career Criminal (Section 4B1.4)

**18 U.S.C. Section 924(e) Predicate**
- Three or more prior convictions for:
  - Violent felony, or
  - Serious drug offense
- Minimum 15 years imprisonment

## Departures

### Downward Departures

**Substantial Assistance (Section 5K1.1)**
- Government motion required (with limited exceptions)
- Consider: Significance, truthfulness, nature, timeliness, reliability
- May depart below mandatory minimum (18 U.S.C. Section 3553(e))

**Overrepresented Criminal History (Section 4A1.3(b))**
- Criminal history category overstates seriousness
- Based on information in presentence report

**Aberrant Behavior (Section 5K2.20)**
- Single criminal occurrence or transaction
- Committed without significant planning
- Limited duration
- Not part of pattern

**Other Downward Departures**
- Diminished capacity (Section 5K2.13)
- Post-offense rehabilitation (Section 5K2.19)
- Military service (Section 5H1.11)
- Age (Section 5H1.1)
- Physical condition (Section 5H1.4)

### Upward Departures

- Inadequate criminal history representation (Section 4A1.3(a))
- Extreme conduct (Section 5K2.8)
- Threatened witness (Section 5K2.15)
- Terrorism (Section 3A1.4)

## Variances Under Section 3553(a)

### The Section 3553(a) Factors

Post-Booker, courts must consider:

1. **Nature and circumstances of the offense**
2. **History and characteristics of the defendant**
3. **Need for the sentence to:**
   - Reflect seriousness of offense
   - Promote respect for law
   - Provide just punishment
   - Afford adequate deterrence
   - Protect public from further crimes
   - Provide defendant with needed treatment/training
4. **Kinds of sentences available**
5. **Applicable guideline range**
6. **Pertinent policy statements**
7. **Need to avoid unwarranted sentencing disparities**
8. **Need to provide restitution to victims**

### Procedural Reasonableness

**Gall v. United States** (2007)
- Calculate correct guideline range
- Permit both parties to argue
- Consider Section 3553(a) factors
- Adequately explain chosen sentence
- Must give serious consideration to extent of deviation

### Substantive Reasonableness

**Appellate Review**
- Abuse of discretion standard
- May consider degree of variance
- Presumption of reasonableness for within-guidelines sentence
- No presumption of unreasonableness for variance

## Mandatory Minimums

### Drug Offenses (21 U.S.C. Section 841)

| Quantity | First Offense | Prior Felony Drug | Two+ Prior Felonies |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1+ kg heroin | 10 years-life | 15 years-life | 25 years-life |
| 100g+ heroin | 5-40 years | 10 years-life | 15 years-life |

### Safety Valve (18 U.S.C. Section 3553(f))

**Requirements**
1. No more than 1 criminal history point
2. No violence or weapons
3. Offense did not result in death or serious injury
4. Not organizer, leader, manager, or supervisor
5. Truthfully provided all information to government

**Effect**: Court may sentence without regard to statutory minimum

### First Step Act Modifications

- Reduced enhancements for prior drug felonies
- Expanded safety valve eligibility
- Allows compassionate release motions by defendants
- Good time credit calculation corrected

## Restitution

### Mandatory Restitution Offenses

- Crimes of violence (18 U.S.C. Section 3663A)
- Property offenses (18 U.S.C. Section 3663A)
- Fraud offenses
- Drug offenses (in certain circumstances)

### Calculation

- Actual loss to identifiable victims
- Return of property or out-of-pocket expenses
- In fraud: Loss to victims, minus recovery

## Supervised Release

### Statutory Minimums

| Offense Class | Minimum Term |
|---------------|--------------|
| Class A or B felony | 3-5 years |
| Class C or D felony | 2-3 years |
| Class E felony or misdemeanor | 1 year |

### Standard Conditions

- Regular reporting to probation officer
- Employment or education
- No possession of controlled substances
- No possession of firearm
- DNA sample
- Substance abuse treatment if appropriate

### Revocation

- Guidelines provide suggested ranges
- Based on violation grade and criminal history
- Court may impose any sentence up to original maximum

## Special Assessments and Fines

### Mandatory Assessment (18 U.S.C. Section 3013)

| Offense Type | Amount |
|--------------|--------|
| Felony | $100 per count |
| Class A Misdemeanor | $25 per count |
| Class B or C Misdemeanor | $10 per count |
| Infraction | $5 per count |

### Fines (Section 5E1.2)

Based on:
- Offense level
- Ability to pay
- Financial burden on dependents
- Restitution ordered
