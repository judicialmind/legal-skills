# Commercial Lease Key Provisions

## Overview

This reference document covers the essential provisions in commercial leases, including office, retail, and industrial leases. Understanding these provisions is critical for both landlords and tenants in negotiating and administering commercial lease agreements.

---

## 1. Fundamental Lease Terms

### 1.1 Premises Description

**Key Elements**:
- Legal description of the property
- Suite or unit number
- Square footage (rentable vs. usable)
- Common areas included in lease
- Parking allocations
- Storage areas

**Measurement Standards**:
- **BOMA Office Standard**: Building Owners and Managers Association method for measuring office space
- **Usable Square Feet (USF)**: Actual space occupied by tenant
- **Rentable Square Feet (RSF)**: USF plus tenant's pro rata share of common areas
- **Load Factor**: Ratio of RSF to USF (typically 10-20% for office)

**Sample Language**:
> The Premises consists of approximately [#] rentable square feet located on the [#] floor of the Building, as shown on Exhibit A attached hereto. The rentable square footage has been calculated in accordance with the BOMA 2017 Office Standard.

### 1.2 Lease Term

**Components**:
- Commencement date
- Expiration date
- Term length
- Early access period
- Rent commencement date (may differ from lease commencement)
- Conditions to commencement

**Commencement Date Alternatives**:
- Fixed date certain
- Upon substantial completion of tenant improvements
- Upon delivery of premises in specified condition
- Upon issuance of certificate of occupancy

**Sample Language**:
> The Term shall commence on the Commencement Date and shall expire at 11:59 p.m. on the last day of the [#]th full calendar month following the Commencement Date, unless sooner terminated or extended as provided herein.

### 1.3 Base Rent

**Structure Options**:
- Flat rent (same throughout term)
- Stepped increases (fixed annual increases)
- CPI adjustments (indexed to inflation)
- Percentage rent (retail)
- Fair market value resets

**Sample Rent Schedule**:
```
Lease Year    Monthly Base Rent    Annual Base Rent    Per RSF
----------    ----------------    ----------------    -------
Year 1        $25,000.00          $300,000.00         $30.00
Year 2        $25,750.00          $309,000.00         $30.90
Year 3        $26,522.50          $318,270.00         $31.83
Year 4        $27,318.18          $327,818.16         $32.78
Year 5        $28,137.72          $337,652.64         $33.77
```

### 1.4 Security Deposit

**Common Terms**:
- Amount (typically 1-3 months' rent)
- Form (cash, letter of credit)
- Interest (required in some jurisdictions)
- Use by landlord (last month's rent, damages)
- Return timing (30-60 days after lease termination)
- Burn-down provisions (reduction over time)

**Letter of Credit Requirements**:
- Issuer requirements (acceptable banks)
- Evergreen provision (auto-renewal)
- Sight draft language
- Transfer rights

**Sample Language**:
> Upon execution of this Lease, Tenant shall deliver to Landlord a Security Deposit in the amount of [Amount] as security for the faithful performance of all Tenant's obligations. Landlord shall hold the Security Deposit without obligation to pay interest and may commingle it with Landlord's general funds.

## 2. Additional Rent and Operating Expenses

### 2.1 Common Area Maintenance (CAM)

**Components Typically Included**:
- Cleaning and janitorial services
- Landscaping and grounds maintenance
- Parking lot maintenance and repairs
- Security services
- Utilities for common areas
- Management fees (typically capped at 3-5%)
- Insurance (property and liability)
- Repairs and maintenance
- Snow and ice removal
- Trash removal
- HVAC maintenance (common areas)

**Exclusions (Tenant Should Negotiate)**:
- Capital expenditures (or amortized over useful life)
- Landlord's general overhead
- Leasing commissions
- Legal fees (except for CAM enforcement)
- Marketing and advertising
- Tenant improvement costs
- Repairs covered by insurance
- Environmental remediation
- Landlord's income taxes
- Depreciation
- Debt service
- Ground rent
- Executive salaries above building level

**CAM Cap Provisions**:
> Notwithstanding the foregoing, controllable Operating Expenses shall not increase by more than [X]% per annum on a cumulative, compounding basis over the Base Year. "Controllable Operating Expenses" means all Operating Expenses other than real estate taxes, insurance premiums, utility costs, and snow removal costs.

### 2.2 Real Estate Taxes

**What's Included**:
- Ad valorem property taxes
- Special assessments (often excluded or amortized)
- BID assessments
- Tax consultant fees

**Tax Protest Rights**:
- Landlord's obligation to protest
- Tenant's right to participate
- Allocation of savings

**Base Year vs. Net Lease**:
- **Base Year**: Tenant pays increases over base year taxes
- **Net Lease**: Tenant pays pro rata share of all taxes

### 2.3 Insurance

**Landlord's Insurance Requirements**:
- Property insurance (replacement cost)
- Liability insurance
- Rental income insurance
- Umbrella coverage

**Tenant's Insurance Requirements**:
- Commercial general liability ($1-2M per occurrence)
- Property insurance (tenant's property)
- Business interruption
- Workers' compensation
- Additional insured requirements
- Waiver of subrogation

**Sample Tenant Insurance Requirements**:
> Tenant shall maintain throughout the Term: (a) Commercial General Liability insurance with limits of not less than $1,000,000 per occurrence and $2,000,000 aggregate; (b) Property insurance covering Tenant's personal property, fixtures, and improvements for full replacement cost; (c) Workers' Compensation insurance as required by law; and (d) Business Interruption insurance covering a minimum of twelve months of rent.

### 2.4 Expense Calculations

**Pro Rata Share Calculation**:
```
Tenant's Pro Rata Share = Tenant's RSF / Building's Total RSF

Example:
Tenant's Premises:     10,000 RSF
Building Total:       100,000 RSF
Pro Rata Share:           10%
```

**Gross-Up Provision**:
> If the Building is less than [95]% occupied during any calendar year, Operating Expenses shall be adjusted to reflect what Operating Expenses would have been if the Building were [95]% occupied.

## 3. Renewal and Extension Options

### 3.1 Renewal Option Terms

**Key Provisions**:
- Number of renewal terms
- Length of each renewal term
- Notice period (typically 6-12 months)
- Rent during renewal term
- Conditions to exercise
- Personal vs. assignable option

**Sample Renewal Option**:
> Provided that (a) no Event of Default exists at the time of exercise or at the commencement of the Renewal Term, and (b) Tenant has not assigned this Lease or sublet more than [25]% of the Premises, Tenant shall have the option to extend the Term for one (1) additional period of five (5) years upon the same terms and conditions, except that Base Rent shall be adjusted to Fair Market Rent.

### 3.2 Fair Market Rent Determination

**Process**:
1. Landlord provides initial determination
2. Tenant accepts or objects
3. Negotiation period
4. Appraisal/arbitration if no agreement

**Arbitration Process**:
> If the parties cannot agree on Fair Market Rent within thirty (30) days, each party shall appoint a licensed MAI appraiser within fifteen (15) days. The two appraisers shall select a third appraiser. Each appraiser shall independently determine Fair Market Rent, and the average of the two closest determinations shall be the Fair Market Rent.

### 3.3 Comparison Factors

**Fair Market Rent Should Consider**:
- Comparable transactions in the building
- Comparable transactions in the market
- Tenant improvement allowances
- Free rent periods
- Lease term length
- Credit quality
- Space size and configuration

## 4. Tenant Improvements and Alterations

### 4.1 Initial Tenant Improvements

**Tenant Improvement Allowance**:
- Dollar amount per RSF (or total)
- Covered costs (hard costs, soft costs, FF&E)
- Disbursement conditions
- Deadline for use
- Unused allowance treatment

**Sample TI Allowance**:
> Landlord shall provide Tenant a Tenant Improvement Allowance of $[50.00] per rentable square foot ($[Amount] total) toward the cost of designing and constructing the Initial Tenant Improvements in accordance with the Work Letter attached as Exhibit B.

**Work Letter Components**:
- Base building condition
- Tenant improvement scope
- Approval process for plans
- Construction management
- Change order procedures
- Completion timeline
- Punch list procedures
- Warranty provisions

### 4.2 Alterations During Term

**Landlord Consent Requirements**:
- Cosmetic changes (often permitted without consent)
- Structural changes (always require consent)
- MEP changes (usually require consent)
- Signage (per signage criteria)

**Conditions to Consent**:
- Plans and specifications review
- Licensed and insured contractors
- Permits obtained
- Compliance with building standards
- Lien waivers
- Restoration requirements

**Sample Alterations Provision**:
> Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises without Landlord's prior written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed for non-structural alterations costing less than [$50,000]. Landlord may require removal of any alterations and restoration of the Premises upon termination.

## 5. Assignment and Subletting

### 5.1 Consent Requirements

**Standard Provisions**:
- Prior written consent required
- Consent not unreasonably withheld
- Factors for reasonable withholding
- Permitted transfers (affiliates, successors)

**Reasonable Factors for Withholding**:
- Financial condition of assignee/subtenant
- Business reputation
- Proposed use
- Compatibility with other tenants
- Exclusivity violations

**Sample Language**:
> Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet all or any portion of the Premises without Landlord's prior written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Landlord may consider the following in determining whether to grant consent: (a) the proposed transferee's financial condition; (b) the proposed transferee's business reputation; (c) the nature of the proposed transferee's business; and (d) the terms of the proposed transfer.

### 5.2 Recapture Rights

**Landlord's Recapture Option**:
> Upon receipt of Tenant's request for consent to an assignment or sublease, Landlord shall have the option, exercisable within thirty (30) days, to terminate this Lease (in the case of an assignment or sublease of substantially all of the Premises) or to remove the affected space from the Premises (in the case of a partial sublease).

### 5.3 Profit Sharing

**Excess Rent Provisions**:
> If Tenant receives rent or other consideration from any assignee or subtenant in excess of the rent payable by Tenant hereunder (after deducting Tenant's reasonable costs of the transfer, including brokerage commissions, legal fees, and tenant improvement costs), [50]% of such excess shall be paid to Landlord.

### 5.4 Permitted Transfers

**Carve-Outs from Consent Requirements**:
- Transfers to affiliates (>50% common ownership)
- Transfers to successors by merger
- Transfers to purchasers of substantially all assets
- Internal reorganizations

## 6. Use and Exclusivity

### 6.1 Permitted Use

**Office Use Example**:
> The Premises shall be used and occupied only for general office purposes, including administrative, executive, professional, and clerical functions, and for no other purpose without Landlord's prior written consent.

**Retail Use Example**:
> The Premises shall be used and occupied only for the operation of a [specific retail business] and for no other purpose. Tenant shall operate continuously during the Term, maintaining business hours of at least [X:00 a.m.] to [X:00 p.m.] Monday through Saturday.

### 6.2 Exclusive Use (Retail)

**Grant of Exclusive**:
> Landlord agrees that during the Term, no other portion of the Shopping Center shall be leased to or occupied by any tenant whose primary business is [specific use], provided that Tenant is open for business and operating in the normal course.

**Exclusive Violations**:
- Rent reduction
- Termination right
- Injunctive relief
- Carve-outs for existing tenants

### 6.3 Prohibited Uses

**Common Prohibitions**:
- Uses that increase insurance rates
- Uses requiring special permits
- Nuisance activities
- Adult entertainment
- Firearms sales
- Hazardous materials storage
- Uses violating other tenant exclusives

## 7. Maintenance and Repairs

### 7.1 Landlord's Obligations

**Typically Responsible For**:
- Structural elements (roof, foundation, exterior walls)
- Building systems (HVAC, electrical, plumbing - base building)
- Common areas
- Elevators and escalators
- Life safety systems
- Parking facilities
- Landscaping

### 7.2 Tenant's Obligations

**Typically Responsible For**:
- Interior non-structural repairs
- Premises-specific HVAC maintenance
- Interior painting and decoration
- Floor coverings
- Fixtures and equipment
- Damage caused by tenant
- Alterations and additions

**Sample Tenant Maintenance**:
> Tenant shall, at its sole cost and expense, maintain the Premises in good order, condition, and repair, including all HVAC equipment serving only the Premises, all plumbing and electrical systems within the Premises, all interior walls, ceilings, and floor coverings, and all doors and windows.

### 7.3 Repair Standards

**Landlord Standard**:
> Landlord shall maintain the Building and common areas in a manner consistent with comparable first-class office buildings in [City], and shall make repairs within a reasonable time after receiving notice from Tenant.

## 8. Damage and Destruction

### 8.1 Landlord's Restoration Obligation

**Typical Provision**:
> If the Premises or Building are damaged by fire or other casualty, Landlord shall repair the damage with reasonable diligence, unless Landlord elects to terminate as provided below. Landlord's restoration obligation shall be limited to the base building condition.

### 8.2 Termination Rights

**Landlord's Termination Right**:
- Damage occurs in final [12-24] months of term
- Insurance proceeds insufficient
- Restoration would take longer than [12] months
- Building is substantially destroyed

**Tenant's Termination Right**:
- Premises substantially damaged
- Restoration not substantially complete within [12] months
- Access materially impaired

### 8.3 Rent Abatement

> If the Premises are rendered wholly or partially untenantable by fire or other casualty, Base Rent and Additional Rent shall abate in proportion to the portion of the Premises rendered untenantable, from the date of the casualty until the earlier of (a) the date Landlord substantially completes restoration, or (b) the date Tenant reoccupies the affected portion.

## 9. Default and Remedies

### 9.1 Tenant Events of Default

**Common Default Triggers**:
- Failure to pay rent (typically 5-10 day grace period)
- Failure to perform other obligations (30 day cure period)
- Bankruptcy filing
- Assignment for benefit of creditors
- Abandonment
- Repeated defaults (habitual default clause)

### 9.2 Landlord Remedies

**Available Remedies**:
- Terminate the lease
- Re-enter and relet
- Accelerate rent (where permitted)
- Sue for damages
- Self-help (limited circumstances)

**Damage Calculation**:
> Upon termination following an Event of Default, Landlord shall be entitled to recover: (a) unpaid rent accrued through termination; (b) the present value of the excess of the rent reserved for the balance of the Term over the fair market rental value of the Premises; (c) unamortized tenant improvement costs and leasing commissions; and (d) all costs and expenses of re-letting.

### 9.3 Tenant Remedies

**Self-Help After Landlord Default**:
> If Landlord fails to perform any obligation and such failure continues for thirty (30) days after written notice (or such longer period as reasonably required if the failure cannot reasonably be cured within thirty days), Tenant may, but shall not be obligated to, cure such failure and recover the reasonable cost thereof from Landlord, or offset such cost against rent due.

---

## 10. Surrender and Holdover

### 10.1 Surrender Obligations

**Tenant's Obligations**:
- Remove all personal property
- Remove alterations (if required)
- Repair damage from removal
- Leave premises broom clean
- Return all keys
- Deliver estoppel/SNDA acknowledgments

### 10.2 Holdover

**Holdover Rent**:
> If Tenant remains in possession after the expiration or termination of this Lease without Landlord's consent, Tenant shall pay holdover rent equal to [150]% of the Base Rent and Additional Rent payable during the last month of the Term, and shall be liable for all damages sustained by Landlord, including lost opportunities to re-let.

**Permitted Holdover Period**:
> Notwithstanding the foregoing, if Tenant provides written notice at least [90] days prior to the scheduled expiration that it requires additional time to vacate, Landlord shall permit Tenant to remain in possession for up to [60] additional days at [125]% of the then-current rent.
