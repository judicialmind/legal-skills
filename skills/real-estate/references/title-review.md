# Title Examination Procedures

## Overview

This reference document covers the procedures for examining title to real property, identifying common title defects, and the instruments used to cure title issues. A thorough title examination is essential for any real estate transaction to ensure the buyer or lender receives the quality of title expected.

---

## 1. Title Examination Process

### 1.1 Ordering the Title Search

**Title Commitment Request Information**:
- Legal description of property
- Property address
- Current owner name
- Transaction type (purchase, refinance, construction)
- Anticipated closing date
- Requested policy amount
- Lender information (if applicable)
- Buyer/borrower entity information

**Search Period Standards**:
- Standard search: 40-60 years (varies by jurisdiction)
- Full search: From patent or sovereignty
- Limited search: Specific time period (updates)
- Current owner search: From last recorded deed

### 1.2 Chain of Title Analysis

**Elements to Verify**:
- Unbroken chain from sovereign or root of title
- Proper conveyances at each transfer
- Correct parties (grantor was prior grantee)
- Adequate consideration or proper conveyancing language
- Proper execution (signatures, acknowledgments)
- Timely recording

**Chain of Title Problems**:
- Gap in chain (missing deed)
- Wild deed (recorded but not in chain)
- Deed from wrong party
- Inadequate property description
- Missing acknowledgment
- Defective corporate authority

### 1.3 Document Review

**Each Recorded Document Review Should Include**:
- Document type identification
- Recording information (book/page, instrument number)
- Date of execution and recording
- Grantor/grantee identification
- Property description comparison
- Terms and conditions
- Signatures and acknowledgments
- Impact on current title

## 2. Title Commitment Components

### 2.1 Schedule A

**Contains**:
- Effective date of commitment
- Policy amount
- Type of policy (owner's, loan)
- Name of proposed insured
- Estate or interest to be insured
- Legal description

**Sample Schedule A**:
```
SCHEDULE A

Effective Date: [Date] at [Time]
Policy Amount: $[Amount]

1. Name of Proposed Insured:
   [Buyer Name]

2. The estate or interest in the Land described herein and which
   is to be covered by this policy is:
   Fee Simple

3. Title to the estate or interest in the Land is vested in:
   [Current Owner Name]

4. The Land referred to in this Commitment is described as follows:
   [Legal Description]
```

### 2.2 Schedule B-I (Requirements)

**Common Requirements**:
- Payment of premium
- Execution and recording of deed
- Execution and recording of mortgage (if applicable)
- Release of existing mortgage
- Evidence of entity authority
- Evidence of payment of taxes
- Affidavits from seller
- FIRPTA certificate
- Gap indemnity

**Sample Requirements**:
```
SCHEDULE B - PART I
(Requirements)

The following requirements must be met:

1. Instruments necessary to create the estate or interest to be
   insured must be properly executed, delivered and recorded.

2. Pay the full consideration to, or for the account of, the
   grantors or mortgagors.

3. Release or satisfaction of Mortgage recorded in Book [X],
   Page [X], securing an original principal amount of $[Amount]
   in favor of [Lender Name].

4. Evidence satisfactory to the Company that real estate taxes
   for the current year have been paid.

5. Affidavit by the seller as to mechanics' liens and parties
   in possession.
```

### 2.3 Schedule B-II (Exceptions)

**Standard/Printed Exceptions**:
1. Rights of parties in possession
2. Survey matters
3. Mechanic's liens
4. Taxes not yet due and payable
5. Easements and restrictions of record

**Special Exceptions (Property-Specific)**:
- Recorded easements
- Recorded restrictions/covenants
- Recorded liens
- Recorded leases
- Other encumbrances

**Sample Special Exceptions**:
```
SCHEDULE B - PART II
(Exceptions from Coverage)

This policy does not insure against loss or damage by reason of
the following:

1. Real estate taxes for the year [Year] and subsequent years,
   which are not yet due and payable.

2. Easement for electric transmission lines granted to [Utility]
   recorded in Book [X], Page [X].

3. Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions recorded
   in Book [X], Page [X], and any amendments thereto.

4. Rights of tenants in possession, as tenants only.

5. Any facts, rights, interests, or claims which would be disclosed
   by an accurate survey and inspection of the premises.
```

## 3. Common Title Defects

### 3.1 Ownership Defects

**Missing or Defective Deeds**
- **Problem**: Gap in chain of title; deed never recorded; deed improperly executed
- **Cure**: Locate and record deed; obtain corrective deed; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

**Forgery**
- **Problem**: Signature on deed was forged
- **Cure**: Quiet title action; title insurance claim
- **Risk Level**: CRITICAL

**Fraud/Undue Influence**
- **Problem**: Deed obtained through fraud or undue influence on grantor
- **Cure**: Quiet title action; title insurance claim
- **Risk Level**: CRITICAL

**Incapacity**
- **Problem**: Grantor lacked mental capacity to convey
- **Cure**: Conservator/guardian deed; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

**Minority**
- **Problem**: Grantor was a minor when deed executed
- **Cure**: Ratification after reaching majority; guardian deed; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

**Marital Status Issues**
- **Problem**: Spouse's interest not properly addressed; community property issues
- **Cure**: Quitclaim from spouse; affidavit of marital status; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

**Undisclosed Heirs**
- **Problem**: Property passed by intestacy; unknown heirs may have claims
- **Cure**: Heir search; affidavit of heirship; quiet title action; title insurance
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

**Invalid Probate**
- **Problem**: Defective probate proceedings; property passed outside probate
- **Cure**: Ancillary probate; administrator's deed; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

### 3.2 Lien Defects

**Unreleased Mortgages**
- **Problem**: Paid-off mortgage not released of record
- **Cure**: Obtain release from lender; affidavit of payment; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: LOW to MODERATE

**Judgment Liens**
- **Problem**: Unsatisfied money judgment creating lien on property
- **Cure**: Satisfy judgment and record release; dispute lien validity
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE to HIGH

**Tax Liens**
- **Problem**: Unpaid property taxes; IRS or state tax liens
- **Cure**: Pay taxes; negotiate payoff; request release
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

**Mechanic's Liens**
- **Problem**: Unpaid contractors or suppliers with lien rights
- **Cure**: Pay claim; bond off lien; dispute validity
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE to HIGH

**HOA/Assessment Liens**
- **Problem**: Unpaid homeowners' association assessments
- **Cure**: Pay assessments; obtain release
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

**Child Support/Alimony Liens**
- **Problem**: Unpaid support obligations creating lien
- **Cure**: Pay arrearages; obtain release
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

### 3.3 Encumbrance Defects

**Easement Issues**
- **Problem**: Unknown easements burdening property; easement location disputes
- **Cure**: Survey; easement agreement; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: VARIES

**Restrictive Covenant Violations**
- **Problem**: Property improvements violate recorded restrictions
- **Cure**: Obtain variance; amend restrictions; remove violation
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE to HIGH

**Encroachments**
- **Problem**: Improvements extend onto neighboring property or easements
- **Cure**: Encroachment agreement; remove improvement; purchase additional land
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE to HIGH

**Boundary Disputes**
- **Problem**: Disagreement over property boundaries
- **Cure**: Boundary line agreement; survey; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

### 3.4 Description Defects

**Inadequate Legal Description**
- **Problem**: Description doesn't close or is ambiguous
- **Cure**: Corrective deed with proper description; surveyor's affidavit
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

**Conflicting Descriptions**
- **Problem**: Different documents describe different parcels
- **Cure**: Survey; corrective deeds; quiet title action
- **Risk Level**: HIGH

**Gap or Gore**
- **Problem**: Unmapped strip between parcels
- **Cure**: Correct descriptions; quiet title action; adverse possession
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

### 3.5 Entity Defects

**Corporate Authority Issues**
- **Problem**: Corporation not authorized to own property; improper authorization
- **Cure**: Corporate resolution; officer's certificate; ratification
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

**Partnership Issues**
- **Problem**: Improper conveyance by/to partnership; missing partner consent
- **Cure**: Partner consents; ratification; dissolution documentation
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

**Trust Issues**
- **Problem**: Trustee exceeded authority; trust not valid
- **Cure**: Trust certification; trustee's deed; court authorization
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE to HIGH

**LLC Issues**
- **Problem**: Manager/member authority unclear; improper conveyance
- **Cure**: Operating agreement; member consent; manager certification
- **Risk Level**: MODERATE

## 4. Curative Instruments

### 4.1 Corrective Deed

**Purpose**: Corrects errors in previously recorded deed
**Use When**: Legal description error; name misspelling; missing recitals

**Key Elements**:
- Reference to original deed (recording information)
- Identification of error
- Corrected language
- All parties from original deed must execute
- Recital that no new consideration exchanged

**Sample Language**:
> CORRECTIVE DEED: This Corrective Deed is executed for the purpose of correcting an error in that certain Warranty Deed recorded in Book [X], Page [X] of the [County] Records. The legal description in said deed incorrectly stated [error]. The correct legal description is [correct description]. This Corrective Deed is not intended to convey title but only to correct the referenced error.

### 4.2 Quitclaim Deed

**Purpose**: Releases whatever interest grantor may have without warranties
**Use When**: Cloud on title; releasing potential interests; clearing up ownership

**Key Elements**:
- Clear identification of grantor
- Statement that grantor "remises, releases, and quitclaims"
- No warranties of title
- Proper execution and acknowledgment

### 4.3 Affidavit of Heirship

**Purpose**: Establishes ownership through intestate succession
**Use When**: Property owner died intestate; no probate conducted

**Key Elements**:
- Statement of decedent's death
- Family history and identification of heirs
- Statement of property ownership at death
- Affiant's relationship to decedent
- Sworn before notary

### 4.4 Affidavit of Marital Status

**Purpose**: Clarifies marital status at time of conveyance
**Use When**: Deed signed by one spouse; community property issues

**Key Elements**:
- Statement of marital status at relevant time
- If married, spouse's consent or explanation
- If divorced, reference to decree
- If widowed, reference to death certificate

### 4.5 Release of Lien/Mortgage

**Purpose**: Extinguishes lien of record
**Use When**: Debt satisfied; lien released

**Key Elements**:
- Identification of original instrument
- Statement of satisfaction
- Release of lien
- Execution by lienholder

### 4.6 Subordination Agreement

**Purpose**: Changes priority of liens
**Use When**: New financing requires priority; restructuring

**Key Elements**:
- Identification of both liens
- Statement of subordination
- Consent of subordinating party
- Often requires lender consent

### 4.7 Boundary Line Agreement

**Purpose**: Resolves boundary disputes between neighbors
**Use When**: Boundary line unclear; encroachment exists

**Key Elements**:
- Agreement on boundary location
- Mutual quitclaims
- Survey reference
- Recorded against both parcels

### 4.8 Quiet Title Action

**Purpose**: Judicial determination of ownership
**Use When**: Other curative methods unavailable; unknown claimants

**Process**:
1. File complaint identifying property and claims
2. Name all known adverse claimants
3. Publication notice for unknown claimants
4. Prove title through evidence
5. Obtain judgment quieting title
6. Record judgment

## 5. Title Examination Checklist

### 5.1 Initial Review

- [ ] Verify legal description matches in all documents
- [ ] Confirm property address corresponds to legal description
- [ ] Check current vesting matches seller/borrower
- [ ] Review effective date of commitment
- [ ] Verify policy amount appropriate for transaction

### 5.2 Schedule B-I Analysis

- [ ] List all requirements
- [ ] Identify responsible party for each
- [ ] Determine documents needed to satisfy
- [ ] Calendar deadlines
- [ ] Confirm ability to satisfy each requirement

### 5.3 Schedule B-II Analysis

For each exception:
- [ ] Obtain copy of underlying document
- [ ] Review impact on intended use
- [ ] Determine if exception acceptable
- [ ] Identify if deletion/modification possible
- [ ] Document objections if applicable

### 5.4 Standard Exception Review

**Rights of Parties in Possession**
- [ ] Order survey or inspection
- [ ] Obtain tenant estoppels
- [ ] Require vacancy affidavit (if applicable)
- [ ] Request deletion with affidavit

**Survey Matters**
- [ ] Order current ALTA survey
- [ ] Review survey for issues
- [ ] Compare survey to title exceptions
- [ ] Request survey endorsement

**Mechanic's Liens**
- [ ] Review for recent construction
- [ ] Obtain lien waivers if applicable
- [ ] Request deletion with affidavit
- [ ] Consider mechanic's lien endorsement

**Taxes**
- [ ] Verify current year status
- [ ] Check for delinquent taxes
- [ ] Review special assessments
- [ ] Calculate proration

### 5.5 Chain of Title Review

- [ ] Verify unbroken chain
- [ ] Check each deed execution
- [ ] Confirm proper acknowledgments
- [ ] Review conveyancing language
- [ ] Identify any wild deeds
- [ ] Check for gaps

### 5.6 Lien Search

- [ ] Judgment search against current owner
- [ ] Judgment search against prior owners (within search period)
- [ ] Tax lien search
- [ ] UCC search (if personal property)
- [ ] Bankruptcy search
- [ ] Federal tax lien search

### 5.7 Final Verification

- [ ] All requirements satisfied
- [ ] All exceptions reviewed and accepted
- [ ] Gap coverage arranged
- [ ] Endorsements ordered
- [ ] Wire instructions verified
- [ ] Recording instructions prepared

## 7. Special Title Issues

### 7.1 FIRPTA Compliance

**Requirements**:
- Transferor certification of non-foreign status, OR
- Withholding of 15% (or reduced rate) of gross sales price
- Form 8288 filing

### 7.2 1031 Exchange Considerations

**Title Issues**:
- Qualified intermediary involvement
- Proper vesting of replacement property
- Direct deeding vs. intermediary deeding
- Related party concerns

### 7.3 Entity Transactions

**Corporate Sellers/Buyers**:
- Resolution authorizing transaction
- Good standing certificate
- Officer's certificate

**LLC Sellers/Buyers**:
- Operating agreement authority review
- Manager/member consent
- Statement of authority

**Trust Transactions**:
- Trust certification
- Trustee authority
- Trust beneficial ownership

### 7.4 Foreclosure Title

**Issues to Review**:
- Proper notice to all parties
- Compliance with foreclosure statutes
- Right of redemption periods
- Deficiency judgment rights
- Junior lien extinguishment
- Federal tax lien survival
