# Marital Property Division Reference Guide

## Overview

Property division in divorce involves identifying, characterizing, valuing, and distributing assets and debts accumulated during the marriage. This guide covers the legal frameworks, valuation methods, and practical considerations for property division.

---

## Property Division Systems

### Community Property States

**Nine Community Property States**
- Arizona
- California
- Idaho
- Louisiana
- Nevada
- New Mexico
- Texas
- Washington
- Wisconsin

**Basic Principles**
- Property acquired during marriage presumptively community property
- Each spouse owns undivided one-half interest
- Equal division is the starting point
- Separate property remains with owner spouse

### Equitable Distribution States

**All Other States**
- 41 states plus District of Columbia
- Court divides property "equitably" (fairly)
- Does not necessarily mean equal
- Judicial discretion based on statutory factors

**Common Equitable Distribution Factors**
- Length of marriage
- Age and health of parties
- Income and earning capacity
- Standard of living during marriage
- Contributions to marital estate
- Economic circumstances at division
- Custody of minor children
- Tax consequences
- Prenuptial or postnuptial agreements
- Dissipation of assets

## Characterization of Property

### Separate Property

**Property That Remains Separate**
- Owned before marriage
- Inherited by one spouse
- Gifted to one spouse
- Personal injury awards (pain and suffering)
- Property designated separate by agreement

**Tracing Requirements**
- Must trace separate property through marriage
- Documentation essential
- Commingling may convert to marital property

**Maintaining Separate Character**
- Keep in separate accounts
- Do not commingle with marital funds
- Document source of acquisition
- Maintain clear records

### Marital/Community Property

**Property Presumptively Marital**
- Earnings during marriage
- Property purchased with earnings
- Appreciation of separate property (in some states)
- Retirement benefits earned during marriage
- Business interests developed during marriage

### Hybrid/Mixed Property

**Commingled Property**
- Separate and marital funds mixed
- Tracing may apportion separate portion
- Burden on claiming party to trace

**Active vs. Passive Appreciation**
- Active appreciation (marital effort): Generally marital
- Passive appreciation (market forces): Varies by jurisdiction

**Example: Premarital Home**
- Equity at marriage: Separate property
- Appreciation during marriage: May be partially marital
- Mortgage payments with marital funds: Creates marital interest
- Improvements with marital funds: Marital contribution

## Valuation Methods

### Real Property

**Appraisal Methods**
- Comparable sales approach
- Income capitalization approach
- Cost approach

**Key Considerations**
- Date of valuation (separation, trial, or other date)
- Encumbrances and liens
- Deferred maintenance
- Market conditions

### Business Interests

**Business Valuation Approaches**

| Approach | Method | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Income | Capitalization of Earnings | Single year earnings divided by cap rate |
| Income | Discounted Cash Flow | Present value of projected future cash flows |
| Market | Comparable Transactions | Sales of similar businesses |
| Market | Public Company Comparables | Multiples from public companies |
| Asset | Net Asset Value | Assets minus liabilities |
| Asset | Liquidation Value | Forced sale value |

**Common Valuation Adjustments**
- Marketability discount (20-40% typical)
- Minority interest discount (15-35% typical)
- Key person discount
- Control premium

**Professional Practice Valuation**
- Goodwill: Personal vs. enterprise
- Some states exclude personal goodwill
- Earnings history analysis
- Comparable professional practices

### Retirement Accounts

**Defined Contribution Plans (401(k), IRA)**
- Account balance is value
- Determine marital portion
- Consider tax consequences

**Defined Benefit Pensions**

*Present Value Method*
- Actuarial calculation
- Reduces to current lump sum
- Requires assumptions about interest, mortality, retirement age

*Coverture Fraction/Time Rule*
- Marital portion = Months of marriage during employment / Total months of employment
- Apply fraction to benefit at retirement
- Deferred distribution approach

### Stock Options and RSUs

**Vested Options**
- Intrinsic value: Stock price minus strike price
- May have tax implications at exercise

**Unvested Options**
- Coverture fraction analysis
- When granted for past services: Marital property
- When granted for future services: May include separate component

**Restricted Stock Units**
- Value of shares at vesting
- Apply coverture fraction for marital portion
- Consider vesting schedule

### Personal Property

**Vehicles**
- Kelley Blue Book, NADA guides
- Private party sale value
- Consider mileage, condition

**Household Furnishings**
- Garage sale value (typically low)
- Replacement cost for insurance purposes
- Itemized lists with agreed values

**Collectibles and Art**
- Professional appraisal required
- Authentication issues
- Specialized market knowledge

## Common Assets Requiring Special Attention

### Marital Residence

**Options for Division**
- Sell and divide proceeds
- One spouse buys out other
- Deferred sale (until children reach age)
- Co-ownership (rare, not recommended)

**Buyout Calculation**
```
Fair Market Value
- Outstanding Mortgage
- Selling Costs (6-8%)
- Capital Gains Tax (if applicable)
= Net Equity
/ 2 (or per agreement)
= Buyout Amount
```

**Tax Considerations**
- Section 121 exclusion ($250K individual, $500K married)
- Must have owned and used as principal residence
- 2 of last 5 years requirement

### Retirement Benefits

**Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO)**
- Required to divide employer retirement plans
- Court order that meets ERISA requirements
- Creates alternate payee status
- Must be pre-approved by plan administrator

**QDRO Key Provisions**
- Participant and alternate payee identification
- Plan name and administrator
- Dollar amount or percentage
- Time period for calculation
- Payment trigger events
- Survivor benefit provisions

**IRA Division**
- QDRO not required
- Transfer incident to divorce
- Trustee-to-trustee transfer recommended
- Tax-free if properly executed

### Businesses

**Division Options**
- One spouse retains, buys out other
- Sell business, divide proceeds
- Continue as co-owners (rare)
- Divide business assets

**Operational Considerations**
- Continuity of operations
- Non-compete agreements
- Client/customer relationships
- Employee retention

### Stock Options and Deferred Compensation

**Division Methods**
- Present value offset
- Deferred distribution
- Reserve jurisdiction

**Tax Considerations**
- Ordinary income vs. capital gains
- AMT implications
- Transfer timing

## Debt Division

### Types of Marital Debt

**Joint Debt**
- Both spouses liable to creditor
- Divorce decree does not bind creditors
- Indemnification provisions important

**Individual Debt Incurred During Marriage**
- Generally marital debt if for marital purposes
- May be separate if for non-marital purposes

### Debt Allocation Considerations

- Who incurred the debt
- Purpose of the debt
- Who benefited from the debt
- Ability to pay
- Asset received in exchange

### Protecting Against Liability

**Refinancing Provisions**
- Require refinancing within specified period
- Remove non-retaining spouse from liability

**Indemnification Clauses**
- Responsible party agrees to hold other harmless
- Include attorney fee provision
- May not prevent creditor collection

**Credit Monitoring**
- Provisions for credit report monitoring
- Notice of default requirements

## Tax Considerations

### Property Transfers Incident to Divorce

**IRC Section 1041**
- No gain or loss recognized on transfers
- Transferee takes transferor's basis
- Must be incident to divorce

**"Incident to Divorce" Requirements**
- Within 1 year of divorce, OR
- Related to cessation of marriage (within 6 years)

### Basis Considerations

**Carryover Basis**
- Recipient takes transferor's adjusted basis
- Important for appreciated assets
- Consider tax-affected values

**Example: Stock Division**
```
Stock Value: $100,000
Cost Basis: $20,000
Potential Gain: $80,000
Tax Rate: 20% (federal LTCG)
Tax on Sale: $16,000
Tax-Affected Value: $84,000
```

### Retirement Account Distributions

**Early Distribution Penalty Exceptions**
- QDRO distributions (no 10% penalty)
- IRA transfers incident to divorce
- Must be direct transfer to recipient

## Special Situations

### Dissipation of Assets

**Definition**
- Intentional waste of marital assets
- During breakdown of marriage
- For non-marital purpose

**Common Examples**
- Gambling losses
- Affair expenses
- Gifts to paramour
- Excessive spending
- Failure to maintain assets

**Remedies**
- Add back dissipated amount to marital estate
- Charge against dissipating spouse's share

### Hidden Assets

**Red Flags**
- Lifestyle exceeds reported income
- Unusual financial transactions
- New debts without explanation
- Missing financial documents
- Controlled business with cash transactions

**Discovery Tools**
- Interrogatories
- Requests for production
- Subpoenas to third parties
- Forensic accountant examination
- Lifestyle analysis

### Premarital Agreements

**Enforcement Considerations**
- Voluntary execution
- Full financial disclosure
- Independent legal counsel
- Not unconscionable at execution
- Not unconscionable at enforcement

## Division Process

### Step 1: Identification

**Full Disclosure Required**
- All assets, regardless of title
- All debts, regardless of whose name
- Sworn financial declarations

**Documentation to Gather**
- Tax returns (3-5 years)
- Bank statements
- Investment account statements
- Retirement account statements
- Business financial records
- Real property deeds
- Vehicle titles
- Loan documents
- Insurance policies

### Step 2: Characterization

**For Each Asset/Debt**
- Marital or separate?
- If mixed, what portions?
- Tracing analysis if needed

### Step 3: Valuation

**Determine Value**
- Select appropriate valuation method
- Engage experts as needed
- Establish valuation date

### Step 4: Distribution

**Equitable Division Factors**
- Apply statutory factors
- Consider tax consequences
- Address liquidity needs
- Maintain retirement security
