# IRS Audit Defense Reference Guide

## Overview

This guide covers the IRS examination process, taxpayer rights, and strategies for audit defense and appeals.

---

## IRS EXAMINATION PROCESS

### Types of Examinations

| Type | Description | Complexity |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Correspondence Audit | Mail-based, specific issues | Lowest |
| Office Audit | In-person at IRS office | Moderate |
| Field Audit | At taxpayer's location | Highest |
| TCMP/NRP Audit | Statistical sampling, comprehensive | Comprehensive |

### Examination Selection

**Selection Methods**:
- **DIF Scoring**: Discriminant Information Function scoring identifies returns with high audit potential
- **Information Matching**: W-2, 1099, K-1 matching against returns
- **Related Examinations**: Connected to another taxpayer under audit
- **Referrals**: Whistleblowers, other agencies, state tax authorities
- **Random Selection**: National Research Program (NRP) statistical sampling
- **Specific Issues**: Identified compliance campaigns

### Statute of Limitations

**General Rule (IRC § 6501(a))**: 3 years from later of:
- Due date of return, or
- Date return was filed

**Exceptions**:
| Circumstance | Period |
|--------------|--------|
| Substantial omission (>25% of gross income) | 6 years |
| Fraud or failure to file | No limit |
| Listed transaction not disclosed | 6 years |
| Foreign income (>$5,000 omitted, certain forms) | 6 years |
| Consent to extend (Form 872) | As agreed |

**Tolling Events**:
- Filing of petition in Tax Court
- Bankruptcy
- Absence from United States
- Third-party contacts

## EXAMINATION PROCEDURES

### Initial Contact

**IRS Initial Contact**:
- Letter 2205 (individual) or Letter 2205-A (business)
- Identifies issues to be examined
- Requests initial documentation
- Schedules appointment or requests response

**Taxpayer Response**:
- Acknowledge receipt
- Gather requested documents
- Consider representation
- Request POA processing (Form 2848)

### Information Document Requests (IDRs)

**IDR Process**:
1. Examiner issues written IDR
2. Reasonable time for response (typically 14-30 days)
3. Extensions may be available
4. Failure to respond may result in summons

**IDR Best Practices**:
- Respond in writing
- Provide only what is requested
- Organize documents logically
- Maintain privilege where applicable
- Document all submissions

### Examiner Summons Authority (IRC § 7602)

**Summons Power**:
- Examine books and records
- Take testimony under oath
- Compel third-party records

**Limitations**:
- Must be relevant to tax determination
- Cannot be overly broad or burdensome
- Subject to privilege claims
- Powell requirements must be met

**Powell Requirements** (United States v. Powell, 379 U.S. 48 (1964)):
1. Issued for legitimate purpose
2. Information may be relevant
3. Information not already in IRS possession
4. Administrative steps followed

### Taxpayer Rights During Examination

**Taxpayer Bill of Rights** (IRC § 7803(a)(3)):
1. Right to be informed
2. Right to quality service
3. Right to pay no more than correct amount
4. Right to challenge IRS position and be heard
5. Right to appeal IRS decision
6. Right to finality
7. Right to privacy
8. Right to confidentiality
9. Right to retain representation
10. Right to a fair and just tax system

**Specific Examination Rights**:
- Right to representation (POA)
- Right to record interviews
- Right to receive explanation of examination process
- Right to request supervisor conference
- Right to know why information is requested

## PRIVILEGE IN TAX EXAMINATIONS

### Attorney-Client Privilege
- Applies to legal advice
- Does not protect underlying facts
- Does not protect tax return preparation
- May be waived by disclosure

### Tax Practitioner Privilege (IRC § 7525)
- Extends attorney-client privilege to federally authorized tax practitioners (CPAs, enrolled agents)
- Limited to non-criminal tax matters before IRS
- Does not apply to:
  - Written advice on tax shelters
  - State tax matters
  - Criminal matters
  - Proceedings in federal court

### Work Product Doctrine
- Protects materials prepared in anticipation of litigation
- Qualified protection (factual work product)
- Near-absolute protection (opinion work product)
- Must be prepared for litigation, not return preparation

### Kovel Arrangements
- Non-attorney (accountant) engaged by attorney
- Communications may be protected
- Must be established properly
- Document arrangement in writing

## EXAMINATION OUTCOMES

### Agreement and Payment

**Agreed Cases**:
- Taxpayer agrees with adjustments
- Signs Form 870 (Waiver of Restrictions on Assessment)
- Assessment becomes final
- No appeal rights (except computational errors)

**Payment Options**:
- Full payment
- Installment agreement (Form 9465)
- Offer in compromise (Form 656)
- Currently not collectible status

### Disagreement Options

**Informal Conference**:
- Request meeting with examiner's supervisor
- Present additional documentation
- Attempt to resolve issues

**30-Day Letter**:
- Preliminary notice of proposed changes
- Right to request Appeals conference
- 30 days to respond
- Protest required for > $25,000

**Protest Requirements** (for Appeals):
- Taxpayer name, address, SSN/EIN
- Statement requesting Appeals conference
- Date and symbols from letter
- Tax periods involved
- Adjustments disagreed with
- Statement of facts supporting position
- Statement of law supporting position
- Penalties of perjury statement
- Signature

### Statutory Notice of Deficiency (90-Day Letter)

**IRC § 6212**:
- Formal notice required before assessment
- Mailed to last known address
- 90 days to petition Tax Court (150 if outside US)
- Assessment prohibited during petition period

**Taxpayer Options**:
1. File Tax Court petition (within 90/150 days)
2. Pay and file refund claim
3. Do nothing (assessment becomes final)

## IRS APPEALS

### Appeals Mission
- Resolve tax controversies without litigation
- Fair and impartial review
- Settlement authority

### Appeals Process

**Access to Appeals**:
- Examination disagreement
- Collection due process
- Penalty appeals
- Offer in compromise rejection
- Innocent spouse denial

**Ex Parte Communication Restrictions**:
- Examiner cannot have substantive contact with Appeals
- Appeals officer independent from Examination
- Document any prohibited contacts

### Appeals Conference

**Preparation**:
- Review protest and supporting documents
- Identify strengths and weaknesses
- Prepare settlement positions
- Gather additional documentation
- Know hazards of litigation

**Settlement Factors (Hazards of Litigation)**:
- Probability of prevailing on each issue
- Amount at stake
- Litigation costs
- Precedential concerns
- Taxpayer circumstances

**Alternative Dispute Resolution**:
- Fast Track Settlement
- Fast Track Mediation
- Post-Appeals Mediation
- Arbitration (limited circumstances)

## TAX COURT PROCEDURES

### Jurisdiction

**Deficiency Cases**:
- Income, estate, gift taxes
- Must file within 90/150 days of statutory notice
- Prepayment forum (no payment required)
- Redetermination of deficiency

**Other Jurisdiction**:
- Partnership proceedings
- Interest abatement
- Innocent spouse
- Collection due process
- Worker classification
- Whistleblower awards

### Filing a Petition

**Requirements**:
- Timely filing (90/150 days from notice)
- Proper form (Form 1 for deficiency)
- Filing fee ($60)
- Copy of notice of deficiency
- Service on IRS Chief Counsel

**Small Tax Case ($50,000 or less)** (IRC § 7463):
- Simplified procedures
- Less formal
- No appeal
- Faster resolution

### Tax Court Litigation

**Stages**:
1. **Petition** - Files case
2. **Answer** - IRS response
3. **Discovery** - Document requests, interrogatories, depositions
4. **Stipulation** - Agreed facts and documents
5. **Trial** - Evidence presentation
6. **Briefs** - Legal arguments
7. **Opinion** - Court decision
8. **Decision** - Final judgment

**Burden of Proof**:
- Generally on taxpayer
- Shifts to IRS if:
  - Taxpayer introduces credible evidence
  - Complies with substantiation requirements
  - Cooperates with reasonable IRS requests (IRC § 7491)
- Always on IRS for fraud, new issues raised by IRS

## ALTERNATIVE FORUMS

### District Court

**Characteristics**:
- Jury trial available
- Full payment required first
- File refund claim, then sue
- 2-year statute from claim denial
- Federal Rules of Civil Procedure apply

### Court of Federal Claims

**Characteristics**:
- No jury trial
- Full payment required first
- File refund claim, then sue
- 2-year statute from claim denial
- Specialized tax judges

### Forum Selection Considerations

| Factor | Tax Court | District Court | CFC |
|--------|-----------|----------------|-----|
| Prepayment | No | Yes | Yes |
| Jury | No | Yes | No |
| Expertise | High | Varies | High |
| Precedent binding | Tax Court only | Circuit | Federal Circuit |
| Discovery | Limited | Full | Full |
| Appeal | Geographic circuit | Geographic circuit | Federal Circuit |

## PENALTY DEFENSE

### Common Penalties

| Penalty | IRC Section | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Failure to file | § 6651(a)(1) | 5%/month, max 25% |
| Failure to pay | § 6651(a)(2) | 0.5%/month, max 25% |
| Accuracy-related | § 6662 | 20% of underpayment |
| Fraud | § 6663 | 75% of underpayment |
| Frivolous return | § 6702 | $5,000 |

### Reasonable Cause Defense

**IRC § 6664(c)** - No penalty if:
- Reasonable cause for underpayment, AND
- Taxpayer acted in good faith

**Reasonable Cause Factors**:
- Reliance on professional advice
- Complexity of law
- Taxpayer's education and experience
- Circumstances beyond taxpayer's control
- Compliance history

### Substantial Authority Defense

**IRC § 6662(d)(2)(B)** - No substantial understatement penalty if:
- Substantial authority for position, OR
- Reasonable basis and adequate disclosure

**Substantial Authority Sources** (Treas. Reg. § 1.6662-4(d)(3)(iii)):
- Internal Revenue Code
- Treasury Regulations
- Court cases
- Revenue Rulings and Procedures
- Tax treaties
- Congressional intent (committee reports)
- IRS notices and announcements
- Private letter rulings (to taxpayer only)

### First-Time Penalty Abatement

**Administrative Waiver**:
- Clean compliance history (3 years)
- No penalties for same tax type in prior 3 years
- All returns filed
- All taxes paid or arranged

## COLLECTION CONSIDERATIONS

### Collection Due Process (CDP)

**IRC § 6320, 6330**:
- Right to hearing before levy or after lien
- 30 days to request hearing
- Review by Appeals
- Tax Court review of Appeals determination

**CDP Hearing Issues**:
- Appropriateness of collection action
- Collection alternatives
- Spousal defenses
- Underlying liability (if no prior opportunity)

### Collection Alternatives

**Installment Agreement**:
- Monthly payments
- Streamlined (< $50,000) vs. non-streamlined
- User fees apply
- Must stay current on future obligations

**Offer in Compromise**:
- Doubt as to collectibility
- Doubt as to liability
- Effective tax administration
- Application fee and initial payment required
