# Deposition Summary Templates

## Overview

Deposition summaries distill lengthy transcripts into usable work product for case analysis, trial preparation, and motion practice. This guide covers three primary summary formats.

## Page-Line Summary Format

### Purpose
- Detailed reference to specific testimony
- Useful for motion practice and trial preparation
- Enables quick location of exact quotes

## Narrative Summary Format

### Purpose
- Readable overview of testimony
- Useful for client updates and new team members
- Quick reference for essential testimony

### Template

```
DEPOSITION SUMMARY
Narrative Format

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DEPOSITION OF: [Witness Name]
DATE: [Date]
PREPARED BY: [Name]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

[2-3 paragraph overview of the most important testimony, written
in narrative form. Focus on what matters for the case.]

John Smith, the defendant's Regional Sales Manager, was deposed on
March 15, 2024. Smith was present at the January 15 meeting where
the parties allegedly reached an agreement. His testimony generally
supports plaintiff's position that an oral agreement was reached,
though he made several admissions that may be helpful to the defense.

The most significant testimony was Smith's confirmation that
delivery by March 15 was discussed as essential, and that
defendant's representative verbally agreed to this deadline.
However, Smith admitted that no written confirmation was sent
and that his contemporaneous email referred only to "mid-March."

Overall Assessment: Moderately helpful witness with some
impeachment exposure.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DETAILED NARRATIVE

BACKGROUND

John Smith has worked for ABC Corporation since 2018. He holds the
position of Regional Sales Manager for the Western region and
reports to Mary Johnson, VP of Sales. Smith was not involved in
drafting the contract at issue but attended several negotiation
meetings as the account representative. (pp. 16-25)

THE JANUARY 15 MEETING

Smith testified at length about the January 15, 2024 meeting, which
he described as the meeting where "we finalized the deal." According
to Smith, the meeting lasted approximately two hours and included
[attendees]. (pp. 26-35)

Smith's recollection of the key terms discussed:

Price: Smith confirmed that $500,000 was discussed as the contract
price. He testified that "everyone agreed on the number" and there
was "no real negotiation on price at that point." (p. 35:4-15)

Delivery Date: This is where Smith's testimony is most helpful. He
stated clearly that delivery by March 15 was discussed as essential:
"I specifically told them that delivery had to be by March 15 or we
would lose the customer. That was the whole point of the meeting."
(p. 28:15-29:8)

However, on cross-examination, Smith admitted:
- He did not put the March 15 deadline in writing at the meeting
  (p. 33:4-12)
- His email the following week referenced only "mid-March" (p. 48:8-15)
- He could not recall the exact words defendant's representative used
  (p. 41:18-42:5)

SUBSEQUENT COMMUNICATIONS

[Continue narrative...]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KEY QUOTES

1. "I specifically told them that delivery had to be by March 15
   or we would lose the customer." (p. 28:15-18)

2. "We shook hands on it. Everyone understood we had a deal."
   (p. 32:8-10)

3. "I don't recall the exact words, but he agreed to the timeline."
   (p. 42:2-4)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ASSESSMENT

Strengths:
- Clear testimony on March 15 deadline
- Present at key meetings
- Generally credible demeanor

Weaknesses:
- Contemporaneous email inconsistent
- Cannot recall exact words of agreement
- Admitted lack of written confirmation

Recommended Use:
- Live witness at trial on contract formation
- Support for summary judgment on existence of agreement
- May need to address impeachment on redirect

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Summary Best Practices

### General Guidelines

1. **Read transcript completely** before summarizing
2. **Identify key issues** in the case first
3. **Flag important testimony** on first read
4. **Include page-line references** for verification
5. **Note impeachment material** clearly
6. **Cross-reference** other depositions and documents
7. **Maintain objectivity** - include adverse testimony
8. **Use consistent formatting** across all summaries

### Choosing the Right Format

| Use Case | Recommended Format |
|----------|-------------------|
| Motion practice | Page-Line |
| Trial preparation | Page-Line + Topic |
| Case strategy | Topic/Issue |
| Client update | Narrative |
| New team member | Narrative |
| Expert preparation | Topic/Issue |
| Mediation | Narrative |
| Cross-examination prep | Page-Line |
