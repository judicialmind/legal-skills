# Federal Court Document Formatting Rules

## Overview

Federal court documents must comply with formatting requirements established by the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure, local court rules, and individual judge's preferences. Non-compliance may result in rejection, striking of documents, or sanctions.

## Federal Rules of Civil Procedure Requirements

### Rule 10: Form of Pleadings

**Caption (Rule 10(a)):**
Every pleading must have a caption with:
- Court name
- Title of action (all parties in complaint; first party each side thereafter)
- Case number (once assigned)
- Designation of pleading type

**Paragraphs and Separate Statements (Rule 10(b)):**
- Numbered paragraphs, each limited to a single set of circumstances
- Separate claims or defenses in separate counts

**Adoption by Reference (Rule 10(c)):**
- Statements in a pleading may be adopted by reference elsewhere
- Exhibits attached are part of the pleading

### Rule 5.2: Privacy Protection

Required redactions in filed documents:
- Social Security numbers: Last 4 digits only (xxx-xx-1234)
- Taxpayer ID numbers: Last 4 digits only
- Dates of birth: Year only (Born: 1985)
- Financial account numbers: Last 4 digits only
- Names of minor children: Initials only (J.D.)

**Exceptions:** Immigration cases, criminal records checks, certain tax filings

### Rule 11: Signature Requirements

**Paper Filings:**
- Signed by attorney (or party if unrepresented)
- Include address, email, telephone number

**Electronic Filings:**
- "/s/ Attorney Name" constitutes signature
- Login credentials authenticate filer
- CM/ECF registration satisfies signature requirement

## Standard Page Format Specifications

### Paper Size and Margins

**Paper Size:** 8.5 x 11 inches (letter size)

**Margins:**
- Top: 1 inch minimum
- Bottom: 1 inch minimum (half inch for page numbers)
- Left: 1 inch minimum
- Right: 1 inch minimum

**Binding Margin:** Some courts require additional left margin (1.5 inches) for binding

### Font Requirements

**Approved Typefaces:**
| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Serif | Times New Roman, Century, Georgia, Garamond |
| Sans Serif | Arial, Helvetica (less preferred for body text) |
| Monospace | Courier (less preferred, wastes space) |

**Font Size:**
- Body text: 12-point minimum (14-point for some courts)
- Footnotes: 10-point minimum (12-point in some districts)
- Block quotes: May be 11-point

**Prohibited:**
- Compressed or condensed fonts
- Light or italic fonts for body text
- Unusual or decorative fonts

### Line Spacing

**General Rule:**
- Double-spaced for body text
- Single-spaced permitted for:
  - Block quotations
  - Footnotes
  - Captions
  - Signature blocks
  - Certificates of service

### Line Numbering

**State Courts (California format):**
- Sequential line numbers on left margin
- 1-28 lines per page

**Federal Courts:**
- Generally NOT required
- Some districts permit or prefer
- Check local rules

### Title Page Requirements

For briefs and memoranda exceeding certain lengths:
- Case caption
- Document title
- Party filing
- Attorney information
- Date of filing

### Table of Contents

**Required When:**
- Brief exceeds page/word limit thresholds (varies by court)
- Appellate briefs (always required)
- Local rule specifies

**Format:**
- List major headings with page numbers
- Include subheadings
- Update page numbers before filing

### Table of Authorities

**Required When:**
- Legal memoranda and briefs (most courts)
- Appellate briefs (always required)

**Organization:**
- Cases (alphabetical)
- Constitutional provisions
- Statutes (by title/code number)
- Rules
- Regulations
- Other authorities (treatises, law review articles)

**Format:**
```
TABLE OF AUTHORITIES

Statutes

28 U.S.C. Section 1331.................................. 3
28 U.S.C. Section 1332.................................. 3, 4

Rules

Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6)................................ 5

* Authorities upon which we chiefly rely are marked with asterisks.
```

## Page Limits and Word Counts

### District Court (General - Check Local Rules)

| Document Type | Typical Limit |
|---------------|---------------|
| Motion | 25 pages |
| Response/Opposition | 25 pages |
| Reply | 10-15 pages |
| Summary Judgment Motion | 25-35 pages |
| Summary Judgment Opposition | 25-35 pages |

### Courts of Appeals (FRAP 32)

| Document Type | Word Limit | Page Limit (Alternative) |
|---------------|------------|--------------------------|
| Principal Brief | 13,000 words | 30 pages |
| Reply Brief | 6,500 words | 15 pages |
| Cross-Appeal Brief | 16,500 words | 35 pages |

### Word Count Certification

**Required Statement:**
"This brief complies with the type-volume limitation of [Rule X] because this brief contains [X,XXX] words, excluding the parts of the brief exempted by [Rule Y]."

**Excluded from Count:**
- Cover page
- Corporate disclosure statement
- Table of contents
- Table of authorities
- Certificate of service
- Signature block

## Citations

### Bluebook Format (Standard)

**Cases:**
- *Brown v. Board of Education*, 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954)
- *Smith v. Jones*, 123 F.3d 456, 460-61 (7th Cir. 2020)

**Statutes:**
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1983 (2018)
- Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6)

**Subsequent History:**
- *Doe v. Roe*, 100 F.3d 200 (5th Cir. 2019), *cert. denied*, 140 S. Ct. 1000 (2020)

### Pinpoint Citations

- Always cite to specific page numbers for quoted or referenced material
- Use "at" for subsequent citations: *Id.* at 465
- Include paragraph numbers for electronic sources: *Jones*, 2024 WL 123456, at *3

### String Citations

- Separate with semicolons
- Order by court hierarchy, then chronologically (most recent first)
- Use explanatory parentheticals

## Quotations

### Short Quotations (Under 50 Words)
- Integrate into text with quotation marks
- "The court held that plaintiff must prove each element by a preponderance of the evidence." *Smith*, 123 F.3d at 460.

### Block Quotations (50+ Words)
- Indent left margin (additional 0.5 inch)
- Single-spaced
- No quotation marks
- Citation follows on new line

### Alterations
- Brackets for changes: [D]efendant
- Ellipses for omissions: "The court . . . granted summary judgment."
- [sic] for errors in original
- Emphasis: (emphasis added) or (emphasis in original)

## Exhibits and Attachments

### Labeling
- Exhibit A, Exhibit B (letters) or Exhibit 1, Exhibit 2 (numbers)
- Declaration exhibits: Ex. 1 to Smith Decl.
- Deposition exhibits: Jones Dep. Ex. 3

### Tab/Separator Requirements
- Labeled tabs for each exhibit
- Tabs visible when document closed
- Sequential numbering throughout

### Bates Numbering
- Required for produced documents in discovery
- Format: ABC000001 (prefix + sequential number)
- Referenced in citations

## Electronic Filing Considerations

### PDF Formatting
- Convert from word processor (not scan) when possible
- Embed fonts
- Bookmarks for sections over 20 pages
- Hyperlinks to cited cases (where available)

### File Size
- Check local limits (typically 35-50 MB)
- Compress images
- Split large documents if necessary

### Redaction
- True redaction (not just black boxes over text)
- Verify redacted text not recoverable
- File separate redacted and unredacted versions if required

## Specific Court Examples

### Southern District of New York
- Individual Practices govern document length
- 25-page presumptive limit for most motions
- Pre-motion conference letters: 3 pages max
- 12-point font minimum

### Northern District of California
- 25-page limit for motions (excluding tables and signature block)
- Proposed orders required as separate documents
- Civil Local Rule 7-2 governs length

### Central District of California
- 35-page limit for dispositive motions
- 25-page limit for non-dispositive motions
- Local Rule 11-6: document formatting requirements

### District of Delaware
- Default Standard: 30 pages for opening brief, 30 pages for answering brief, 15 pages for reply
- D. Del. LR 7.1.3

## Certificate of Service

**Required Elements:**
- Date of service
- Parties served
- Method of service (ECF, mail, email, personal delivery)
- Server's signature

**Example:**
```
CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I hereby certify that on [DATE], I electronically filed the foregoing with
the Clerk of Court using the CM/ECF system, which will send notification of
such filing to all counsel of record.

/s/ John Smith
John Smith
```

## Practice Recommendations

1. **Review Local Rules First:** Formatting requirements vary significantly
2. **Check Individual Judge's Practices:** Many judges have specific requirements
3. **Use Templates:** Maintain compliant templates for each court
4. **Final Review:** Check formatting after converting to PDF
5. **Leave Buffer:** Stay under page/word limits by 5-10%
6. **Professional Appearance:** Consistent formatting reflects credibility
7. **Proofread:** Formatting errors distract from substantive arguments
