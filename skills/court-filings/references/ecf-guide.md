# Electronic Case Filing (ECF) and PACER Guide

## Overview

The Case Management/Electronic Case Files (CM/ECF) system is the federal judiciary's comprehensive case management system used by federal district courts, bankruptcy courts, and courts of appeals. PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) provides public access to case information filed through CM/ECF.

## CM/ECF Registration

### Who Must Register

**Mandatory Filers:**
- Attorneys admitted to practice in the court
- Attorneys admitted pro hac vice
- United States Attorneys and Department of Justice attorneys
- Trustees in bankruptcy cases
- Federal government agencies appearing in litigation

**Exemptions:**
- Pro se litigants (may opt in with court permission in many districts)
- Sealed or ex parte filings in certain circumstances

### Registration Process

**Step 1: Bar Admission**
- Must be admitted to the court's bar before ECF registration
- Some courts allow simultaneous admission and ECF registration

**Step 2: Training Requirements**
- Most courts require completion of ECF training
- Options: online tutorial, in-person training, or certification exam
- Training materials available on each court's website

**Step 3: Registration Application**
- Complete court's ECF registration form
- Provide: name, bar number, firm affiliation, contact information
- Agree to Electronic Filing Policies and Procedures

**Step 4: Credentials Issued**
- Login and password provided by Clerk's office
- Some courts use PACER login credentials for ECF
- Unified login system (PACER.uscourts.gov) now used by most courts

### PACER Account Setup

**Registration:** pacer.uscourts.gov
- Individual or group accounts available
- Requires: name, address, email, payment information

**Fees (as of 2024):**
- $0.10 per page accessed (capped at $3.00 per document)
- Quarterly billing
- Audio files: $2.40 per file
- Exemptions available for qualified researchers and certain users

## Filing Procedures

### Before Filing

**Document Preparation:**
1. Create document in word processor
2. Convert to PDF (see PDF Requirements below)
3. Redact personal identifiers per Fed. R. Civ. P. 5.2
4. Verify all attachments are included
5. Ensure document is signed (electronic signature)

**Personal Identifier Redactions (Fed. R. Civ. P. 5.2):**
- Social Security numbers: Last 4 digits only
- Taxpayer ID numbers: Last 4 digits only
- Birth dates: Year only
- Financial account numbers: Last 4 digits only
- Names of minor children: Initials only

### Filing a Document

**Step 1: Login to CM/ECF**
- Access via court's website or PACER
- Use assigned login credentials

**Step 2: Select Filing Type**
- Civil, Criminal, or Miscellaneous
- Select case number

**Step 3: Select Document Type**
- Choose from event menu (motion, response, notice, etc.)
- If unsure, use generic category or contact Clerk's office

**Step 4: Upload Document**
- Browse and attach main document (PDF)
- Attach exhibits and supporting documents
- Verify correct documents attached

**Step 5: Associate Parties**
- Link document to relevant parties
- Select filer(s)

**Step 6: Enter Description**
- Provide meaningful docket text
- Include relevant details (e.g., "Motion to Compel Discovery Responses")

**Step 7: Review and Submit**
- Verify all information
- Submit filing
- System generates Notice of Electronic Filing (NEF)

### Notice of Electronic Filing (NEF)

Upon successful filing, the system generates an NEF containing:
- Date and time of filing
- Case name and number
- Document number
- Document description
- Electronic link to document (one free look for recipients)
- List of parties receiving electronic notice

**One Free Look Rule:**
- Recipients registered for e-filing receive one free access to filed documents via NEF link
- Link expires after first access or after specified time (varies by court)

### Filing Deadlines

**Local Time Rules:**
- Filings are stamped with the time received by the court's server
- Most courts use Eastern Time for deadline purposes
- Check local rules for specific court's time zone

**Midnight Deadline Rule:**
- Documents due on a particular day may be filed until 11:59:59 PM in the court's time zone (unless local rule specifies otherwise)
- Fed. R. Civ. P. 6(a)(4): Electronic filing received before midnight is timely

**Technical Failure Provisions:**
- If CM/ECF is unavailable, filing deadline may be extended
- Filer must demonstrate technical failure (not user error)
- Document filing attempts and error messages
- File as soon as possible when system restored

### Service via CM/ECF

**Automatic Electronic Service:**
- Filing through CM/ECF constitutes service on all registered users
- NEF serves as proof of service
- Fed. R. Civ. P. 5(b)(2)(E)

**Non-Electronic Service Required:**
- Parties not registered for e-filing must be served conventionally
- Include certificate of service identifying method used for non-ECF parties

## PDF Requirements

### Technical Specifications

**Format:**
- PDF version 1.4 or later (Acrobat 5.0+)
- Text-based PDF (not image-only scans when possible)
- Text-searchable (OCR applied to scanned documents)

**File Size Limits:**
- Varies by court (typically 35-50 MB per document)
- Large documents may need to be split
- Check local administrative procedures

**Security Settings:**
- No password protection
- No document restrictions (must allow copying, printing)
- No digital rights management (DRM)

**Naming Conventions:**
- Avoid special characters (use only letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores)
- Keep filenames reasonably short
- Descriptive names helpful (e.g., "Motion_to_Dismiss.pdf")

### Creating Compliant PDFs

**From Word Processors:**
- Use "Save As" or "Export" to PDF function
- Ensure fonts are embedded
- Produces text-searchable PDF

**From Scanned Documents:**
- Scan at 300 DPI minimum (200 DPI acceptable)
- Black and white preferred (reduces file size)
- Apply OCR for searchability
- Verify legibility before filing

**Combining Multiple Documents:**
- Use PDF software to merge documents
- Create bookmarks for navigation
- Maintain sequential page numbering

### Exhibits and Attachments

**Filing Methods:**
- Attach as separate PDFs to main document
- Large exhibits may require separate filings
- Use CM/ECF attachment feature

**Bookmarking:**
- Create bookmarks for each exhibit
- Label clearly (Exhibit A, Exhibit B, etc.)

**Voluminous Exhibits:**
- Check local rules for page limits
- Consider filing representative samples with reference to complete materials
- Sealed exhibits require separate handling

## PACER Access

### Searching for Cases

**Search Options:**
- Party name
- Case number
- Date range
- Attorney name
- Nature of suit

**Case Information Available:**
- Docket sheet
- Filed documents (subject to access restrictions)
- Party and attorney information
- Court events and deadlines

### Downloading Documents

**Individual Documents:**
- Click document link on docket sheet
- Pay per-page fee (or use free look from NEF)
- Download PDF to local system

**Batch Downloads:**
- Some third-party services available
- PACER Case Locator for cross-district searches

### Fee Exemptions

**Eligible Users:**
- Federal judiciary employees (for official use)
- Parties in a case (limited access to own case)
- CJA panel attorneys (for appointed cases)
- Certain academic and nonprofit researchers (apply to court)

## Court-Specific Variations

### District Courts
- Each district may have supplemental electronic filing procedures
- Check local rules and administrative orders
- Standing orders may impose additional requirements

### Bankruptcy Courts
- Use same CM/ECF system
- Additional event types for bankruptcy-specific filings
- Proof of claim filing deadlines strictly enforced

### Courts of Appeals
- CM/ECF for circuit courts
- Appellate-specific events (notice of appeal, briefs, appendices)
- Some circuits have distinct technical requirements

### District-Specific Examples

**S.D.N.Y.:**
- ECF Rules and Instructions (available on court website)
- Specific exhibit filing procedures
- Pro se electronic filing pilot program

**N.D. Cal.:**
- ADR/Mediation filings through ECF
- Technical failure reporting procedures
- Specific PDF naming conventions

**E.D. Va.:**
- "Rocket Docket" - expedited procedures
- Early disclosure requirements via ECF

## Common Filing Errors and Solutions

| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| File too large | Exceeds size limit | Compress PDF or split into parts |
| Invalid PDF | Corrupted or wrong format | Re-create PDF, verify format |
| Wrong event selected | Incorrect docket entry | File corrected document or contact Clerk |
| Missing attachment | Forgot to attach exhibit | File notice of errata with attachment |
| Wrong case number | Filed in wrong case | Contact Clerk immediately |
| Deadline missed | Technical failure | Document issue, seek relief from court |

## Emergency and After-Hours Filings

### Regular Hours
- Clerk's office: typically 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM local time
- ECF available 24/7

### After-Hours Emergencies
- TRO or emergency motion requiring immediate attention
- Contact duty clerk or emergency line (posted on court website)
- File via ECF and separately alert chambers

### Court Closures
- Federal holidays: filing deadline extends to next business day
- Weather closures: check court website for orders extending deadlines

## Best Practices

1. **Test System Access:** Login before filing deadlines to verify credentials work

2. **Prepare Early:** Don't wait until deadline; system slowdowns common near close of business

3. **Maintain Records:** Save NEFs and copies of all filed documents

4. **Monitor Docket:** Review docket regularly for court orders and opposing filings

5. **Update Contact Information:** Keep email and address current in CM/ECF profile

6. **Redaction Review:** Double-check for personal identifiers before filing

7. **Naming Conventions:** Use clear, descriptive filenames

8. **Service List:** Maintain accurate list of parties requiring conventional service

9. **Technical Documentation:** Screenshot errors and document system failures

10. **Local Rules Review:** Consult court-specific ECF procedures before filing
