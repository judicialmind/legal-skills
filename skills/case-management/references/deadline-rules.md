# Deadline Calculation Rules

## Federal Rules of Civil Procedure - Rule 6

### Rule 6(a) - Computing Time

#### Day of the Event
The day of the event that triggers the period is **not counted**.

Example: Motion filed Monday, January 15. A 14-day response period begins Tuesday, January 16.

#### Intermediate Days
Count every day, including weekends and holidays, unless the period is less than 11 days (pre-2009 rules).

#### Last Day of Period

**If the last day falls on:**
- Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday → deadline extends to next business day
- Day the clerk's office is inaccessible → extends to next accessible day

### Rule 6(d) - Additional Time After Service

When a party must act within a specified time after being served and service is made by:

| Service Method | Additional Days |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Mail (First Class) | +3 days |
| Electronic Service | +3 days |
| Leaving with Clerk | +3 days |
| Other means consented to | +3 days |

**Note:** The 3 additional days are added AFTER the base period expires.

Example: 21-day response period + 3 days for mail service = 24 days total.

## Court Days vs. Calendar Days

### Calendar Days (Standard)
Most federal deadlines use calendar days:
- Include weekends and holidays in count
- If deadline falls on weekend/holiday, extends to next business day

### Court Days (Business Days)
Some state courts and specific rules use court days:
- Count only days the court is open
- Exclude weekends, holidays, court closures

### Identifying Which Applies
- Federal courts: Calendar days (FRCP 6)
- State courts: Check local rules
- Administrative agencies: Check specific regulations
- Contract deadlines: As specified in agreement

## Backward Calculation Method

For working backward from a hearing or trial date:

### Steps
1. Start with the target date (hearing/trial)
2. Subtract the required number of days
3. Check if result falls on weekend/holiday
4. If yes, move EARLIER (not later) to ensure compliance
5. Add buffer for filing logistics

### Example: Motion Hearing

```
Hearing Date: Monday, March 18, 2024
Motion Due: 21 days before hearing

Calculation:
March 18 - 21 days = February 26, 2024 (Monday)
February 26 is a business day → Motion due February 26

If February 26 were Sunday:
Move to Friday, February 23 (earlier date ensures compliance)
```

## State Court Variations

### California
- CCP 1013: +5 days for mail (in-state), +10 days (out-of-state)
- +2 days for electronic service
- Court holidays per Government Code 6700

### New York
- CPLR 2103: +5 days for mail service
- Business days used for some calculations
- Note: NY uses "days" and "business days" inconsistently

### Texas
- TRCP 21a: +3 days for mail/email/fax
- File by 5:00 PM on due date
- Electronic filing permitted after hours

### Illinois
- 735 ILCS 5/2-101: Standard mail adds 3 days
- Check circuit court local rules

## Deadline Calculation Checklist

- [ ] Identify triggering event (service, filing, order)
- [ ] Determine applicable rule set (federal, state, local)
- [ ] Count from day after trigger
- [ ] Apply correct day type (calendar vs. court)
- [ ] Add service method days if applicable
- [ ] Check if falls on weekend/holiday
- [ ] Verify court closure dates
- [ ] Apply local rule modifications
- [ ] Build in buffer for review
- [ ] Calendar primary AND backup reminders

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Forgetting service days** - Always add 3 days for non-hand service
2. **State vs. federal rules** - Don't assume same calculation method
3. **Electronic filing cutoffs** - Know if it's midnight or 5 PM
4. **Time zone issues** - Use court's local time
5. **Local rules** - May modify standard deadlines
6. **Holiday variations** - State holidays differ from federal
