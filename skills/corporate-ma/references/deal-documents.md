# M&A Transaction Document Checklist

## Overview

This checklist covers the essential documents required for mergers and acquisitions transactions, from initial negotiations through closing. The specific documents vary based on deal structure (asset purchase, stock purchase, or merger), transaction size, and regulatory requirements.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-LOI Documents

### Confidentiality Agreement (NDA)
- **Purpose**: Protects proprietary information exchanged during due diligence
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Definition of "Confidential Information" (broad vs. narrow)
  - Permitted disclosures (representatives, advisors, financing sources)
  - Standstill provisions (restrict hostile actions)
  - Non-solicitation of employees
  - Term (typically 2-3 years for confidentiality, 12-24 months for standstill)
  - Return/destruction of materials
  - Residual knowledge carve-out
  - Securities law disclaimer (material non-public information)

### Teaser/Blind Profile
- One-page anonymous company description
- High-level financial metrics
- Industry and geographic overview
- Transaction rationale

### Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM)
- Detailed company overview
- Historical financials (3-5 years)
- Management team profiles
- Market analysis and competitive positioning
- Growth opportunities
- Risk factors

### Management Presentation Materials
- Slide deck for buyer meetings
- Q&A preparation documents
- Site visit protocols

---

## Phase 2: Letter of Intent (LOI) / Term Sheet

### Non-Binding LOI Components
- **Purchase Price**: Fixed amount, formula, or range
- **Consideration Structure**: Cash, stock, earnout, seller notes
- **Transaction Type**: Asset, stock, or merger
- **Key Assumptions**: Debt-free, cash-free, normalized working capital
- **Due Diligence Period**: Typically 30-90 days
- **Exclusivity Period**: 45-90 days (binding provision)
- **Key Conditions**: Financing, regulatory approvals, third-party consents
- **Confidentiality**: Reference to existing NDA (binding provision)
- **Expenses**: Each party bears own costs
- **Governing Law**: Jurisdiction selection
- **Break-up Fee**: If applicable (binding provision)

### Binding LOI Provisions
- Exclusivity/No-Shop
- Confidentiality
- Expense allocation
- Governing law
- Dispute resolution

### Term Sheet Variations
- Equity term sheet (for stock deals)
- Debt commitment term sheet (acquisition financing)
- Management equity term sheet (rollover/incentive equity)

## Phase 3: Due Diligence Request List

### Corporate Documents
- Certificate of incorporation and bylaws (and all amendments)
- Good standing certificates (all jurisdictions)
- Minute books (board and shareholder meetings)
- Stock ledger and capitalization table
- Shareholder agreements
- Voting agreements and proxies
- Organizational chart (legal entities)

### Financial Documents
- Audited financial statements (3 years)
- Interim unaudited financials
- Tax returns (federal, state, local - 3 years)
- Budget and projections
- Accounts receivable aging
- Accounts payable aging
- Debt schedule with agreements
- Working capital analysis

### Material Contracts
- Customer contracts (top 10-20)
- Supplier/vendor agreements
- Distribution agreements
- Licensing agreements (IP in and out)
- Joint venture agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Government contracts

### Employment Matters
- Employment agreements (executives)
- Offer letters (key employees)
- Employee handbook
- Benefit plan documents (401(k), health, equity)
- Union/collective bargaining agreements
- WARN Act compliance history
- I-9 audit results
- Discrimination/harassment claims

### Intellectual Property
- Patent schedule with status
- Trademark registrations
- Copyright registrations
- Trade secret inventory
- Domain name portfolio
- Software licenses
- IP assignment agreements
- Open source software usage

### Litigation and Regulatory
- Pending litigation (pleadings, discovery status)
- Threatened claims (demand letters)
- Regulatory investigations
- Consent decrees and settlements
- Insurance claims history
- Environmental reports and permits

## Phase 4: Definitive Agreement (Stock Purchase Agreement / Asset Purchase Agreement / Merger Agreement)

### Preamble and Recitals
- Party identification
- Transaction background
- Defined terms section

### Purchase and Sale Provisions
- **Stock Purchase**: Shares being acquired, per-share price
- **Asset Purchase**: Included/excluded assets, assumed/excluded liabilities
- **Merger**: Conversion of shares, treatment of options/warrants

### Purchase Price Mechanics
- Base purchase price
- Working capital adjustment mechanism
  - Target working capital
  - Estimation at closing
  - Post-closing true-up
  - Dispute resolution (accountant arbitration)
- Earnout provisions (if applicable)
  - Performance metrics (revenue, EBITDA)
  - Measurement period
  - Accounting principles
  - Acceleration events
  - Catch-up provisions
- Escrow arrangements
  - Indemnification escrow (typically 10-15% for 12-24 months)
  - Working capital escrow
  - Escrow agent selection

### Representations and Warranties
**Seller Representations**:
- Organization and good standing
- Authorization and enforceability
- Capitalization (for stock deals)
- Financial statements
- Absence of undisclosed liabilities
- Absence of material adverse change
- Compliance with laws
- Litigation
- Taxes
- Employee benefits
- Environmental matters
- Intellectual property
- Material contracts
- Insurance
- Real property
- Customers and suppliers
- Related party transactions
- Brokers' fees

**Buyer Representations**:
- Organization and good standing
- Authorization and enforceability
- Financing capability
- No conflicts
- Litigation
- Investment intent (for private deals)

### Covenants
**Pre-Closing Covenants**:
- Conduct of business in ordinary course
- Negative covenants (no extraordinary actions)
- Access to information
- Notification of developments
- Regulatory filings
- Third-party consents
- Financing cooperation
- No-shop/exclusivity

**Post-Closing Covenants**:
- Non-competition (seller, key employees)
- Non-solicitation
- Confidentiality
- Books and records access
- Tax matters
- Further assurances
- R&W insurance cooperation

### Conditions to Closing
**Mutual Conditions**:
- Regulatory approvals (HSR, CFIUS, industry-specific)
- No injunctions or legal impediments
- Third-party consents

**Buyer's Conditions**:
- Accuracy of seller representations
- Seller covenant compliance
- No material adverse effect
- Deliverables received

**Seller's Conditions**:
- Accuracy of buyer representations
- Buyer covenant compliance
- Deliverables received

### Indemnification
- Survival periods
  - General representations (12-24 months)
  - Fundamental representations (statute of limitations or longer)
  - Tax representations (statute of limitations plus extensions)
- Baskets (deductible vs. tipping)
- Caps (percentage of purchase price)
- Exclusions from caps (fraud, willful breach, fundamental reps)
- Exclusive remedy provision
- Materiality scrape (for determining breach vs. calculating damages)
- Indemnification procedures
- Third-party claim procedures
- Set-off rights

### Termination
- Mutual termination right
- Outside date
- Regulatory failure
- Breach of representations or covenants
- Material adverse effect
- Termination fees and expense reimbursement

### Miscellaneous
- Expenses
- Notices
- Amendment and waiver
- Governing law
- Jurisdiction and venue
- Specific performance
- Entire agreement
- Severability
- Assignment
- Third-party beneficiaries
- Counterparts and electronic signatures

## Phase 5: Disclosure Schedules

### Purpose and Function
- Exceptions to representations and warranties
- Qualifying and limiting seller's disclosure obligations
- Creating factual record at signing

### Common Schedules
- Schedule 3.1: Subsidiaries and equity investments
- Schedule 3.3: Consents and approvals required
- Schedule 3.4: Capitalization details
- Schedule 3.6: Financial statement exceptions
- Schedule 3.7: Undisclosed liabilities
- Schedule 3.8: Material adverse changes since balance sheet date
- Schedule 3.10: Tax matters
- Schedule 3.11: Real property (owned and leased)
- Schedule 3.12: Intellectual property
- Schedule 3.13: Material contracts
- Schedule 3.14: Employee matters and benefit plans
- Schedule 3.15: Environmental matters
- Schedule 3.16: Pending and threatened litigation
- Schedule 3.17: Insurance policies
- Schedule 3.18: Permits and licenses
- Schedule 3.19: Customers and suppliers
- Schedule 3.20: Related party transactions
- Schedule 3.21: Bank accounts and powers of attorney

### Drafting Considerations
- Specific vs. general disclosures
- Cross-references between schedules
- Updating schedules pre-closing (bring-down issues)
- Knowledge qualifiers

---

## Phase 6: Ancillary Agreements

### Employment and Compensation
- Employment agreements (key executives)
- Severance agreements
- Retention bonus agreements
- Non-competition agreements
- Consulting agreements (transitional)
- Management equity agreements (rollover, options, profits interests)

### Seller-Related
- Escrow agreement
- Non-competition agreement (with selling shareholders)
- Transition services agreement
- Intellectual property license/assignment
- Real property lease (if seller retains property)
- Supply agreement (ongoing relationship)

### Financing Documents
- Credit agreement (acquisition financing)
- Security agreements
- Guarantees
- Intercreditor agreement
- Pledge agreements

### Corporate Documents
- Officer and director resignations
- Stock powers
- Assignment of contracts
- Bill of sale (asset deals)
- Assignment and assumption agreement
- IP assignment documents

### Regulatory and Third-Party
- HSR filing and supporting documents
- CFIUS filing (if applicable)
- Third-party consent letters
- Landlord estoppel certificates
- Customer consent letters

---

## Phase 7: Closing Documents

### Seller Closing Deliverables
- Secretary's certificate (resolutions, incumbency, charter documents)
- Good standing certificates
- Executed definitive agreement
- Executed ancillary agreements
- Stock certificates with blank stock powers
- Payoff letters for existing debt
- FIRPTA certificate
- Legal opinions
- Bring-down certificate
- Release of liens (UCC-3s)
- Resignations (officers, directors as requested)

### Buyer Closing Deliverables
- Secretary's certificate
- Good standing certificates
- Executed definitive agreement
- Executed ancillary agreements
- Evidence of financing
- Legal opinions
- Bring-down certificate
- Purchase price wire instructions

### Post-Closing Filings
- UCC financing statements (new liens)
- State secretary of state filings (mergers, name changes)
- SEC filings (if applicable)
- Press release
- Employee communications
- Customer/vendor notifications

## Document Management Best Practices

### Virtual Data Room Organization
- Maintain consistent folder structure
- Index all documents
- Track access and downloads
- Implement version control
- Secure watermarking for sensitive documents

### Signature Coordination
- Create signature packet checklists
- Coordinate simultaneous execution
- Verify authorized signatories
- Maintain execution copies
- Distribute fully executed sets

### Closing Set Compilation
- Organize by document type
- Include table of contents
- Maintain original signatures where required
- Provide digital copies to all parties
