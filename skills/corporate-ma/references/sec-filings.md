# SEC Filing Requirements for M&A Transactions

## Overview

Public company M&A transactions trigger numerous SEC filing obligations. This reference covers the primary filings required under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and the Securities Act of 1933, including timing requirements, content requirements, and common compliance issues.

---

## Current Reports (Form 8-K)

### Purpose
Form 8-K discloses material events that shareholders should know about between regular periodic reports. M&A transactions are among the most significant reportable events.

### Filing Deadlines
- **Standard deadline**: 4 business days after triggering event
- **Certain items**: Different deadlines may apply
- **Voluntary 8-Ks**: May be filed at any time

### M&A-Related Triggering Events

#### Item 1.01 - Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement
**When Required**: Upon execution of definitive M&A agreement
**Content Requirements**:
- Date of agreement
- Parties to the agreement
- Material terms and conditions:
  - Purchase price and consideration structure
  - Material representations and warranties
  - Covenants and conditions to closing
  - Termination rights and fees
  - Regulatory approvals required
- Brief description of the business being acquired
- Any material relationships between parties

**Exhibit Requirements**:
- Full agreement filed as Exhibit 2.1
- Confidential treatment requests for sensitive schedules
- Note: Disclosure schedules typically omitted with summary disclosure

#### Item 1.02 - Termination of a Material Definitive Agreement
**When Required**: Upon termination of previously disclosed M&A agreement
**Content Requirements**:
- Date of termination
- Reason for termination
- Any material termination fees or payments
- Whether parties have any continuing obligations

#### Item 2.01 - Completion of Acquisition or Disposition of Assets
**When Required**: Upon closing of a significant acquisition or disposition
**Significance Tests** (any one triggers filing):
- **Investment test**: Target assets exceed 10% of acquirer's total assets
- **Asset test**: Target assets exceed 10% of acquirer's total assets
- **Income test**: Target pretax income exceeds 10% of acquirer's income

**Content Requirements**:
- Date of closing
- Description of assets acquired/disposed
- Identity of other party
- Nature and amount of consideration
- Source of funds (for acquisitions)
- Pro forma financial information (if significant)

**Financial Statement Requirements** (for significant acquisitions):
- Target's audited financial statements (if >20% significant)
- Pro forma financial statements
- Filed within 71 days of closing if not included in initial 8-K

#### Item 2.05 - Costs Associated with Exit or Disposal Activities
**When Required**: Board commits to exit/disposal plan with material costs
**Content Requirements**:
- Date of commitment
- Material actions being taken
- Estimated range of costs
- Estimated charges and cash expenditures

#### Item 2.06 - Material Impairments
**When Required**: Conclusion that material asset impairment charge required
**Content Requirements**:
- Date of determination
- Assets involved
- Estimated amount of impairment
- Facts leading to conclusion

#### Item 3.01 - Notice of Delisting or Failure to Satisfy Listing Standards
**When Required**: Notice from exchange or company determination
**Content Requirements**:
- Date of notice/determination
- Listing rule(s) at issue
- Actions company intends to take

#### Item 5.01 - Changes in Control of Registrant
**When Required**: Change in control occurs
**Content Requirements**:
- Identity of persons acquiring control
- Date and description of transaction
- Percentage of voting securities acquired
- Source of consideration
- Identity of persons from whom control passed

#### Item 5.02 - Departure/Appointment of Directors or Officers
**When Required**: Departure/election of directors, appointment of officers
**Content Requirements**:
- Name and position of departing/incoming individual
- Date and circumstances of departure
- Biographical information for new directors/officers
- Related party transactions
- Compensatory arrangements

#### Item 9.01 - Financial Statements and Exhibits
**Required Exhibits for M&A**:
- (a) Financial statements of acquired business
- (b) Pro forma financial information
- (d) Exhibits (agreements, press releases)

## Annual Reports (Form 10-K)

### Filing Deadlines by Filer Category
| Filer Category | Deadline | Public Float Threshold |
|----------------|----------|----------------------|
| Large Accelerated | 60 days | $700M+ |
| Accelerated | 75 days | $75M - $700M |
| Non-Accelerated | 90 days | Under $75M |
| Smaller Reporting | 90 days | Under $250M revenue |

### M&A Disclosure Requirements

#### Part I
**Item 1 - Business**:
- Description of acquired businesses
- Integration status
- Synergy realization
- Material changes to operations

**Item 1A - Risk Factors**:
- Integration risks
- Retention of key employees
- Customer/supplier relationships
- Regulatory approval risks
- Financing risks

#### Part II
**Item 7 - MD&A**:
- Impact on results of operations
- Pro forma comparisons
- Integration costs
- Goodwill and intangible asset values
- Synergy expectations

**Item 8 - Financial Statements**:
- Acquisition accounting (ASC 805)
- Purchase price allocation
- Goodwill and intangibles
- Pro forma financial information
- Contingent consideration

#### Exhibit Requirements
- Material agreements (Exhibits 2.1, 10.x)
- Updated list of subsidiaries (Exhibit 21)
- Consent of accountants (Exhibit 23)

## Schedule 13D (Beneficial Ownership Report)

### Filing Trigger
- Acquisition of more than 5% beneficial ownership of any class of voting equity securities
- Filed within 10 days of crossing 5% threshold

### Content Requirements

**Item 1 - Security and Issuer**:
- Title of class of securities
- Name and address of issuer

**Item 2 - Identity and Background**:
- Name, address, citizenship of reporting person
- Business/employment information
- Criminal convictions (securities related)
- Civil judgments (securities related)

**Item 3 - Source and Amount of Funds**:
- Total consideration paid
- Source of funds (borrowed funds, terms)
- Material agreements for financing

**Item 4 - Purpose of Transaction**:
- Purpose of acquisition
- Plans or proposals for:
  - Acquisition of additional securities
  - Extraordinary corporate transactions (merger, reorganization)
  - Sale of material assets
  - Changes in board composition
  - Changes in capitalization
  - Delisting or deregistration
  - Changes to charter or bylaws
  - Other material changes

**Item 5 - Interest in Securities of Issuer**:
- Number of shares beneficially owned
- Percentage of class
- Sole voting power
- Shared voting power
- Sole dispositive power
- Shared dispositive power
- Transactions in past 60 days

**Item 6 - Contracts, Arrangements, Understandings**:
- Material contracts regarding securities
- Voting agreements
- Joint filing agreements
- Options, warrants, convertible securities

**Item 7 - Material to Be Filed as Exhibits**:
- Agreements relating to filing
- Letters, contracts regarding Item 4 matters

### Amendment Requirements
- **Prompt amendment** required for material changes
- Change in holdings of 1% or more
- Material change in purpose or plans
- No specific deadline but "promptly" interpreted as 1-2 business days

### Schedule 13G (Short-Form Alternative)
**Eligible Filers**:
- Qualified Institutional Investors (QIIs)
- Passive investors (under 20%, no control intent)
- Exempt investors

**Filing Deadlines**:
- QIIs: 45 days after year-end (annual), 10 days after month-end if >10%
- Passive investors: 10 days after acquisition

## Section 16 Filings (Forms 3, 4, and 5)

### Form 3 - Initial Statement of Beneficial Ownership

**Who Files**: Directors, officers, and 10% shareholders
**When Filed**: Within 10 days of becoming an insider
**Content**: All equity securities beneficially owned

### Form 4 - Statement of Changes in Beneficial Ownership

**Who Files**: Directors, officers, and 10% shareholders
**When Filed**: Within 2 business days of transaction
**Content**:
- Transaction date
- Securities acquired or disposed
- Price per share
- Direct or indirect ownership
- Transaction codes (P=purchase, S=sale, A=award, etc.)

### Form 5 - Annual Statement of Beneficial Ownership

**Who Files**: Directors, officers, and 10% shareholders
**When Filed**: Within 45 days after fiscal year-end
**Content**: Transactions not previously reported, small acquisitions, gifts

### M&A-Related Section 16 Issues
- Merger consideration (automatic Form 4 trigger)
- Option acceleration
- Restricted stock vesting
- Equity award assumption by acquirer
- Director/officer changes following merger

## Proxy Statements (Schedule 14A)

### When Required for M&A
- Shareholder vote required for transaction
- Merger under state law
- Sale of substantially all assets
- Amendment to charter
- Issuance of shares exceeding thresholds

### Preliminary vs. Definitive Proxy

**Preliminary Proxy Statement**:
- Filed at least 10 days before definitive
- SEC review period
- Confidential treatment available for M&A

**Definitive Proxy Statement**:
- Filed on or before mailing to shareholders
- Includes all shareholder meeting materials

### Required M&A Disclosures

**Item 14 - Mergers, Consolidations, Acquisitions**:
- Summary of transaction
- Background of transaction (chronology of negotiations)
- Reasons for transaction
- Board recommendation
- Fairness opinions
- Interests of directors and officers
- Financing arrangements
- Regulatory approvals required
- Accounting treatment
- Material federal income tax consequences
- Dissenters'/appraisal rights

**Financial Information Requirements**:
- Selected historical financial data
- Pro forma financial information
- Comparative per share data
- Quantified material benefits to insiders

### Fairness Opinion Disclosure
- Identity of financial advisor
- Opinion summary
- Material relationships with parties
- Compensation arrangements
- Analyses performed
- Assumptions and limitations

## Registration Statements (Form S-1 and Form S-4)

### Form S-4 (Business Combinations)

**When Required**:
- Securities issued in merger/acquisition
- Exchange offers

**Part I - Information Required in Prospectus**:
- Summary
- Risk factors
- Selected financial data
- Pro forma financial information
- Description of securities being offered
- Comparison of shareholder rights

**Part II - Information Not Required in Prospectus**:
- Expenses of issuance
- Indemnification of directors and officers
- Exhibits and financial statement schedules

### Form S-1 (General Registration)

**When Used in M&A**:
- Registering securities for resale
- Registering acquisition consideration
- Shelf registration for acquisition currency

### SEC Review Process
- Initial filing and comment period (30 days typical)
- Response to comments
- Acceleration request
- Effectiveness

## Hart-Scott-Rodino (HSR) Act Filings

### Notification Requirements
While not an SEC filing, HSR intersects with securities disclosures.

**Size of Transaction Test** (2024 thresholds):
- $119.5 million (minimum threshold)
- $478 million (no size-of-person test needed)

**Size of Person Test** (when transaction $119.5M - $478M):
- One party has $239 million in assets or sales
- Other party has $23.9 million in assets or sales

### Filing and Waiting Periods
- Initial waiting period: 30 days (15 days for cash tender offers)
- Second request: Additional 30 days after substantial compliance
- Early termination available

## Tender Offer Filings (Schedule TO and Schedule 14D-9)

### Schedule TO (Bidder's Statement)

**When Required**: Commencement of tender offer
**Filing Deadline**: As soon as practicable on commencement date

**Content Requirements**:
- Summary term sheet
- Subject company information
- Identity and background of bidder
- Terms of transaction
- Past contacts with subject company
- Purpose and plans
- Source and amount of funds
- Financial statements (if consideration includes securities)
- Conditions to offer
- Regulatory requirements
- Fees and expenses

### Schedule 14D-9 (Target's Response)

**When Required**: Within 10 business days of tender offer commencement
**Content Requirements**:
- Recommendation of board (accept, reject, neutral, unable to take position)
- Reasons for recommendation
- Intent of directors and officers regarding their shares
- Interests of directors and officers
- Background of transaction
- Financial information

### All-Cash Offer Amendments
- Report on tendered shares
- Extension notices
- Changes in terms

## Compliance Best Practices

### Timeline Management
1. Create filing calendar at transaction outset
2. Identify all filing triggers
3. Assign responsibility for each filing
4. Build in review time before deadlines
5. Monitor for late-breaking material events

### Coordination Between Counsel
- Securities counsel
- M&A counsel
- Tax counsel
- Antitrust counsel
- Accounting advisors

### Common Pitfalls
- Missing Item 2.01 financial statement deadlines
- Inadequate background disclosure in proxy statements
- Failing to update Schedule 13D promptly
- Inconsistent disclosure across multiple filings
- Form 4 deadline calculation errors

### Document Retention
- Maintain filing records for minimum 5 years
- Preserve drafts showing diligence
- Document materiality determinations
- Retain counsel communications (attorney-client privilege)
