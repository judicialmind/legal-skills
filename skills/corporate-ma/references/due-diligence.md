# M&A Due Diligence Checklist

## Overview

This comprehensive checklist covers all major due diligence areas for M&A transactions. Customize based on target company industry, deal size, and specific risk areas.

---

## 1. Corporate Organization

### 1.1 Formation Documents
- [ ] Certificate/Articles of Incorporation (and all amendments)
- [ ] Bylaws (and all amendments)
- [ ] Operating Agreement (for LLCs)
- [ ] Partnership Agreement (for partnerships)
- [ ] Good Standing Certificates (state of incorporation + qualified states)
- [ ] Qualifications to do business in foreign jurisdictions

### 1.2 Corporate Records
- [ ] Board of Directors meeting minutes (3-5 years)
- [ ] Shareholder meeting minutes (3-5 years)
- [ ] Written consents of Board and shareholders
- [ ] Committee meeting minutes (audit, compensation)
- [ ] Current list of officers and directors
- [ ] Director and officer questionnaires
- [ ] Indemnification agreements

### 1.3 Capitalization
- [ ] Capitalization table (fully diluted)
- [ ] Stock ledger
- [ ] Stock certificates (sample)
- [ ] Stock option plans and agreements
- [ ] Warrant agreements
- [ ] Convertible note agreements
- [ ] SAFEs or other convertible instruments
- [ ] Anti-dilution provisions
- [ ] 409A valuations

### 1.4 Shareholder Matters
- [ ] Shareholder agreements
- [ ] Voting agreements
- [ ] Registration rights agreements
- [ ] Right of first refusal agreements
- [ ] Co-sale agreements
- [ ] Investor rights agreements
- [ ] Management rights letters

### 1.5 Subsidiaries and Affiliates
- [ ] Organizational chart
- [ ] List of subsidiaries (with jurisdiction and ownership %)
- [ ] Intercompany agreements
- [ ] Joint venture agreements
- [ ] Minority investments

## Priority Matrix

### Critical (Deal Breakers)
- Undisclosed material litigation
- Title/ownership issues
- Regulatory violations
- Material contract defaults
- Financial statement fraud

### High (Price/Terms Adjustment)
- Pending claims
- Customer concentration
- Key person dependency
- Compliance gaps
- Deferred maintenance

### Medium (Post-Closing Items)
- Documentation gaps
- Policy updates needed
- Minor compliance issues
- Integration considerations

### Low (Monitor)
- Minor administrative issues
- Best practice improvements
- Future considerations
