# Patent Prosecution Reference Guide

## Overview

Patent prosecution is the process of preparing, filing, and pursuing patent applications before the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO). This guide covers application preparation, examination procedures, office action responses, and appeal strategies.

---

## Patent Application Types

### Utility Patents

**Coverage**
- New and useful processes
- Machines
- Manufactures
- Compositions of matter
- Improvements thereof

**Protection Term**
- 20 years from earliest non-provisional filing date
- Subject to maintenance fees

### Design Patents

**Coverage**
- New, original, and ornamental design for article of manufacture
- Protects appearance, not function

**Protection Term**
- 15 years from grant date (applications filed on/after May 13, 2015)
- No maintenance fees

### Plant Patents

**Coverage**
- New and distinct varieties of asexually reproduced plants
- Excludes tuber-propagated and uncultivated plants

**Protection Term**
- 20 years from filing date
- No maintenance fees

## Application Components

### Specification

**Required Elements**

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Title | Brief description of invention |
| Cross-References | Related applications |
| Background | Prior art context |
| Summary | Brief description of invention |
| Brief Description of Drawings | List of figures |
| Detailed Description | Enable one skilled in art to make and use |
| Claims | Define scope of protection |
| Abstract | Technical summary (150 words max) |

### Detailed Description Requirements

**Enablement (35 U.S.C. Section 112(a))**
- Enable person skilled in art to make and use invention
- Without undue experimentation
- As of filing date

**Written Description (35 U.S.C. Section 112(a))**
- Describe invention in sufficient detail
- Show possession of claimed invention
- Support for claim scope

**Best Mode (35 U.S.C. Section 112(a))**
- Disclose best mode contemplated by inventor
- As of filing date
- Not examined, but defense to infringement

### Claims

**Independent Claims**
- Stand alone
- Broadest scope of protection
- Must include all essential elements

**Dependent Claims**
- Refer to and further limit another claim
- Incorporate all limitations of referenced claim
- Narrower scope

**Claim Structure**
```
[Preamble] - [Transitional phrase] - [Body]

Example:
"A widget [preamble] comprising [transitional]:
a first component configured to perform X [body element 1]; and
a second component connected to the first component [body element 2]."
```

**Transitional Phrases**

| Phrase | Type | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|
| Comprising | Open | Additional elements allowed |
| Consisting of | Closed | Only recited elements |
| Consisting essentially of | Hybrid | Additional non-material elements allowed |

## Filing Procedures

### Provisional Applications

**Benefits**
- Establishes priority date
- "Patent Pending" status
- Lower cost
- 12 months to file non-provisional

**Requirements**
- Written description (specification)
- Drawings if necessary
- Cover sheet
- Filing fee
- No claims required (but recommended)

**Limitations**
- Does not mature into patent
- Not examined
- Must file non-provisional within 12 months

### Non-Provisional Applications

**Filing Requirements**
- Specification with claims
- Drawings (if necessary)
- Filing fee, search fee, examination fee
- Oath/declaration (can be filed later)
- Application data sheet (ADS)

**Priority Claims**
- Domestic priority to provisional (12 months)
- Foreign priority (12 months from foreign filing)
- Continuation priority to parent application

### International Filing (PCT)

**Patent Cooperation Treaty**
- Single filing for multiple countries
- International search report and written opinion
- 30/31 months to enter national phase
- Not a world patent

## Examination Process

### Initial Processing

**Pre-Examination**
1. Filing receipt issued
2. Application assigned to Technology Center
3. Assigned to Examiner
4. Application published at 18 months (unless opted out)

**First Action Timeline**
- Average: 14-24 months from filing
- Varies by Technology Center
- Prioritized examination available

### Office Actions

**Types of Office Actions**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Non-Final Rejection | First rejection; full response right |
| Final Rejection | Second or subsequent; limited response options |
| Notice of Allowance | Application allowed |
| Restriction Requirement | Multiple inventions; elect one |
| Ex Parte Quayle | Claims allowed; formal matters only |

### Common Rejections

**35 U.S.C. Section 101 (Subject Matter)**
- Laws of nature
- Natural phenomena
- Abstract ideas
- Alice/Mayo framework

**35 U.S.C. Section 102 (Novelty)**
- Prior art anticipates claim
- All claim elements in single reference
- Strict identity required

**35 U.S.C. Section 103 (Obviousness)**
- Prior art renders claim obvious
- Combination of references
- Graham v. John Deere factors

**35 U.S.C. Section 112(a) (Written Description/Enablement)**
- Inadequate written description
- Not enabled
- Claims broader than disclosure

**35 U.S.C. Section 112(b) (Indefiniteness)**
- Claims unclear
- Ambiguous scope
- Cannot determine boundaries

## Responding to Office Actions

### Response Deadlines

**Non-Final Office Actions**
- 3 months (extendable to 6 months)
- Extensions available with fee
- Response required to continue prosecution

**Final Office Actions**
- 3 months to respond
- Limited options available

### Amendment Strategies

**Claim Amendment Options**
- Narrow claims to distinguish prior art
- Add limitations from specification
- Cancel and add claims
- Rewrite in different form

**Argument Strategies**
- Distinguish prior art references
- Argue references not combinable
- Challenge motivation to combine
- Emphasize unexpected results
- Secondary considerations of non-obviousness

### Section 103 Response Framework

**Graham v. John Deere Analysis**
1. Determine scope and content of prior art
2. Ascertain differences between prior art and claims
3. Resolve level of ordinary skill
4. Evaluate secondary considerations

**Secondary Considerations**
- Commercial success
- Long-felt but unsolved need
- Failure of others
- Copying
- Unexpected results
- Industry praise
- Licensing

**Teaching Away Argument**
- Prior art discourages combination
- References lead away from invention
- No motivation to modify

### Section 101 Response Framework

**Alice/Mayo Two-Step Test**
1. Is claim directed to abstract idea, law of nature, or natural phenomenon?
2. If yes, is there "something more" that transforms into patent-eligible application?

**Response Strategies**
- Argue claim not directed to abstract idea
- Identify specific technical improvement
- Point to unconventional steps
- Demonstrate practical application
- Amend to add technical limitations

## After Final Practice

### Options After Final Rejection

| Option | Description | Cost |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Amend | Limited amendments allowed | Filing fee |
| AFCP 2.0 | After Final Consideration Pilot | $0 |
| RCE | Restart examination | $1,360+ |
| Appeal | PTAB review | $800 filing + $2,000 forwarding |
| Abandon | End prosecution | $0 |

### After Final Consideration Pilot (AFCP 2.0)

**Requirements**
- Response to final rejection
- Non-broadening amendment
- Must put application in condition for allowance
- No fee

**Process**
- Examiner gets additional search time
- Interview if appropriate
- May expedite allowance

### Request for Continued Examination (RCE)

**Effect**
- Reopens prosecution
- Resets to non-final status
- Maintains same application number
- Filing date preserved

**Strategic Use**
- When additional amendments needed
- When new evidence to submit
- When claims need significant revision

## Interviews

### Examiner Interviews

**Benefits**
- Clarify issues
- Understand Examiner's position
- Present arguments directly
- Expedite prosecution

**Best Practices**
- Schedule in advance
- Provide agenda
- Prepare proposed amendments
- Discuss allowable subject matter
- Document results

**Types**
- In-person (if at USPTO)
- Video conference
- Telephone

## Appeals

### Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB)

**Appeal Process**
1. Notice of Appeal (2 months from final rejection)
2. Appeal Brief (2 months from notice)
3. Examiner's Answer
4. Reply Brief (optional, 2 months)
5. Oral Hearing (optional)
6. PTAB Decision

### Appeal Brief Requirements

**Required Sections**
- Real party in interest
- Related appeals and interferences
- Status of claims
- Status of amendments
- Summary of claimed subject matter
- Argument (organized by ground of rejection)
- Claims appendix
- Evidence appendix

### PTAB Decision Options

**Board May**
- Affirm rejection
- Reverse rejection
- Enter new ground of rejection
- Remand to Examiner

**After Adverse Decision**
- Request rehearing (within 2 months)
- Reopen prosecution
- Appeal to Federal Circuit

## Continuation Practice

### Continuation Application

**Purpose**
- Pursue additional claims
- Same disclosure
- Must be copending with parent

**Uses**
- Broader claims
- Alternative claim formats
- Different claim focus

### Continuation-in-Part (CIP)

**Purpose**
- Add new matter
- Continue pursuit of original claims
- New matter gets new priority date

**Considerations**
- New matter may affect obviousness
- Disclosure requirements
- Prior art considerations

### Divisional Application

**Purpose**
- Pursue claims restricted out
- Required when restriction requirement made
- Must be copending

**Benefits**
- Priority date preserved
- Same disclosure
- Safe harbor from double patenting

## Double Patenting

### Statutory Double Patenting

**Same Invention Test**
- Claims identical in scope
- Cannot be overcome by terminal disclaimer
- One claim must be canceled

### Obviousness-Type Double Patenting

**One-Way Test**
- Later claims obvious over earlier claims
- Can be overcome by terminal disclaimer

**Terminal Disclaimer**
- Disclaims term beyond earlier patent
- Creates common ownership requirement
- Preserves both patents
