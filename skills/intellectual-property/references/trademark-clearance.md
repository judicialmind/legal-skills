# Trademark Clearance Reference Guide

## Overview

Trademark clearance is the process of determining whether a proposed mark is available for use and registration. This guide covers search methodologies, likelihood of confusion analysis, and TTAB procedures for trademark disputes.

---

## Trademark Fundamentals

### What is a Trademark?

**Definition**
- Word, phrase, symbol, design, or combination
- Identifies and distinguishes source of goods/services
- Indicates consistent quality from single source

**Types of Marks**

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Word Mark | Text only, any style | APPLE |
| Design Mark | Logo or graphic | Apple silhouette |
| Combined Mark | Words and design | APPLE with logo |
| Sound Mark | Distinctive sound | NBC chimes |
| Trade Dress | Product appearance | Coca-Cola bottle shape |

### Trademark Strength Spectrum

**Spectrum of Distinctiveness**

| Category | Description | Protection |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Fanciful | Invented words | Strongest |
| Arbitrary | Real words, unrelated to goods | Strong |
| Suggestive | Suggests qualities, requires thought | Moderate |
| Descriptive | Describes goods/services | Weak (requires secondary meaning) |
| Generic | Common name for goods | None |

**Examples**
- Fanciful: XEROX for copiers
- Arbitrary: APPLE for computers
- Suggestive: NETFLIX for streaming (internet + flicks)
- Descriptive: SHARP for televisions
- Generic: "Computer" for computers

## Clearance Search Methodology

### Types of Searches

**Knockout Search**
- Quick preliminary search
- Federal register only
- Identifies obvious conflicts
- 1-2 day turnaround

**Full Comprehensive Search**
- Federal and state registrations
- Common law uses
- Domain names
- Business names
- International databases

### Search Sources

**Federal Sources**
- USPTO TESS database
- Trademark Electronic Search System
- Live and dead registrations
- Pending applications

**State Sources**
- State trademark registrations
- Secretary of State databases
- All 50 states

**Common Law Sources**
- Business directories
- Trade publications
- Internet search engines
- Social media platforms
- Domain name registries
- Industry databases

### Search Strategies

**Direct Hit Search**
- Exact mark search
- Same spelling
- Same goods/services

**Phonetic Search**
- Sound-alike marks
- Variations in spelling
- Homonyms

**Visual Search**
- Similar design elements
- Design codes (USPTO)
- Logo databases

**Conceptual Search**
- Marks with same meaning
- Foreign language equivalents
- Synonyms

## Likelihood of Confusion Analysis

### DuPont Factors

**In re E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co.** established 13 factors:

1. **Similarity of Marks**
   - Appearance, sound, connotation, commercial impression
   - Consider marks in their entireties
   - Do not dissect

2. **Similarity of Goods/Services**
   - Relatedness of products
   - Same or related channels of trade
   - Complementary products

3. **Similarity of Trade Channels**
   - Same distribution channels
   - Same types of stores
   - Same customer base

4. **Conditions of Purchase**
   - Degree of care by purchasers
   - Price point
   - Sophistication of buyers

5. **Fame of Prior Mark**
   - Commercial strength
   - Recognition by public
   - Advertising expenditures

6. **Number of Similar Marks**
   - Third-party registrations
   - Crowded field
   - Dilution of distinctiveness

7. **Actual Confusion**
   - Evidence of past confusion
   - Misdirected communications
   - Survey evidence

8. **Length of Concurrent Use**
   - Time marks have coexisted
   - Without confusion

9. **Variety of Goods**
   - Likelihood of expansion
   - Natural zone of expansion

10. **Market Interface**
    - Agreements or restrictions
    - Consent agreements

11. **Applicant's Right to Exclude**
    - Extent of exclusive right
    - Breadth of coverage

12. **Potential Confusion**
    - Types of confusion possible
    - Point-of-sale vs. post-sale

13. **Other Facts**
    - Any probative evidence
    - Case-specific factors

### Key DuPont Factors in Practice

**Most Important Factors**
1. Similarity of marks
2. Similarity of goods/services
3. Trade channels
4. Conditions of purchase

**Analysis Approach**
- Not all factors relevant in every case
- No single factor dispositive
- Weigh factors based on evidence

### Mark Comparison Principles

**Compare Marks as Wholes**
- Overall commercial impression
- Not side-by-side dissection
- As encountered in marketplace

**Dominant Portions**
- Some elements more prominent
- Generic/descriptive portions less important
- Focus on distinctive elements

**Sound, Sight, Meaning**
- Phonetic similarity
- Visual similarity
- Connotation and commercial impression

### Goods/Services Analysis

**Identification of Goods**
- As described in registration/application
- Not actual goods sold
- Presumed to cover full scope

**Relatedness Factors**
- Same or complementary
- Common purchasers
- Same trade channels
- Common source assumption

## Search Report Analysis

### Evaluating Conflicts

**Risk Assessment Categories**

| Risk Level | Description | Recommendation |
|------------|-------------|----------------|
| High | Clear conflict likely | Do not adopt |
| Moderate | Potential conflict | Proceed with caution |
| Low | Minor similarity | Generally clear |
| Minimal | No meaningful conflict | Clear to proceed |

### Red Flags

**Strong Indicators of Conflict**
- Identical or near-identical marks
- Same goods/services
- Same trade channels
- Prior mark is famous
- Prior mark is federally registered

**Mitigating Factors**
- Different goods/services
- Different trade channels
- Sophisticated purchasers
- Crowded field
- Concurrent use history

### Opinion Letter Components

**Clearance Opinion Should Include**
1. Search methodology description
2. Potentially conflicting marks identified
3. DuPont factor analysis for key conflicts
4. Risk assessment
5. Recommendation
6. Caveats and limitations

## Filing Considerations

### Bases for Filing

**Use in Commerce (Section 1(a))**
- Mark already in use
- Use in interstate commerce
- Specimen required

**Intent to Use (Section 1(b))**
- Bona fide intent to use
- No current use required
- Statement of Use required before registration

**Foreign Application (Section 44(d))**
- Based on foreign application
- Within 6 months of foreign filing
- Priority date benefit

**Foreign Registration (Section 44(e))**
- Based on foreign registration
- Must be from country of origin

**Madrid Protocol (Section 66(a))**
- International registration designating U.S.
- Through WIPO

### Goods and Services Classification

**Nice Classification System**
- 45 classes (34 goods, 11 services)
- Must specify goods/services in each class
- Filing fee per class

**Identification Requirements**
- Specific and definite
- Cannot be overly broad
- Use USPTO ID Manual language

### Specimens

**Goods Specimens**
- Tags, labels, packaging
- Product displays
- Website showing mark associated with goods

**Services Specimens**
- Advertising showing mark and services
- Website pages
- Brochures describing services

## TTAB Proceedings

### Opposition Proceedings

**Timeline**
- 30-day opposition period after publication
- Extension of time available (90 days total possible)
- Opposition filed within period

**Standing Requirements**
- Belief of damage
- Real interest in proceeding
- Not merely theoretical injury

**Grounds for Opposition**
- Likelihood of confusion
- Merely descriptive
- Deceptively misdescriptive
- Primarily geographic
- Dilution (famous marks)
- False suggestion of connection

### Cancellation Proceedings

**Timing**
- First 5 years: Any ground
- After 5 years: Limited grounds (fraud, genericness, abandonment)

**Grounds for Cancellation**
- Same as opposition
- Plus: Abandonment, genericness, fraud

### TTAB Procedure

**Discovery Phase**
- Written discovery
- Depositions
- Document production
- Limited to relevant issues

**Trial Phase**
- Testimony by declaration or deposition
- No live testimony
- Written briefs
- Oral hearing (optional)

**Timeline**

| Stage | Typical Duration |
|-------|------------------|
| Discovery | 6-8 months |
| Plaintiff's Trial | 1 month |
| Defendant's Trial | 1 month |
| Rebuttal | 15 days |
| Briefing | 2-3 months |
| Decision | 2-6 months |

### Settlement Options

**Consent Agreements**
- Parties agree to coexist
- Define limitations
- Filed with Board

**Coexistence Agreements**
- Geographic limitations
- Trade channel restrictions
- Field of use restrictions

**Assignment**
- Transfer of registration/application
- With or without goodwill

## Concurrent Use Proceedings

### When Available

**Requirements**
- More than one party entitled to use
- Concurrent use without confusion
- Different geographic areas
- Different modes of use

**Outcome**
- Geographically restricted registrations
- Each party has exclusive rights in territory

## Post-Registration Maintenance

### Section 8 Declaration

**Due**
- Between 5th and 6th year after registration
- During 6-month grace period with fee

**Requirements**
- Declaration of continued use
- Specimen of current use
- Fee

### Section 9 Renewal

**Due**
- Every 10 years
- Combined with Section 8 at 10 years

### Section 15 Incontestability

**Requirements**
- 5 years continuous use
- No pending proceeding
- No adverse decision
- File between 5th and 6th year

**Benefits**
- Conclusive evidence of validity
- Right to use
- Ownership
- Registration

**Limitations**
- Still subject to certain defenses
- Fraud
- Abandonment
- Genericness

## Common Law Rights

### Acquisition

**Through Use**
- First to use in commerce
- Geographic scope of actual use
- No registration required

**Scope**
- Limited to geographic area of use
- Limited to goods/services actually offered
- No constructive notice

### Federal Registration Benefits

| Benefit | Common Law | Federal Registration |
|---------|------------|---------------------|
| Geographic Scope | Area of actual use | Nationwide |
| Constructive Notice | No | Yes |
| Incontestability | No | Yes (after 5 years) |
| Federal Court | Based on diversity | Direct jurisdiction |
| Customs Recordation | No | Yes |
| Presumptions | None | Validity, ownership |

## International Considerations

### Madrid Protocol

**Advantages**
- Single application for multiple countries
- Manage through one central registration
- Cost effective for broad protection

**Disadvantages**
- Central attack doctrine (first 5 years)
- Dependent on home registration
- Not available in all countries

### Country-Specific Searches

**Important Markets**
- European Union (EUIPO)
- United Kingdom
- China
- Japan
- Canada
- Australia

**Considerations**
- First-to-file vs. first-to-use
- Translation requirements
- Use requirements
- Local counsel requirements
