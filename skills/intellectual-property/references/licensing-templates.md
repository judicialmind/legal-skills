# IP Licensing Key Terms Reference Guide

## Overview

Intellectual property licensing allows IP owners to monetize their assets while retaining ownership. This guide covers key terms for patent, trademark, copyright, and trade secret licenses, including deal structures, common provisions, and negotiation considerations.

---

## Licensing Fundamentals

### Types of IP Licenses

**By Intellectual Property Type**

| IP Type | What is Licensed | Typical Terms |
|---------|------------------|---------------|
| Patent | Right to make, use, sell invention | Fixed term, royalty-based |
| Trademark | Right to use mark on goods/services | Ongoing, quality control required |
| Copyright | Right to reproduce, distribute, perform | Fixed term or perpetual |
| Trade Secret | Right to use confidential information | Perpetual with confidentiality |
| Software | Right to use, copy, modify code | Perpetual or subscription |

### License Grant Structures

**Exclusive License**
- Licensee has sole right to exploit IP
- Even licensor may be excluded
- Higher royalties justified
- Greater licensee investment expected

**Sole License**
- Only licensee may exploit IP
- Licensor retains right to use
- Not truly exclusive
- Intermediate pricing

**Non-Exclusive License**
- Licensor may grant to multiple licensees
- No exclusivity commitment
- Lower royalties typical
- Broader market coverage

## Grant of Rights Provisions

### Scope of Grant

**Core Grant Language**
```
Licensor hereby grants to Licensee a [exclusive/non-exclusive/sole],
[worldwide/territory-limited], [sublicensable/non-sublicensable],
[transferable/non-transferable] license under the Licensed Patents
to [make, have made, use, sell, offer for sale, and import]
Licensed Products in the Field of Use in the Territory during
the Term.
```

### Field of Use Restrictions

**Purpose**
- Limit license to specific applications
- Allow licensor to license other fields separately
- Protect different market segments

**Examples**
- Medical devices vs. consumer products
- Automotive vs. aerospace
- Enterprise vs. consumer software

**Drafting Considerations**
- Define field precisely
- Anticipate technological evolution
- Address boundary disputes
- Consider complementary vs. competing uses

### Territory Restrictions

**Geographic Scope Options**
- Worldwide
- Specific countries
- Regions (North America, EMEA, APAC)
- Exclusive in some territories, non-exclusive in others

**Considerations**
- Market potential by region
- Licensee's capabilities
- Regulatory requirements
- Manufacturing locations

### Sublicensing Rights

**With Sublicense Rights**
- Licensee can authorize third parties
- Extends licensee's reach
- May dilute control
- Often requires licensor approval

**Without Sublicense Rights**
- Licensee must perform directly
- Greater licensor control
- May limit commercialization
- Common for valuable IP

**Sublicense Provisions**
- Prior approval requirements
- Flow-down of terms
- Audit rights
- Termination upon license termination

## Financial Terms

### Royalty Structures

**Running Royalties**
- Percentage of sales
- Per-unit fee
- Ongoing obligation
- Most common structure

**Typical Royalty Rates by Industry**

| Industry | Patent Royalty Range |
|----------|---------------------|
| Pharmaceuticals | 5-15% net sales |
| Medical Devices | 3-8% net sales |
| Consumer Electronics | 1-5% net sales |
| Software | 10-25% license revenue |
| Industrial Equipment | 2-6% net sales |

**Factors Affecting Royalty Rate**
- Exclusivity
- Field of use breadth
- Territory scope
- Patent strength
- Alternative technologies
- Stage of development
- Licensee capabilities

### Royalty Base Definitions

**Net Sales Definition**
```
"Net Sales" means the gross amount invoiced by Licensee for
Licensed Products sold to third parties, less:
(a) trade, cash, and quantity discounts actually taken;
(b) credits and allowances for returned products;
(c) freight, shipping, and insurance charges;
(d) sales, use, excise, and similar taxes; and
(e) customs duties and similar government charges.
```

**Key Negotiation Points**
- Included deductions
- Intercompany sales treatment
- Bundled products allocation
- Most favored licensee provisions

### Upfront Payments

**Types**
- License fee (non-refundable)
- Option fee
- Signing bonus
- Technology access fee

**Crediting**
- Fully creditable against royalties
- Partially creditable
- Non-creditable
- Cap on credit period

### Milestone Payments

**Development Milestones**
- Prototype completion
- Clinical trial phases
- Regulatory approvals
- Product launch

**Commercial Milestones**
- First commercial sale
- Sales volume thresholds
- Market share targets
- Geographic expansion

**Example Milestone Structure**
| Milestone | Payment |
|-----------|---------|
| Execution | $500,000 |
| Phase II completion | $1,000,000 |
| Phase III completion | $2,500,000 |
| FDA approval | $5,000,000 |
| First commercial sale | $2,000,000 |
| $100M cumulative sales | $5,000,000 |

### Minimum Royalties

**Purpose**
- Ensure minimum compensation
- Incentivize commercialization
- Basis for termination

**Structure**
- Annual minimum amounts
- Escalating over time
- Grace periods
- Cure rights before termination

## Intellectual Property Provisions

### Ownership of Improvements

**Licensor-Owned Model**
- All improvements owned by licensor
- Licensee assigns improvements
- Licensee may receive license back
- Favors licensor

**Licensee-Owned Model**
- Licensee owns its improvements
- May or may not license to licensor
- Licensee retains R&D value
- Favors licensee

**Joint Ownership Model**
- Improvements owned jointly
- Each party can use freely
- Complications in enforcement
- Often avoided

**Allocation Approaches**
- By inventorship
- By funding source
- By subject matter
- By level of improvement

### Patent Prosecution and Maintenance

**Licensor Responsibilities**
- File and prosecute applications
- Maintain existing patents
- Inform licensee of status
- Consider licensee input

**Licensee Responsibilities**
- Pay prosecution/maintenance costs (often)
- Provide technical assistance
- Review and comment on filings
- Assist with foreign filings

**Step-In Rights**
- If licensor abandons prosecution
- Licensee may continue at own expense
- Ownership questions addressed
- Royalty adjustment may apply

### Infringement and Enforcement

**Third-Party Infringement**

| Issue | Exclusive License | Non-Exclusive License |
|-------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Notice Obligation | Both parties | Both parties |
| Right to Sue | Licensee (usually) | Licensor only |
| Cost Allocation | Negotiated | Licensor bears |
| Recovery Sharing | Negotiated | To licensor |

**Enforcement Provisions**
- Notification requirements
- Consultation before action
- Cost sharing arrangements
- Settlement approval rights
- Recovery allocation

**Third-Party Claims Against Licensee**
- Indemnification by licensor
- Scope limitations
- Caps and deductibles
- Insurance requirements

## Term and Termination

### License Term

**Term Options**
- Fixed term (5, 10, 20 years)
- Patent life (until expiration)
- Perpetual
- Renewable with conditions

**Renewal Provisions**
- Automatic vs. mutual consent
- Renewal fee
- Performance conditions
- Notice requirements

### Termination Rights

**Termination for Breach**
```
Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice
if the other party materially breaches any provision and fails
to cure such breach within [30-60] days after written notice
specifying the breach.
```

**Termination for Convenience**
- With or without cause
- Notice period (90-180 days typical)
- Wind-down provisions
- May require payment

**Termination for Insolvency**
- Bankruptcy filing
- Assignment for creditors
- Receivership
- Insolvency
- Consider Section 365(n) protections

**Termination for Patent Challenge**
- Licensor may terminate if licensee challenges patent validity
- Increasingly scrutinized
- May be unenforceable in some contexts
- Consider carve-outs

### Post-Termination Obligations

**Licensee Obligations**
- Cease manufacture
- Sell-off period for inventory
- Return confidential information
- Assign sublicenses (or terminate)
- Final royalty payment

**Survival Provisions**
- Confidentiality (perpetual or long-term)
- Indemnification
- Audit rights (limited period)
- Accrued payment obligations

## Quality Control (Trademarks)

### Requirement for Quality Control

**Legal Basis**
- Failure to control = naked license
- Abandonment of trademark rights
- Essential for valid trademark license

**Quality Standards**
```
Licensee shall maintain the quality of all goods and services
offered under the Licensed Marks at a level at least equal to
the quality maintained by Licensor as of the Effective Date.
Licensor shall have the right to inspect and test such goods
and services to verify compliance.
```

### Quality Control Mechanisms

**Approval Rights**
- New products
- Packaging and labeling
- Advertising and marketing materials
- Distribution channels

**Inspection Rights**
- Facility inspections
- Product sampling
- Quality audits
- Records review

**Compliance**
- Quality manuals
- Specifications
- Training requirements
- Reporting obligations

## Confidentiality

### Confidentiality Obligations

**Standard Provisions**
- Definition of confidential information
- Exceptions (public, prior knowledge, independent development)
- Use restrictions
- Disclosure restrictions
- Employee obligations
- Return or destruction

**Trade Secret Licenses**
- Enhanced confidentiality critical
- Longer duration (perpetual)
- Specific security measures
- Limited personnel access
- No reverse engineering

### Information Exchange

**Know-How Transfer**
- Training programs
- Technical documentation
- Ongoing technical support
- Personnel visits
- Documentation requirements

## Reporting and Audit

### Royalty Reports

**Standard Requirements**
- Quarterly reports
- Sales by product and territory
- Calculation of royalties due
- Deductions itemized
- Certification by officer

**Report Contents**
```
Each report shall include:
(a) number of Licensed Products sold;
(b) gross revenues by product and territory;
(c) itemized deductions from gross revenues;
(d) Net Sales calculation;
(e) royalties due; and
(f) exchange rates used.
```

### Audit Rights

**Standard Audit Provision**
- Independent auditor access
- During normal business hours
- Upon reasonable notice
- Limited frequency (annual)
- Confidentiality of audit results

**Underpayment Consequences**
- Payment of deficiency plus interest
- If underpayment exceeds threshold (5-10%), licensee pays audit costs
- Multiple underpayments may be breach

## Representations and Warranties

### Licensor Representations

**Typical Representations**
- Owns or controls licensed IP
- Right to grant license
- No conflicting agreements
- No known infringement
- Patents are valid (limited)

**Warranty Limitations**
```
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, LICENSOR MAKES NO
REPRESENTATIONS OR WARRANTIES OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY,
FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.
```

### Licensee Representations

**Typical Representations**
- Authority to enter agreement
- Capability to perform
- Compliance with laws
- No conflicting agreements

## Indemnification

### Licensor Indemnification

**Typical Scope**
- Third-party IP infringement claims
- Breach of representations
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct

**Limitations**
- Knowledge limitations
- Modification exclusions
- Combination exclusions
- Cap on liability

### Licensee Indemnification

**Typical Scope**
- Product liability claims
- Regulatory violations
- Breach of agreement
- Unauthorized use

## Special License Types

### Research License

**Characteristics**
- Limited to research purposes
- No commercial rights
- Often royalty-free
- Time-limited
- Option for commercial license

### Evaluation License

**Characteristics**
- Testing and evaluation only
- Short term (30-90 days)
- Confidential results
- Path to commercial license
- Option fee may apply

### Cross-License

**Characteristics**
- Each party licenses to other
- May be royalty-free or with balancing payment
- Freedom to operate
- Often between competitors
- Field restrictions common

### Standards-Essential Patent License

**Characteristics**
- FRAND terms (fair, reasonable, non-discriminatory)
- Non-exclusive
- Worldwide
- Royalty stacking considerations
- Industry-specific rates
