# Contract Negotiation Playbook Template

## Overview

A contract negotiation playbook establishes standardized positions, fallback alternatives, and approval requirements for recurring contract types. This template provides the framework for creating playbooks that enable consistent, efficient negotiations while managing legal and business risk.

---

## Section 1: Playbook Structure

### 1.1 Document Organization

```
PLAYBOOK: [Contract Type]
├── Executive Summary
├── Scope and Applicability
├── Standard Terms (Preferred Position)
├── Acceptable Alternatives (Fallback Positions)
├── Unacceptable Terms (Red Lines)
├── Approval Matrix
├── Negotiation Guidance
├── Issue Escalation Procedures
└── Appendices (Sample Language, Decision Trees)
```

### 1.2 Version Control
- Version number and effective date
- Change log with revision history
- Author and approver identification
- Review cycle (annual minimum)
- Distribution list

## Section 3: Standard Terms (Preferred Position)

### 3.1 Commercial Terms

#### Payment Terms
**Standard Position**: Net 30 days from invoice date
**Rationale**: Balances cash flow management with vendor relationships
**Supporting Language**:
> Payment shall be due thirty (30) days following receipt of a valid invoice. Invoices must include purchase order number, description of deliverables, and itemized charges.

#### Pricing
**Standard Position**: Fixed pricing for term of agreement
**Rationale**: Budget predictability, protection against mid-term increases
**Supporting Language**:
> Prices set forth in this Agreement shall remain fixed for the Initial Term. Any price adjustments for Renewal Terms shall not exceed [X]% annually and require [90] days' prior written notice.

#### Term and Renewal
**Standard Position**: Annual term with mutual renewal option
**Rationale**: Flexibility to reassess relationship; avoid long-term lock-in
**Supporting Language**:
> This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of one (1) year ("Initial Term"). Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods ("Renewal Terms") unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

### 3.2 Liability Terms

#### Limitation of Liability
**Standard Position**: Cap at greater of (a) fees paid in prior 12 months or (b) $[X]
**Rationale**: Proportional risk allocation based on contract value
**Supporting Language**:
> EXCEPT FOR A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, GROSS NEGLIGENCE, WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM, OR (B) [AMOUNT].

#### Exclusion of Consequential Damages
**Standard Position**: Mutual exclusion with carve-outs
**Rationale**: Protect against unpredictable, disproportionate damages
**Supporting Language**:
> IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOST DATA, REGARDLESS OF THE FORM OF ACTION OR THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

**Standard Carve-Outs**:
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Breach of confidentiality obligations
- Infringement of intellectual property rights
- Indemnification obligations
- Violation of applicable law

#### Indemnification
**Standard Position**: Mutual indemnification for core risks
**Rationale**: Allocate responsibility to party best positioned to control risk
**Supporting Language**:
> Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Customer from and against any Claims arising from: (a) Vendor's breach of any representation, warranty, or obligation; (b) Vendor's negligence or willful misconduct; (c) infringement of any third-party intellectual property rights by Vendor's deliverables; (d) violation of applicable law by Vendor.

### 3.3 Intellectual Property Terms

#### Ownership of Deliverables
**Standard Position**: Customer owns all work product created specifically for Customer
**Rationale**: Investment in custom development should yield ownership
**Supporting Language**:
> All Deliverables created by Vendor specifically for Customer under this Agreement shall be deemed "works made for hire" and shall be owned exclusively by Customer. To the extent any Deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire, Vendor hereby irrevocably assigns to Customer all right, title, and interest in and to such Deliverable.

#### License to Pre-Existing IP
**Standard Position**: Perpetual, royalty-free license to vendor's pre-existing materials incorporated in deliverables
**Supporting Language**:
> Vendor grants Customer a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use, modify, and create derivative works from any Pre-Existing Materials incorporated into the Deliverables, solely in connection with Customer's use of the Deliverables.

### 3.4 Data Protection Terms

#### Confidentiality
**Standard Position**: Mutual confidentiality with standard exceptions
**Duration**: 3 years post-termination (5 years for trade secrets)
**Supporting Language**:
> Each party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other party's Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care. Confidential Information shall not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach; (b) was rightfully known prior to disclosure; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information.

#### Data Processing
**Standard Position**: Vendor processes Customer data solely as directed; Customer retains ownership
**Supporting Language**:
> Vendor shall process Customer Data solely in accordance with Customer's documented instructions and for no other purpose. Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to Customer Data. Vendor acquires no rights in Customer Data except the limited rights expressly granted in this Agreement.

### 3.5 Operational Terms

#### Service Levels
**Standard Position**: Measurable SLAs with credits for non-performance
**Supporting Language**:
> Vendor shall provide the Services in accordance with the Service Level Agreement attached as Exhibit [X]. Failure to meet Service Levels shall entitle Customer to the Service Credits specified therein. Service Credits shall be Customer's sole remedy for failure to meet Service Levels (except for persistent, material failures constituting breach).

#### Termination for Convenience
**Standard Position**: Either party may terminate with 90 days' notice
**Rationale**: Exit flexibility; avoid perpetual obligations
**Supporting Language**:
> Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other party. Upon termination for convenience by Customer, Customer shall pay for Services performed through the effective date of termination.

#### Termination for Cause
**Standard Position**: Immediate termination for material breach after cure period
**Supporting Language**:
> Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice; (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed; or (c) ceases to conduct business in the normal course.

## Section 4: Fallback Positions

### 4.1 Fallback Position Framework

For each standard term, define:
1. **Preferred Position**: Ideal term (Section 3)
2. **First Fallback**: Minor concession, minimal risk increase
3. **Second Fallback**: Moderate concession, acceptable risk
4. **Final Position**: Maximum concession before escalation
5. **Red Line**: Non-negotiable; reject or escalate

### 4.2 Example: Limitation of Liability Fallback Ladder

| Position | Cap Amount | Approval Level |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Standard | Greater of 12-month fees or $500K | No approval needed |
| Fallback 1 | 12-month fees (no floor) | Contract Manager |
| Fallback 2 | 24-month fees | Senior Counsel |
| Fallback 3 | 36-month fees | General Counsel |
| Red Line | Unlimited liability | Reject |

### 4.3 Example: Indemnification Fallback Ladder

| Position | Scope | Approval Level |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Standard | Mutual indemnification | No approval needed |
| Fallback 1 | Enhanced vendor indemnification + Customer indemnification for its gross negligence | Contract Manager |
| Fallback 2 | One-way vendor indemnification (Customer provides none) | Senior Counsel |
| Fallback 3 | Limited one-way (cap at liability limit) | General Counsel |
| Red Line | Unlimited one-way indemnification by Customer | Reject |

### 4.4 Example: Governing Law Fallback Ladder

| Position | Jurisdiction | Approval Level |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Standard | Customer's home state (e.g., Delaware) | No approval needed |
| Fallback 1 | New York | Contract Manager |
| Fallback 2 | Neutral state (e.g., Illinois) | Senior Counsel |
| Fallback 3 | Vendor's home state (within US) | General Counsel |
| Red Line | Foreign jurisdiction | Escalate to business |

### 5.1 Authority Levels

| Level | Title | Maximum Contract Value | Term Authority |
|-------|-------|----------------------|----------------|
| 1 | Contract Specialist | $50,000 | Standard terms only |
| 2 | Contract Manager | $250,000 | First fallback positions |
| 3 | Senior Contract Manager | $500,000 | Second fallback positions |
| 4 | Associate General Counsel | $2,000,000 | Final positions |
| 5 | General Counsel | $5,000,000 | Red line exceptions |

### 5.2 Deviation Approval Requirements

| Deviation Type | Required Approvals |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Price above budget | Finance + Legal at appropriate level |
| Extended payment terms (>Net 45) | Finance + Contract Manager |
| Multi-year commitment (>3 years) | Finance + Senior Counsel |
| Unlimited liability | General Counsel + CFO |
| Broad indemnification | General Counsel |
| Foreign governing law | General Counsel |
| Assignment restrictions waived | Senior Counsel |
| Audit rights waived | Internal Audit + Senior Counsel |

## Section 6: Standard vs. Non-Standard Terms

### 6.1 Standard Terms (Pre-Approved)

Terms that may be accepted without additional approval:
- Mutual confidentiality (standard duration)
- Standard limitation of liability
- Mutual indemnification for breach
- Annual term with 60-day renewal notice
- Net 30 payment terms
- Customer ownership of custom deliverables
- Standard termination for convenience (90 days)
- Delaware or New York governing law
- Standard force majeure

### 6.2 Non-Standard Terms (Approval Required)

Terms requiring case-by-case review:
- Extended liability caps
- Uncapped indemnification
- One-way indemnification
- Auto-renewal without notice
- Payment terms beyond Net 45
- Prepayment requirements
- Minimum purchase commitments
- Exclusive dealing arrangements
- Non-solicitation provisions
- Liquidated damages
- Personal guarantees
- Cross-default provisions

### 6.3 Prohibited Terms (Red Lines)

Terms that must be rejected or require executive approval:
- Unlimited liability
- Waiver of jury trial (in certain jurisdictions)
- Mandatory arbitration with unfavorable rules
- Confession of judgment
- Waiver of consequential damages exclusion (by Customer)
- Assignment of all IP without limitation
- Perpetual license grants to Customer data
- Audit rights extending to privileged materials
- Unilateral amendment rights
- Automatic renewal with price increases
- Choice of law in high-risk jurisdictions

## Section 7: Negotiation Guidance

### 7.1 Opening Position Strategy
- Begin with standard terms (full preferred position)
- Present terms as "standard" and "market"
- Avoid conceding in first round
- Identify counterparty's priorities early

### 7.2 Quid Pro Quo Framework
- Never make unilateral concessions
- Document value given and received
- Package related issues together
- Reserve high-value concessions for closers

### 7.3 Issue Prioritization

**Must-Have** (Non-negotiable):
- Limitation of liability cap
- IP ownership for custom work
- Termination for cause rights
- Confidentiality protections
- Compliance with law

**Should-Have** (Strong preference):
- Termination for convenience
- Favorable governing law
- Service level credits
- Audit rights
- Insurance requirements

**Nice-to-Have** (Tradeable):
- Payment term extensions
- Renewal notice period
- Specific performance metrics
- Reporting frequency

### 7.4 Common Counterparty Arguments and Responses

**Counterparty**: "This is our standard agreement."
**Response**: "We have our own standards based on legal and business requirements. Let's discuss which provisions need alignment."

**Counterparty**: "We can't change our liability cap."
**Response**: "Our risk exposure requires appropriate protection. We can discuss mutual caps or specific carve-outs that address both parties' concerns."

**Counterparty**: "All our other customers accept this."
**Response**: "Each customer has unique requirements. Let's focus on what works for our specific relationship and risk profile."

## Section 8: Escalation Procedures

### 8.1 When to Escalate
- Red line term requested
- Authority limit exceeded
- Business pressure to accept unfavorable terms
- Legal or regulatory risk identified
- Novel issue not covered by playbook
- Strategic relationship considerations

### 8.2 Escalation Process
1. Document the issue and positions
2. Prepare risk analysis
3. Identify decision-maker per approval matrix
4. Present options with recommendations
5. Document decision and rationale
6. Update playbook if precedent set

### 8.3 Escalation Documentation Template
```
ESCALATION REQUEST

Contract: [Name/Number]
Counterparty: [Name]
Value: [Amount]
Requestor: [Name]
Date: [Date]

[Description of non-standard term or position]

[Per playbook]

[Legal, financial, operational risks]

OPTIONS:
1. [Option with pros/cons]
2. [Option with pros/cons]
3. [Option with pros/cons]

RECOMMENDATION:
[Recommended path forward]

REQUIRED APPROVAL LEVEL:
[Per approval matrix]
```
