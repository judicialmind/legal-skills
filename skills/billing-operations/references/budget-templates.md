# Legal Budget Templates

## Overview

Effective legal budgeting requires phase-based planning, regular tracking, and transparent communication. This guide provides templates and frameworks for various matter types and fee arrangements.

## Phase-Based Litigation Budget

### Standard Phases

| Phase | Description | Typical % of Total |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1. Case Assessment | Initial evaluation, strategy | 5-10% |
| 2. Pleadings | Complaint/Answer, early motions | 10-15% |
| 3. Discovery | Written, depositions, experts | 35-45% |
| 4. Motions Practice | Dispositive motions | 10-15% |
| 5. Trial Preparation | Witnesses, exhibits, briefs | 15-20% |
| 6. Trial | Attendance, daily prep | 10-15% |
| 7. Post-Trial/Appeal | Motions, appeal if needed | 5-10% |

## Alternative Fee Arrangements (AFAs)

### Fixed Fee Arrangements

#### Employment Matter - Fixed Fee

```
ENGAGEMENT: Employment Discrimination Defense
FIXED FEE: $75,000

Scope Includes:
- Initial assessment and strategy
- Answer and early motions
- Written discovery (3 sets propounded, 3 responded)
- Up to 6 depositions (4 defense, 2 plaintiff)
- Motion for summary judgment
- Mediation preparation and attendance

Excluded (Hourly at Standard Rates):
- Additional depositions beyond scope
- Appeals
- Trial (separate fee to be negotiated)

Payment Schedule:
- $25,000 upon engagement
- $25,000 at close of discovery
- $25,000 upon MSJ filing
```

#### Transaction - Fixed Fee

```
ENGAGEMENT: Asset Purchase Agreement
FIXED FEE: $125,000

Phase 1 - Due Diligence ($40,000)
- Legal due diligence review
- Issue identification memo
- Risk assessment matrix

Phase 2 - Documentation ($60,000)
- APA drafting and negotiation
- Schedules and exhibits
- Ancillary documents
- Closing checklist

Phase 3 - Closing ($25,000)
- Closing coordination
- Document execution
- Post-closing items

Excluded:
- Regulatory filings (separate quote)
- Financing documents
- Litigation of purchase price disputes
```

### Capped Fee Arrangements

```
ENGAGEMENT: IP Portfolio Audit
FEE CAP: $50,000

Hourly Rates Apply:
- Partner: $550/hour
- Associate: $350/hour
- Paralegal: $175/hour

Terms:
- Fees billed hourly up to cap
- Cap applies to fees only, not expenses
- Scope defined in Exhibit A
- Scope changes require cap adjustment
- Monthly billing with running total
```

### Success Fee / Contingency Hybrid

```
ENGAGEMENT: Contract Dispute
BASE FEE: Reduced hourly (50% of standard)
SUCCESS FEE: 15% of recovery above $500,000

Standard Rates:         Reduced Rates:
Partner $600           Partner $300
Associate $400         Associate $200

Success Fee Calculation:
- Recovery $750,000
- Threshold $500,000
- Success Fee Basis: $250,000
- Success Fee (15%): $37,500
```

### Blended Rate Arrangements

```
ENGAGEMENT: General Litigation Support
BLENDED RATE: $425/hour (all timekeepers)

Standard Rate Comparison:
Partner: $650 → $425 (35% discount)
Senior Associate: $500 → $425 (15% discount)
Junior Associate: $350 → $425 (premium)

Volume Commitment: 500+ hours/year
Rate applies to all timekeepers regardless of level
Encourages senior attorney involvement
```

## Budget Tracking Template

```
------------------------------------------------------------

Total Budget:      $315,618

Remaining:         $144,968
Projected at Rate: $341,300 (108% of budget)

Variance Explanations:
- Document review running under budget (TAR efficiency)
- Expert fees below estimate (fewer experts needed)
- Depositions on track

Forecast Adjustments:
- Additional 2 depositions anticipated
- Expert rebuttal report needed
- Recommend increasing Phase 3 budget by $15,000
```

## Budget Negotiation Framework

### Client Concerns and Responses

| Client Concern | Response Strategy |
|----------------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Offer phased approach, scope reduction |
| "Unpredictable" | Propose cap or collar arrangement |
| "Want skin in game" | Hybrid with success component |
| "Fixed fee only" | Define scope carefully with exclusions |
| "Need predictability" | Monthly/quarterly caps with true-up |

### Scope Definition Checklist

- [ ] Jurisdictions covered
- [ ] Number of parties
- [ ] Document volume estimate
- [ ] Deposition count limits
- [ ] Expert limitations
- [ ] Motion practice included
- [ ] Trial included/excluded
- [ ] Appeal included/excluded
- [ ] Third-party subpoenas
- [ ] Regulatory agency interaction

## Budget Communication Best Practices

1. **Set expectations early** - Provide budget with engagement letter
2. **Report monthly** - Variance reports with explanations
3. **Flag issues promptly** - Don't wait for overruns
4. **Document scope changes** - Written approval for additions
5. **Quarterly reviews** - In-person budget discussions
6. **Post-matter analysis** - Compare actual to budget for learning
