# Bankruptcy Claims Reference Guide

## Overview

A claim in bankruptcy is a right to payment or right to an equitable remedy. Understanding claims procedures, priorities, and treatment is essential for creditors, debtors, and their counsel. This guide covers proof of claim requirements, claim categories, priority rules, and objection procedures.

---

## Filing Proofs of Claim

### Requirement to File

**When Filing Required**
- Chapter 7: Required for any distribution
- Chapter 11: Scheduled claims deemed filed if not disputed
- Chapter 12: Required for distributions
- Chapter 13: Required for any distribution

**Scheduled Claims**
- Debtor's schedules may list claims
- Scheduled claims not listed as "disputed, contingent, or unliquidated" are deemed allowed
- Creditor should still file to ensure accuracy

### Proof of Claim Form

**Official Form 410**

**Required Information**
- Creditor name and address
- Basis for claim
- Amount of claim
- Whether claim includes interest or charges
- Whether claim is secured, unsecured priority, or unsecured nonpriority
- Supporting documentation

### Deadlines (Bar Dates)

**Chapter 7 and 11**
- Governmental units: 180 days after order for relief
- Other creditors: Set by court (typically 70-90 days after meeting of creditors)

**Chapter 12 and 13**
- Governmental units: 180 days after order for relief
- Other creditors: 70 days after order for relief

**Late Filed Claims**
- Generally disallowed unless excusable neglect shown
- Pioneer Investment factors (reasonableness, delay, good faith)
- Shorter deadline in Chapter 13 cases

### Supporting Documentation

**Required Attachments**
- Copy of written instrument (contract, note, invoice)
- Itemization of interest, fees, and charges
- Evidence of perfection (for secured claims)
- Account statements or ledgers

## Categories of Claims

### Secured Claims (11 U.S.C. Section 506)

**Definition**
- Claim secured by lien on property of estate
- Value of claim cannot exceed value of collateral

**Bifurcation**
- Secured portion: Value of collateral
- Unsecured portion: Amount exceeding collateral value

**Section 506(a) Valuation**
- Determined by court
- Replacement value standard (Associates Commercial Corp. v. Rash)
- Purpose of valuation matters

**Types of Liens**
- Consensual liens (mortgages, security interests)
- Judicial liens (judgment liens)
- Statutory liens (mechanic's liens, tax liens)

### Priority Claims (11 U.S.C. Section 507)

**Order of Priority**

| Priority | Claim Type | Limit |
|----------|------------|-------|
| 1 | Domestic support obligations | No limit |
| 2 | Administrative expenses | No limit |
| 3 | Gap claims (involuntary cases) | No limit |
| 4 | Employee wages | $15,150* per employee |
| 5 | Employee benefit plans | $15,150* per employee |
| 6 | Grain farmers/fishermen | $7,575* |
| 7 | Consumer deposits | $3,350* per individual |
| 8 | Tax claims | No limit (varies by type) |
| 9 | FDIC claims | No limit |
| 10 | Death/personal injury from DUI | No limit |

*Amounts adjusted periodically for inflation

### Administrative Expense Claims (11 U.S.C. Section 503)

**Qualifying Expenses**
- Actual, necessary costs of preserving estate
- Wages and salaries for services post-petition
- Professional fees (attorneys, accountants)
- Taxes incurred by estate
- Lease obligations post-rejection grace period

**503(b)(9) Claims**
- Goods received by debtor within 20 days before filing
- Sold in ordinary course of business
- Administrative priority even if pre-petition

**Request Procedure**
- File application or motion
- Chapter 11: Often filed at confirmation
- Court approval required

### Unsecured Nonpriority Claims

**General Unsecured Claims**
- No collateral security
- No statutory priority
- Paid pro rata after secured and priority claims
- Often receive partial payment or nothing

## Special Claim Types

### Contingent Claims

**Definition**
- Debtor's liability depends on future uncertain event
- Example: Guarantee not yet called

**Treatment**
- Allowable in bankruptcy
- May be estimated for distribution purposes
- Holder must file proof of claim

### Unliquidated Claims

**Definition**
- Liability exists but amount not determined
- Example: Pending lawsuit

**Treatment**
- File proof of claim
- Court may estimate amount (11 U.S.C. Section 502(c))
- May delay distribution if estimation needed

### Disputed Claims

**Definition**
- Debtor or party in interest contests validity or amount

**Treatment**
- Claim objection filed
- Hearing to determine allowance
- May require reserve in distributions

### Interest and Fees

**Pre-Petition Interest**
- Generally allowable as part of claim
- Include in proof of claim

**Post-Petition Interest**
- Unsecured claims: Generally not allowed
- Secured claims: Allowed if collateral value exceeds claim
- Priority claims: Generally not allowed

**Fees and Costs**
- Pre-petition: Allowable if contractually authorized
- Post-petition: May be allowable for oversecured claims

## Claims Allowance and Objection

### Allowance of Claims

**Deemed Allowed**
- Proof of claim filed constitutes prima facie evidence
- Claim allowed unless objection filed
- Burden shifts to objector after proper filing

### Grounds for Objection (11 U.S.C. Section 502(b))

| Ground | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Unenforceable | Unenforceable against debtor under applicable law |
| Unmatured Interest | Claim for unmatured interest |
| Tax Penalty | Non-compensatory tax penalty |
| Insider/Attorney Fees | Excessive fees for insider services |
| Unmatured Alimony | Unmatured domestic support obligation |
| Landlord Cap | Capped at 1 year rent for lease rejection |
| Employment Damages | Capped for terminated employment contract |

### Objection Procedures

**Filing Objection**
- Debtor, trustee, or party in interest may object
- Written objection required
- Serve on claimant with notice of hearing
- 30 days notice typically required (check local rules)

**Evidentiary Burdens**
1. Properly filed proof of claim = prima facie validity
2. Objector must produce evidence rebutting claim
3. If rebutted, claimant bears ultimate burden of proof

**Common Objections**
- No basis for claim
- Incorrect amount
- Mischaracterization of priority
- Time-barred claim
- Previously discharged
- Duplicate claim

### Estimation of Claims

**When Used**
- Unliquidated or contingent claims
- Would unduly delay case administration
- For voting purposes or distribution reserves

**Procedure**
- Motion to estimate
- Court determines appropriate method
- Not binding for other purposes (except voting/distribution)

## Secured Claim Issues

### Perfection Requirements

**UCC Article 9 Security Interests**
- Must be properly perfected pre-petition
- Unperfected interests may be avoided
- Trustee's strong-arm power (11 U.S.C. Section 544)

**Real Property Liens**
- Must be recorded pre-petition
- Check state recording requirements
- Race-notice vs. pure race jurisdictions

### Adequate Protection

**When Required**
- Stay relief requests
- Cash collateral use
- Section 363 sales

**Forms of Adequate Protection**
- Cash payments
- Additional liens
- Replacement liens
- Other relief resulting in indubitable equivalent

### Relief from Stay

**Grounds (11 U.S.C. Section 362(d))**
1. For cause, including lack of adequate protection
2. No equity in property AND not necessary for reorganization
3. Single asset real estate special rules
4. Vehicle/personal property in Chapter 13

## Priority Claim Details

### Domestic Support Obligations (Section 507(a)(1))

**Definition**
- Debt owed to spouse, former spouse, child, guardian
- Nature of alimony, maintenance, or support
- Established by court order or agreement

**Treatment**
- First priority
- Not dischargeable
- Trustee may pursue collection

### Administrative Expenses (Section 507(a)(2))

**Post-Petition Obligations**
- Actual, necessary costs of preserving estate
- Trustee and professional fees
- Post-petition lease obligations

**Request Process**
- File administrative expense application
- Court must approve
- Typically paid at confirmation or closing

### Tax Claims (Section 507(a)(8))

**Priority Taxes**
- Income taxes for years ending within 3 years of filing
- Property taxes within 1 year of filing
- Withholding taxes
- Employment taxes
- Excise taxes

**Non-Priority Tax Claims**
- Taxes outside lookback period
- Penalties (except compensatory)
- Dischargeable in some cases

## Claims in Different Chapters

### Chapter 7

**Treatment of Claims**
- Trustee liquidates assets
- Distributes proceeds per priority
- Secured claims: Adequate protection or relief from stay
- Unsecured claims: Pro rata from remaining assets

**No Distribution Cases**
- Many Chapter 7 cases are "no asset"
- No claims process activated
- Notice sent if assets discovered

### Chapter 11

**Claims Treatment in Plan**
- Secured claims: Retain lien, receive present value
- Priority claims: Full payment (unless class agrees)
- Unsecured claims: Plan proposes treatment
- Equity: Last in priority

**Voting on Plan**
- Class accepts if majority in number AND two-thirds in amount
- Impaired classes vote
- Cramdown if not all classes accept

### Chapter 13

**Treatment**
- Priority claims: Full payment through plan
- Secured claims: Retain lien, receive present value
- Unsecured claims: Best interests test and projected disposable income

**Special Rules**
- 910-day rule for vehicle purchase money security interests
- Anti-modification for principal residence mortgages
- Cure of mortgage arrears allowed

## Claim Transfers

### Assignment of Claims

**Requirements**
- File evidence of transfer with court
- Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 3001(e)
- Court may require documentation

**Procedure**
- Notice to debtor and trustee
- 21-day objection period
- Court enters order if no objection

### Claims Trading

**Chapter 11 Context**
- Active market in large cases
- Buyers may acquire claims for plan voting control
- Valuation issues

**Disclosure Requirements**
- Rule 2019 disclosures for committees and groups
- Identify members and claims
- Court may require additional disclosure

---

## Reconsideration of Claims

### Motion to Reconsider (11 U.S.C. Section 502(j))

**Grounds**
- Newly discovered evidence
- Fraud
- Legal error
- Changed circumstances

**Timing**
- Before case closes
- Court discretion
- Balance finality with justice

## Claims Checklist

### For Creditors

- [ ] Determine if proof of claim required
- [ ] Gather supporting documentation
- [ ] Calculate claim amount accurately
- [ ] Identify claim category (secured, priority, unsecured)
- [ ] File proof of claim before bar date
- [ ] Attach required documentation
- [ ] Monitor case for objections
- [ ] Respond to any objection timely
- [ ] Track distributions

### For Debtors/Trustees

- [ ] Review schedules for accuracy
- [ ] Monitor claims register
- [ ] Review proofs of claim for errors
- [ ] File objections where appropriate
- [ ] Estimate contingent/unliquidated claims
- [ ] Calculate distributions by priority
- [ ] Reserve for disputed claims
- [ ] Process allowed claims for distribution

### For Claims Objections

- [ ] Identify specific grounds for objection
- [ ] Gather evidence supporting objection
- [ ] Draft objection with specificity
- [ ] Serve on claimant properly
- [ ] Provide adequate notice of hearing
- [ ] Prepare for evidentiary hearing
- [ ] Propose order for court
