# Due Diligence Report Templates

## Overview

This document provides standardized templates for due diligence reports used in M&A transactions. These templates ensure consistent, comprehensive reporting that facilitates informed decision-making by deal teams, management, and boards of directors.

---

## 3. Issue Tracking Log

### 3.1 Purpose
Tracks all identified issues from initial identification through resolution, serving as the working document for the due diligence team.

### 3.2 Template (Spreadsheet Format)

```
COLUMNS:

A. Issue ID          - Unique identifier (e.g., IP-015)
B. Category          - Functional area (Corporate, IP, Employment, etc.)
C. Subcategory       - Specific topic within category
D. Date Identified   - When issue was first identified
E. Summary           - Brief description of issue
F. Detailed Description - Full explanation of issue
G. Source            - Document/interview where discovered
H. Risk Level        - GREEN/YELLOW/ORANGE/RED
I. Likelihood        - Probability of adverse outcome (1-5)
J. Impact            - Severity if issue materializes (1-5)
K. Risk Score        - Likelihood x Impact
L. Financial Exposure Low  - Lower bound estimate
M. Financial Exposure High - Upper bound estimate
N. Owner             - Team member responsible
O. Status            - Open/In Progress/Resolved/Accepted
P. Resolution Action - Required steps to resolve
Q. Target Date       - Date for resolution
R. Actual Resolution Date - When resolved
S. Resolution Notes  - How issue was resolved
T. PA Implication    - Impact on purchase agreement
U. Disclosure Schedule - Which schedule affected
V. Priority          - 1 (Highest) to 5 (Lowest)
```

## 4. Deal-Breaker Identification Framework

### 4.1 Definition
A deal-breaker is an issue that, if not satisfactorily resolved, should prevent closing of the transaction.

### 4.2 Categories of Deal-Breakers

**Category A: Fundamental Business Risks**
- Core technology owned by third party
- Key customer contracts non-assignable and material
- Regulatory prohibition on transaction
- Target lacks rights to conduct core business
- Undisclosed competing claims to ownership

**Category B: Material Undisclosed Liabilities**
- Environmental contamination requiring remediation exceeding [X]% of purchase price
- Pending litigation with realistic exposure exceeding [X]% of purchase price
- Tax liabilities exceeding disclosed amounts by material percentage
- Undisclosed debt or obligations materially impacting value

**Category C: Fundamental Misrepresentations**
- Financial statements materially misstated
- Core representations demonstrably false
- Intentional concealment of material facts
- Fraud or illegality in target's operations

**Category D: Integration Impossibility**
- Technology incompatibility preventing integration
- Regulatory barriers to combined operations
- Key employee/management retention impossible
- Customer consent requirements cannot be satisfied

## 5. Risk Categorization System

### 5.1 Risk Categories

**Financial Risks**
- Working capital deficiency
- Revenue quality issues
- EBITDA adjustments
- Accounts receivable collectability
- Inventory obsolescence
- Unrecorded liabilities
- Off-balance sheet obligations

**Legal Risks**
- Contractual breaches
- Regulatory violations
- Litigation exposure
- IP infringement claims
- Employment claims
- Environmental liability

**Operational Risks**
- Key person dependency
- Customer concentration
- Supplier dependency
- Technology obsolescence
- Capacity constraints
- Quality issues

**Strategic Risks**
- Market position threats
- Competitive dynamics
- Regulatory changes
- Technology disruption
- Integration challenges

**Compliance Risks**
- Regulatory non-compliance
- License/permit deficiencies
- Data privacy violations
- Trade compliance issues
- Healthcare compliance (if applicable)
- Financial services compliance (if applicable)

### 5.2 Risk Rating Criteria

| Rating | Likelihood | Impact | Combined Assessment |
|--------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| GREEN | Remote (<20%) | De minimis | Normal course of business |
| YELLOW | Possible (20-50%) | Moderate | Manageable with attention |
| ORANGE | Likely (50-75%) | Significant | Requires remediation/adjustment |
| RED | Highly Likely (>75%) | Severe/Material | Potential deal-breaker |

### 5.3 Financial Impact Thresholds

| Level | Individual Issue | Aggregate Category |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| De Minimis | <$50,000 | <$100,000 |
| Minor | $50,000 - $250,000 | $100,000 - $500,000 |
| Moderate | $250,000 - $1,000,000 | $500,000 - $2,000,000 |
| Significant | $1,000,000 - $5,000,000 | $2,000,000 - $10,000,000 |
| Material | >$5,000,000 | >$10,000,000 |

## 6. Reporting Deliverables Summary

### 6.1 Standard Deliverables Package

| Deliverable | Audience | Timing |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Initial Issues List | Deal Team | Week 1-2 |
| Weekly Status Update | Deal Team | Weekly |
| Interim Summary | Management | Mid-point |
| Draft Final Report | Deal Team | Pre-signing |
| Final Report | Board/Committee | Signing |
| Disclosure Schedule Input | Transaction Counsel | Pre-signing |

### 6.2 Special Reports (As Needed)

- Standalone IP Report (for technology deals)
- Environmental Phase I/II Summary
- Tax Structure Analysis
- Compliance Gap Assessment
- Integration Risk Assessment
- Cybersecurity Assessment Summary

### 6.3 Quality Control Checklist

Before finalizing any report:
- [ ] All sections completed
- [ ] Cross-references verified
- [ ] Financial calculations checked
- [ ] Risk ratings consistently applied
- [ ] Recommendations actionable
- [ ] Confidentiality markings applied
- [ ] Version control updated
- [ ] Distribution list confirmed
- [ ] Reviewer sign-off obtained
