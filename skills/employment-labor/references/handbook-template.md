# Employee Handbook Key Policies Reference Guide

## Overview

An employee handbook establishes company policies, communicates expectations, and provides legal protection. This guide covers essential policies, legally required disclosures, and best practices for handbook development.

---

## At-Will Employment

### At-Will Doctrine

**Definition**
```
Employment with [Company] is "at-will." This means that either
the employee or the Company may terminate the employment
relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or
without notice. Nothing in this handbook or any other Company
document creates or implies a contract of employment for any
specific duration.
```

### At-Will Disclaimers

**Key Elements**
- Clear statement of at-will status
- No contract for specific duration
- Either party may terminate at any time
- No modification except in writing by authorized officer
- Handbook does not create contract

**Prominent Placement**
- Acknowledgment form (signed)
- Introduction section
- Employment section
- Discipline section

### Exceptions to At-Will

**Cannot Terminate For**
- Discriminatory reasons (protected class)
- Retaliation for protected activity
- Exercising statutory rights
- Public policy violations
- Implied contract (if created)
- Covenant of good faith (some states)

## Equal Employment Opportunity

### EEO Policy Statement

**Model Policy**
```
[Company] is an equal opportunity employer. We prohibit
discrimination and harassment based on race, color, religion,
sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender
identity), national origin, age, disability, genetic information,
veteran status, or any other protected characteristic under
applicable federal, state, or local law.

This policy applies to all employment practices, including
hiring, placement, promotion, termination, layoff, recall,
transfer, leaves of absence, compensation, and training.
```

### Protected Categories

**Federal Protected Classes**
- Race
- Color
- Religion
- Sex (includes pregnancy, LGBTQ+ per EEOC)
- National origin
- Age (40+)
- Disability
- Genetic information
- Veteran status

**Additional State/Local Classes**
- Marital status
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity
- Political affiliation
- Criminal history
- Credit history
- Domestic violence victim status
- Others vary by jurisdiction

## Anti-Harassment Policy

### Policy Components

**Prohibited Conduct**
```
[Company] prohibits harassment based on any protected
characteristic. Harassment includes unwelcome conduct that
is based on a protected characteristic and:
(1) creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work
environment; or
(2) unreasonably interferes with an individual's work
performance; or
(3) affects an individual's employment opportunities.
```

### Sexual Harassment

**Types**
- Quid pro quo: Employment benefits conditioned on sexual favors
- Hostile environment: Unwelcome conduct creating abusive environment

**Examples of Prohibited Conduct**
- Unwanted sexual advances
- Requests for sexual favors
- Sexual jokes or comments
- Displaying sexually explicit materials
- Inappropriate touching
- Sending suggestive messages
- Making sexual gestures
- Commenting on physical appearance

### Complaint Procedure

**Reporting Options**
- Direct supervisor (unless harasser)
- Human Resources
- Department head
- Anonymous hotline
- Any manager
- Alternative contacts (if small company)

**Investigation Commitment**
```
All complaints will be investigated promptly, thoroughly,
and impartially. [Company] will maintain confidentiality
to the extent possible consistent with adequate investigation
and appropriate corrective action. Retaliation against anyone
who reports harassment or participates in an investigation
is strictly prohibited and will result in discipline.
```

### State-Specific Requirements

**California**
- Written policy required
- Training required (2 hours supervisors, 1 hour employees)
- Specific policy elements mandated

**New York**
- Written policy required
- Annual training required
- Model policy available

**Illinois**
- Annual training required
- Restaurant/bar specific requirements

## Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)

### Eligibility Requirements

**Employee Must**
- Work for covered employer (50+ employees)
- Have worked 12 months
- Have worked 1,250 hours in preceding 12 months
- Work at location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

### Leave Entitlement

**12 Weeks Unpaid Leave For**
- Birth and care of newborn
- Placement of child for adoption or foster care
- Care for spouse, child, or parent with serious health condition
- Employee's own serious health condition
- Qualifying exigency for military family member

**26 Weeks For**
- Care for covered servicemember with serious injury/illness

### Serious Health Condition

**Definition**
- Inpatient care, or
- Continuing treatment by health care provider:
  - Incapacity of more than 3 consecutive days with treatment
  - Pregnancy or prenatal care
  - Chronic condition requiring periodic treatment
  - Permanent or long-term condition
  - Multiple treatments for restorative surgery or condition requiring absence

### Employer Obligations

**Notice Requirements**
- Post FMLA poster
- Include FMLA information in handbook
- Provide eligibility notice within 5 days of request
- Provide rights and responsibilities notice
- Provide designation notice within 5 days of determination

**Certification**
- May require medical certification
- May require periodic recertification
- May require fitness-for-duty certification
- Cannot require second opinion for every absence

### Policy Elements

**Leave Request Procedure**
- 30 days advance notice when foreseeable
- As soon as practicable when not foreseeable
- Call-in procedures for unforeseeable leave
- Designation of FMLA coordinator

**Benefits During Leave**
- Health insurance continues
- Employee pays normal contribution
- Other benefits per policy

**Return to Work**
- Same or equivalent position
- Same pay, benefits, terms
- Fitness-for-duty certification permitted

## ADA and Reasonable Accommodations

### Policy Statement

```
[Company] is committed to providing equal employment
opportunities to qualified individuals with disabilities.
We will provide reasonable accommodations to enable qualified
individuals to perform the essential functions of their
positions, unless doing so would cause undue hardship.
```

### Disability Definition

**ADA Definition**
- Physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities
- Record of such impairment
- Regarded as having such impairment

**Major Life Activities**
- Caring for oneself
- Performing manual tasks
- Seeing, hearing, eating, sleeping
- Walking, standing, lifting, bending
- Speaking, breathing, learning
- Reading, concentrating, thinking
- Communicating, working
- Major bodily functions

### Interactive Process

**Steps**
1. Employee requests accommodation or need is apparent
2. Engage in interactive dialogue
3. Identify essential functions of position
4. Identify barriers to performance
5. Explore possible accommodations
6. Select and implement effective accommodation
7. Monitor effectiveness

**Documentation**
- Record all discussions
- Document accommodations considered
- Explain reasons for decisions
- Follow up on effectiveness

### Reasonable Accommodations

**Examples**
- Modified work schedule
- Job restructuring
- Reassignment to vacant position
- Modified equipment
- Accessible facilities
- Part-time or modified schedule
- Leave of absence
- Work from home
- Quiet workspace
- Modified policies

**Undue Hardship Factors**
- Nature and cost of accommodation
- Financial resources of employer
- Size and type of operation
- Impact on operations

## Workplace Safety

### General Safety Policy

```
[Company] is committed to providing a safe and healthy
workplace for all employees. All employees are expected
to follow safety rules and practices, report unsafe
conditions, and take reasonable care for their own safety
and that of others.
```

### OSHA Compliance

**Employer Obligations**
- Provide workplace free from recognized hazards
- Comply with OSHA standards
- Post required notices
- Keep required records (Form 300, 300A, 301)
- Report fatalities within 8 hours
- Report hospitalizations within 24 hours

**Employee Rights**
- Safe workplace
- Report unsafe conditions
- Access to injury and illness records
- Request OSHA inspection
- Protection from retaliation

### Workplace Violence Prevention

**Policy Elements**
- Zero tolerance statement
- Definition of workplace violence
- Reporting procedures
- Investigation process
- Protective measures
- Prohibited items (weapons)

**Warning Signs**
- Threats (direct or indirect)
- Intimidating behavior
- Fascination with violence
- Substance abuse
- Extreme stress indicators
- Domestic violence spillover

## Drug and Alcohol Policy

### Policy Statement

```
[Company] is committed to maintaining a drug-free workplace.
The use, possession, distribution, or sale of illegal drugs,
or being under the influence of alcohol or drugs, on Company
premises or while conducting Company business is strictly
prohibited and will result in disciplinary action, up to and
including termination.
```

### Testing Programs

**Types of Testing**
- Pre-employment
- Reasonable suspicion
- Post-accident
- Random (if permitted)
- Return to duty
- Follow-up

**Legal Considerations**
- State law varies significantly
- Medical marijuana issues
- ADA implications
- Privacy concerns
- Prescription medication

### Drug-Free Workplace Act

**Federal Contractor Requirements**
- Publish policy statement
- Establish awareness program
- Notify employees of conviction reporting requirement
- Notify federal agency of conviction within 10 days
- Take appropriate action within 30 days

## Social Media and Technology

### Social Media Policy

**Appropriate Use**
```
Employees may use social media in accordance with this policy.
When identifying yourself as a [Company] employee or discussing
Company-related matters:
- Be respectful and professional
- Do not disclose confidential information
- Make clear views are your own
- Do not speak on behalf of Company without authorization
- Comply with all other Company policies
```

**Protected Activity (NLRA)**
- Discussing wages and working conditions
- Concerted activity for mutual aid
- Cannot prohibit all negative comments
- Cannot require prior approval of all posts

### Electronic Communications

**Monitoring Disclosure**
```
Company electronic resources, including email, internet,
and computer systems, are Company property provided for
business purposes. Employees should have no expectation
of privacy in their use of Company systems. The Company
reserves the right to monitor all electronic communications
and access all data stored on Company systems.
```

**Acceptable Use**
- Business purposes primary
- Limited personal use may be permitted
- Prohibited activities clearly stated
- Password and security requirements

## Wage and Hour

### Pay Practices

**Required Information**
- Pay periods and paydays
- Timekeeping requirements
- Overtime policy
- Deductions from pay
- Final pay procedures

**Overtime**
```
Non-exempt employees will receive overtime pay at 1.5 times
their regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.
All overtime must be approved in advance. Working unauthorized
overtime is prohibited and may result in discipline; however,
all hours worked will be compensated.
```

### Meal and Rest Breaks

**Federal**
- No federal requirement for meal/rest breaks
- If provided, short breaks (5-20 min) are compensable
- Bona fide meal periods (30+ min) not compensable if relieved of duty

**State Requirements Vary**
- California: 30-min meal break by 5th hour; 10-min rest per 4 hours
- Other states have varying requirements
- Check applicable jurisdiction

## Discipline and Termination

### Progressive Discipline

**Typical Steps**
1. Verbal warning (documented)
2. Written warning
3. Final written warning/suspension
4. Termination

**Reservations**
```
[Company] generally follows progressive discipline but
reserves the right to skip steps or proceed directly to
termination depending on the severity of the conduct.
This policy does not alter the at-will employment
relationship.
```

### Immediate Termination Offenses

**Examples**
- Violence or threats
- Theft or fraud
- Gross insubordination
- Serious safety violations
- Harassment
- Drug/alcohol violations
- Falsification of records

## Handbook Acknowledgment

### Acknowledgment Form

```
I acknowledge that I have received a copy of the [Company]
Employee Handbook. I understand that:

1. The handbook describes important information about my
   employment and I should read it carefully.

2. The policies may be changed at any time at the Company's
   discretion.

3. My employment is at-will and may be terminated by either
```
