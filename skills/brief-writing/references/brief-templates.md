# Brief Structure Templates

## Overview

Effective legal briefs follow established structures while adapting to specific court requirements. This guide provides templates for motion briefs, appellate briefs, and amicus briefs.

## Motion Brief Template

```

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Table of Authorities ............................................. ii

Introduction .................................................... 1

Statement of Facts .............................................. 2

Argument ....................................................... 5

    I.   [FIRST MAJOR ARGUMENT HEADING] ......................... 5

         A.  [Subheading] ....................................... 6

         B.  [Subheading] ....................................... 8

    II.  [SECOND MAJOR ARGUMENT HEADING] ....................... 10

         A.  [Subheading] ...................................... 11

         B.  [Subheading] ...................................... 13

    III. [THIRD MAJOR ARGUMENT HEADING] ........................ 15

Conclusion ..................................................... 17

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TABLE OF AUTHORITIES

Cases                                                          Page

[Case Name], [Citation] ........................................ X
[Case Name], [Citation] ........................................ X

Statutes

[Statute Citation] ............................................. X

Rules

[Rule Citation] ................................................ X

Other Authorities

[Secondary Source] ............................................. X

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INTRODUCTION

[1-2 paragraphs that:
 - Identify the motion
 - State the relief sought
 - Summarize the key argument in 2-3 sentences
 - Preview why the court should grant the motion]

Example:
Defendant ABC Corporation respectfully moves for summary judgment
on all claims. The undisputed facts establish that Plaintiff
cannot prove an essential element of its breach of contract
claim—namely, that the parties formed a valid agreement.
Because no reasonable jury could find that a contract existed,
this Court should enter judgment as a matter of law.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATEMENT OF FACTS

[Organized chronologically or by topic. Include:
 - Only facts supported by record evidence
 - Citations to the record (declarations, exhibits, depositions)
 - Facts favorable AND unfavorable (to maintain credibility)
 - Focus on facts relevant to the legal arguments]

Structure:
A. Background
B. The [Relevant Events]
C. This Litigation

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARGUMENT

[Use IRAC or CRAC structure for each section:
 - Issue/Conclusion: State the legal issue or conclusion
 - Rule: Set forth the applicable legal standard
 - Application: Apply the rule to the facts
 - Conclusion: Conclude on the issue]

I. [ARGUMENT HEADING - WRITTEN AS A COMPLETE, PERSUASIVE SENTENCE]

   [Topic sentence stating the conclusion]

   [Rule paragraph explaining the legal standard]

   [Application paragraphs applying law to facts]

   [Transition to next section]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, [Party] respectfully requests that
this Court [specific relief requested].

Dated: [Date]               Respectfully submitted,

                           _______________________________
                           [Attorney Name]
                           [Firm Name]
                           [Address]
                           [Phone]
                           [Email]
                           Attorneys for [Party]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Section-by-Section Guidance

### Introduction Best Practices

- **Length**: 1-2 paragraphs (half page to one page)
- **Purpose**: Hook the reader and frame the case favorably
- **Content**: Motion + relief + best argument + why you win
- **Tone**: Confident but not overreaching

### Statement of Facts Best Practices

- **Tell a story**: Chronological narrative is usually best
- **Be accurate**: Include unfavorable facts
- **Cite everything**: Every factual assertion needs a citation
- **Show, don't tell**: Use specific facts, not characterizations
- **Length**: Proportional to complexity (typically 3-8 pages)

### Argument Best Practices

- **One idea per paragraph**: Topic sentence + support
- **Lead with strength**: Best arguments first
- **Use headings**: Persuasive, complete sentences
- **Address counterarguments**: Preemptively defuse weaknesses
- **Apply, don't just cite**: Connect law to your facts

### Conclusion Best Practices

- **Be specific**: State exact relief requested
- **Be brief**: Usually 1-2 paragraphs
- **Reinforce**: Remind court why you should win

## Word/Page Limits

| Court | Document Type | Limit |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| Federal District | Motion | Local rule (often 25 pages) |
| Federal Circuit | Opening Brief | 13,000 words |
| Federal Circuit | Response Brief | 13,000 words |
| Federal Circuit | Reply Brief | 6,500 words |
| Supreme Court | Merits Brief | 13,000 words |
| Supreme Court | Amicus Brief | 9,000 words |
