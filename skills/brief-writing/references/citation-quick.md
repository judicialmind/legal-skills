# Legal Citation Quick Reference

## Bluebook Essentials

### Case Citations

**Basic Format:**
```
[Case Name], [Volume] [Reporter] [Page], [Pincite] ([Court] [Year]).
```

**Examples:**

```
Supreme Court:
Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954).

Federal Circuit Court:
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020).

Federal District Court:
Doe v. Roe, 456 F. Supp. 3d 789, 792 (S.D.N.Y. 2021).

State Court:
People v. Johnson, 123 Cal. App. 4th 456, 460 (2020).
```

### Statute Citations

**Federal Statutes:**
```
[Title] U.S.C. § [Section] ([Year]).

42 U.S.C. § 1983 (2018).
15 U.S.C. §§ 1-7 (2018).
```

**State Statutes:**
```
[State Abbrev.] [Code Name] § [Section] ([Year]).

Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 (West 2020).
N.Y. Gen. Bus. Law § 349 (McKinney 2021).
Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Ann. § 17.41 (West 2019).
```

**Session Laws:**
```
[Name], Pub. L. No. [Number], [Section], [Statutes at Large Citation] ([Year]).

Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, Pub. L. No. 101-336, 104 Stat. 327.
```

### Regulatory Citations

**Code of Federal Regulations:**
```
[Title] C.F.R. § [Section] ([Year]).

17 C.F.R. § 240.10b-5 (2023).
```

**Federal Register:**
```
[Volume] Fed. Reg. [Page] ([Date]).

88 Fed. Reg. 12,345 (Feb. 27, 2023).
```

## Short Form Citations

### Cases

**Id.:**
```
Full cite: Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020).
Short form: Id. at 462.
Short form (same page): Id.
```

**Short Case Name:**
```
After full cite: Smith, 123 F.3d at 463.
Or: Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d at 463.
```

**Supra (for cases cited earlier in same document):**
```
Smith, supra, at 464.
```

### Statutes

```
Full cite: 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (2018).
Short form: § 1983.
Short form: 42 U.S.C. § 1983.
```

### Rules

```
Full cite: Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6).
Short form: Rule 12(b)(6).
```

## Introductory Signals

### Support Signals

| Signal | Meaning | Use |
|--------|---------|-----|
| [no signal] | Direct support | Cite directly states proposition |
| *See* | Clear support | Cite clearly supports but doesn't directly state |
| *See also* | Additional support | Cite provides additional support |
| *Cf.* | Analogous support | Cite supports by analogy |
| *Accord* | Agreement | Multiple cites agree |

### Comparison Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| *Compare* ... *with* | Comparison shows proposition |

### Contradiction Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| *Contra* | Directly contradicts |
| *But see* | Clearly contradicts |
| *But cf.* | Contradicts by analogy |

### Background Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| *See generally* | Background information |

## Parenthetical Information

### Quote Parentheticals
```
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020) ("The statute
requires actual knowledge.").
```

### Explanatory Parentheticals
```
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020) (holding that
actual knowledge is required).

Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020) (applying the
good faith exception to the exclusionary rule).
```

### Prior/Subsequent History
```
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456 (9th Cir. 2019), aff'd, 140 S. Ct.
1234 (2020).

Smith v. Jones, 456 F. Supp. 3d 789 (S.D.N.Y. 2020), rev'd,
12 F.4th 345 (2d Cir. 2021).
```

### Weight of Authority
```
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020) (en banc).
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020) (per curiam).
Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (9th Cir. 2020) (plurality opinion).
```

## Quotations

### Block Quotes (50+ words)
- Indent left and right margins
- No quotation marks
- Single-spaced (where court rules allow)

### Alterations
```
Original: "The defendant knew about the defect."
Altered: "[D]efendant knew about the defect."
Altered: "The defendant [Smith] knew about the defect."
```

### Omissions
```
"The defendant . . . knew about the defect."
"The defendant knew about the defect. . . ."
```

### Emphasis
```
"The defendant knew about the defect." (emphasis added).
"The defendant knew about the defect." (emphasis in original).
```

## String Citations

**Order of Authority:**
1. Constitutions (federal, then state)
2. Statutes (federal, then state)
3. Treaties
4. Cases (by court hierarchy, then date)
5. Administrative materials
6. Secondary sources

**Separating Cites:**
```
Semicolon between cites: Case A; Case B; Case C.
Signal groups: See Case A; Case B. See also Case C.
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Incorrect spacing** - No space before section symbol: § 1983
2. **Missing pincites** - Always include page numbers
3. **Wrong reporter** - Use official reporter when available
4. **Outdated citations** - Check for subsequent history
5. **Incorrect court abbreviation** - Verify court identifier
6. **Block quote errors** - 50+ words requires block format
7. **Missing year** - Always include decision year
8. **Incorrect Id. usage** - Only for immediately preceding cite
